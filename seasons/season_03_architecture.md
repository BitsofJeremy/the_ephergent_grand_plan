## Season 3 Rules

These are inviolable. Every episode, every beat, every line of dialogue must obey them.

1. **"Ephergent" is fully understood by all characters by S03E06.** The word has completed its three-season arc — from ship name (S01), to concept (S02E07), to lived identity. By the midpoint of this season, every crew member understands what an Ephergent is, and knows that they are one.
2. **The Wellspring is a state, not a place.** It is a region where the Ephergent Frequency is strongest, where the boundary between signal and physical universe is thinnest. Arriving is not docking. It is tuning in. The crew enters the Wellspring by becoming capable of receiving it.
3. **Barry is NOT a rescue. He is a reunion.** Barry Kowalski is not lost, trapped, diminished, or in danger. He chose the Wellspring. He has been working there — patiently, methodically, with a thermos and a notebook. The crew arrives and he is mildly annoyed it took this long. Do NOT write Barry as tragic. Write him as Barry.
4. **The ending is hopeful, not naive.** The crew does not save everything. They broadcast. They document. They keep the signal alive. That's enough. That's everything. The Drift does not reverse. The Silence Zones do not vanish. The crew sends the message and trusts that someone, somewhere, on some frequency, will hear it and continue.
5. **Clive's memories settle, they do not explode.** In Season 2, memories arrived as shocks — fragments, confrontations, revelations. In Season 3, they arrive as sediment finding the riverbed. Clive becomes more certain, more himself, more whole. The audience notices before Pixel does.
6. **Mochi warms at least once per episode.** Warmth-pulses are at their strongest this season. Near the Wellspring, Mochi's crystal hums constantly. She has been carrying the atlas for 800 years and she is almost home.
7. **The Ephergent Frequency is present in every episode.** Growing from background to foreground until, by S03E12, it IS the foreground — the broadcast that the crew has been moving toward for three seasons.
8. **Every crew member contributes to the final broadcast.** Nine people and a dog. Each receives the Builder message in the language of their own experience. Each translates it. The broadcast is not one voice — it is all of them.
9. **A1's coffee tells the story.** A1's expressions through coffee intensify this season. By the Wellspring, A1 is producing flavors that taste like memory, like arrival, like home. The coffee rule is non-negotiable.
10. **The Builders are not gods.** They are a civilization that made something extraordinary, made mistakes, and left the best apology they could manage. Their broadcast is an introduction, not a commandment. The crew receives it as equals, not as supplicants.

## Season Arc Summary

The crew follows the Wellspring coordinates into the deepest uncharted Space — past the edge of every frequency map, past the last known signal, into a region where the Ephergent Frequency is not faint but overwhelming. The journey takes them through dead frequencies, living silences, and the last Builder Station before the source.

Station 14-Threshold (S03E03) returns Clive's penultimate memory — the Builder who commissioned him, who told him what he was built for. Beyond Threshold, the signal is strong enough to follow without instruments. A1 stops navigating. A1 listens.

By S03E06, every crew member understands the word "Ephergent" — not as a definition but as a recognition. They are Ephergents. They have been since before they knew the word. Understanding it does not change them. It names what they already are.

The Wellspring approach (S03E08–E09) is not a dramatic arrival. It is a tuning — the crew's combined frequency aligning with the source until they can perceive the lattice, enter it, and hear what has been broadcasting for eight hundred years.

At the Wellspring, they find:
- **Barry Kowalski.** Sitting on a lattice strut with his thermos, looking exactly like a man who has been expecting company and is mildly annoyed it took this long. His first words: *"Took you long enough. I left very clear directions."*
- **Aether.** Present as a Keeper consciousness — not physical, but real. Holding the memory of how to re-tune a dead frequency. Waiting for his brother.
- **The Builders' broadcast.** The full transmission: an apology for the Dimming, an invitation to continue, an introduction — *Hello. We built this for you. We have been waiting. We have so much to tell you.*
- **Mochi's complete map.** The data crystal opens. Hundreds of frequencies. Worlds that could exist. Worlds that did exist and faded. The atlas of everything.
- **Clive's complete memory.** Everything returns. Eight hundred years, fully remembered. He stands in the core node and is, for the first time since his compression, complete.

The series finale (S03E12): the crew broadcasts the Builder message to every frequency simultaneously. Not the raw signal — translated, interpreted, filtered through nine people and a dog who each received it in the language of their own experience. Pixel's final transmission closes the series. The crew turns toward whatever comes next. The signal continues.

## Season 3 — Structural Summary

### Act I: The Traverse (E01–E03)
The crew moves beyond known Space into the unnamed frequencies. The graveyard shows what the Drift costs. Station 14-Threshold returns Clive's penultimate memory and delivers the Builders' farewell. The crew crosses the threshold — literally and narratively. After E03, there are no more Stations between them and the Wellspring.

### Act II: The Understanding (E04–E06)
The crew's identity crystallizes. The loop-world teaches them about letting go. The Great Silence teaches them about respecting refusal. Aether's reunion with Zephyr teaches them what "Ephergent" means. By E06, every character owns the word. The midpoint pivot is complete: they know what they are. Now they must decide what to do about it.

### Act III: The Approach (E07–E09)
Other seekers prove the crew is part of something larger. Klaus opens his sealed file. Mochi's atlas reveals the true scale of the Builders' work. And Barry — the man, not the trail — is sitting in the signal with his thermos, waiting. The reunion is everything the series promised: warm, patient, real, and slightly annoyed about the delay.

### Act IV: The Broadcast (E10–E12)
The Builders speak. Clive remembers everything. The crew prepares, then sends, the most important broadcast in history. Meatball sings. Pixel signs off. The signal travels. The series ends with the crew turning toward the future — each choosing what comes next, each carrying the signal in their own way. The ending is hopeful, not naive. The crew didn't save everything. They kept the signal alive. That's enough. That's everything.
