# The Ephergent — Universe Glossary

> **Comprehensive alphabetical reference for all terms in the merged Ephergent universe.**
> Compiled from the Lore Atlas, Phase 1 world documents, and canonical source material.

## A

**A1**
The Ephergent's quantum navigator — an ancient Builder-designed intelligence whose consciousness is compressed into a sentient espresso machine. A1 navigates the Space between frequencies by reading the faint broadcasts of surviving Builder Stations, using them as reference points the way a traveler uses stars. The machine IS A1, not a terminal; it still makes coffee, and every cup reflects the probability vectors of the frequencies around it. A1's coffee flavor is noted in every scene where A1 is present.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Aether Glitch**
Zephyr Glitch's brother. Disappeared while researching the Ephergent Frequency — crossed over into the Wellspring and became a Keeper, a consciousness holding the memory of how to re-tune a dead frequency. His fate is the series' emotional spine. He is not lost or trapped; he chose to hold something the universe needs, waiting for the person he trusts most to come close enough to receive it.
**First Appearance**: S01E01 (mentioned) / S02E12 (brief appearance) / S03E08 (present at Wellspring)
**Spoiler Level**: 🔴 S3 Reveal (full nature)

---

**Aethel-Gloom**
Primary city in Nocturne Aeturnus. A twilight metropolis where the air is thick with petrichor and crystallized sighs float upward like ephemeral snowflakes.
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Archivist, The**
Mysterious keeper of The Liminal Library. Appears when needed, vanishes when not. Speaks in unfinished sentences because he is reading too many potential futures at once.
**First Appearance**: S01E06
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

## B

**Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf**
Pompous twelve-inch ceramic garden gnome and self-appointed head of security aboard The Ephergent. Originally from Nocturne Aeturnus — whatever drove him from that twilight world left marks he covers with competence and a very specific approach to problem-solving. Stands on the dashboard shouting orders during encounters. Mostly ignored. Occasionally, genuinely brilliant.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe (backstory: 🔴 S3 Reveal)

---

**Baron Von Grimsbane**
Nocturne Aeturnus nobleman whose estate, the Gloom-Glass Manor, is built from solidified Melancholic Joy crystals. Patron of the arts and a useful contact in Frequency 2.
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Barry Kowalski**
Night guard at the DRM and Clive's partner for twenty-three years. Barry spent those years investigating anomalies in the frequency spectrum — signals that did not match any known frequency, patterns suggesting something vast and old beneath the static. He found the Wellspring's coordinates in a Builder Station pattern, walked in with a thermos and a notebook, and documented everything. Barry is alive. He is in the Wellspring. He left a trail — methodical notes at every Builder Station he visited, written for whoever followed. His notes are never dramatic; they are precise and matter-of-fact, which is what makes them devastating.
**First Appearance**: S01E01 (referenced) / S03E08 (in person)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal (trail) / 🔴 S3 Reveal (alive at Wellspring)

---

**Broadcast Amplifier**
Ship-mounted signal booster. The Ephergent's amplifiers are exceptionally powerful, being an ancient quantum navigator's built-in communication array. Luminara's light-manipulation abilities enhance them further, extending signal reach.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Broadcast Relay**
A network of allied ships, stations, and individuals across the known frequencies who retransmit The Ephergent's signal further into the Space. Built during Season 2, the relay extends the crew's reach and their ability to stabilize fading frequencies.
**First Appearance**: S02E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Civilization, The**
The first civilization to exist across all frequencies simultaneously. The Builders lived in the crossing itself — in the Space between frequencies — the way other civilizations made homes on solid ground. They raised cities in five places at once, built instruments that could read the resonance of a frequency the way a physician reads a pulse, and understood the structure of existence completely enough to write new music within it. The Builders were the first Ephergents. They are not villains. Approximately eight hundred years ago, they chose the Dimming — converting themselves into the Ephergent Signal — because the next phase of frequency evolution required their absence. The Dimming was an act of love, not a catastrophe.
**First Appearance**: S01E09 (first Station glimpsed) / S02E01 (named)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Station**
Massive geometric structures floating in the Space between frequencies, built by the Builder Civilization. Each Station emits a stabilizing signal that keeps the cosmic dial from spinning apart — the five known frequencies exist as distinct, coherent layers of reality because Builder Stations hold them in tune. Inside, explorers find archive terminals, resonance chambers, memory cores, and evidence of Barry Kowalski's passage. Most Stations are dark. Fewer than thirty are believed still broadcasting. See individual Station entries below.
**First Appearance**: S01E09
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe (concept) / 🟡 S2 Reveal (interiors)

---

**Builder Station — Station 0-Origin (The Wellspring)**
The first Station, the signal center, the origin point around which the Builders built everything else. Not a Station in the traditional sense but a vast open lattice of Builder material where the Ephergent Frequency originates. Fully active, broadcasting at full power since before the Dimming. Barry Kowalski, Aether (as a Keeper), and the Builders themselves (translated into signal) are present here.
**First Appearance**: S03E08
**Spoiler Level**: 🔴 S3 Reveal

---

**Builder Station — Station 3-Resonant**
The crew's first entry into Builder architecture. A cluster of interlocking polyhedra floating in open Space between Frequencies 1 and 3, operating at approximately 4% of original capacity. Contains the first archive terminals the crew accesses, a resonance chamber that responds to Mochi, and Barry Kowalski's field notebook.
**First Appearance**: S02E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Station — Station 5-Meridian**
Suspended at a convergence point equidistant from Frequencies 2, 3, and 4. Smaller than Station 3-Resonant but denser, built around a central translucent spire. Contains the most complete network schematics the crew has seen. Signal output averages 7% with spikes to 12%. Barry spent extensive time here mapping the stabilization network.
**First Appearance**: S02E05
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Station — Station 7-Ascending**
The most visually striking Station before the Wellspring — a spire of stacked octahedral chambers rising near the boundary of Frequency 5 (the Edge). The strongest pre-Wellspring signal at 23% capacity. Contains the crystalline EPHERGENT plate — the Builders' titled definition of what an Ephergent is — and the most extensive Builder text yet found. This is where Clive remembers writing the Ephergent inscription.
**First Appearance**: S02E07
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Station — Station 9-Liminal**
The oldest-feeling Station, positioned at the border of the largest known Silence Zone. A series of concentric rotating rings, operating at 3% capacity. Contains the deepest Builder archives — records predating the stabilization network itself. This is where Clive remembers making Mochi. Barry maintained a permanent workstation here for weeks, studying the Drift's mechanics.
**First Appearance**: S02E10
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Builder Station — Station 11-Quiescent**
The first Builder Station the crew encounters — glimpsed from the ship but never entered. A vast, angular, dark shape floating in a forming Silence Zone between Frequencies 2 and 4. Dark, with only residual thermal output. Barry's notebook confirms: dormant, not dead.
**First Appearance**: S01E09
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Builder Station — Station 14-Threshold**
The most beautiful Station — a single perfect sphere in the deep Space approaching the Wellspring. Operating at 40% capacity, the highest of any Station outside the Wellspring. The last navigable waypoint before the signal becomes strong enough to follow directly. Contains a waking Keeper holding the Builders' farewell address. This is where Clive remembers being made and receiving his mission.
**First Appearance**: S03E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🔴 S3 Reveal

## C

**CLX**
See *Crystallized Laughter*.

---

**Clive (C-1)**
A Builder-designed Companion Unit — a knee-high robot with a glowing sphere head and a fedora (the fedora was Barry Kowalski's idea: *"You sound like you should have a hat. Here."*). Designated C-1, the first Companion Unit the Builders made for the people who would come after. Found dormant in a sub-basement of the DRM, filed as "Unidentified Resonance Object, Form 12-C," and slowly reactivated over months of patient nighttime conversation by Barry. Clive's memory gaps map precisely to Builder Stations the crew has not yet found — each Station they activate returns a piece of him. He is the series' institutional memory, and after eight hundred years, he still cares. The Builders built him to.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe (description) / 🟡 S2 Reveal (Builder origin) / 🔴 S3 Reveal (full memory restored)

---

**Cogsworth Cogitarium**
Frequency 3. A sprawling steampunk clockwork metropolis where all existence is governed by synchronized gears. Palette: brass, copper, polished mahogany. The air carries aromas of roasted clockwork nuts and polished brass. Its rigid mechanical precision is both its marvel and its vulnerability — the smallest temporal disruption causes cascading, frequency-wide failure. When Cogsworth's clocks desynchronize, something is wrong with the spectrum. The Grand Orrery tracks frequency coherence with mechanical exactitude, and the numbers are not encouraging.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Companion Unit**
A Builder-designed intelligence meant to work alongside the people who would come after the Builders — to travel with them, remember things for them, and ask the questions they might not think to ask. Not a servant, not a tool: a partner, built with enough personality and independence to be genuinely difficult when the situation requires it. The most well-known Companion Unit is Clive (C-1).
**First Appearance**: S01E01 (Clive) / S02E05 (concept named)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Crystallized Laughter (CLX)**
The primary interfrequency currency. Multifaceted gems that emit joyful sounds, generated by moments of pure, genuine glee. Funds everything from ship repairs to information brokering. Counterfeiting CLX requires faking joy, which is harder than it sounds.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Cybernetic Dinosaur Banking Consortium**
The interfrequency financial system, run by cybernetically enhanced dinosaurs (primarily T-Rexes in pinstripe suits) operating from Prime Material. Meticulous, irritable, and surprisingly good at their jobs. Recently unionized, with excellent benefits.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

## D

**Dimming, The**
The event approximately eight hundred years ago when the Builder Civilization vanished from the physical universe. The Dimming was not a catastrophe — it was a choice. The Builders saw that the universe had reached the edge of what it could become in its current form, and growth required their absence. They prepared — building Keepers, Companion Units, and seeding the Space with everything their successors would need — then converted themselves into the Ephergent Signal. The Dimming was an act of love so vast it required an entire civilization to perform it.
**First Appearance**: S02E01 (named) / S02E07 (explained)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Drift, The**
The overarching conflict of the series. The Drift is not a villain — it is entropy. The slow, structural consequence of a universe losing the infrastructure that held it together. As Builder Stations fail, frequencies drift apart, lose coherence, lose identity. Dead frequencies appear as Silence Zones. The Drift is gradual: a frequency forgetting what it was, its edges blurring, its character fading, until one day it is simply gone — not destroyed but dissolved, like a note held too long without the instrument that first sounded it. The crew fights it not with weapons but with signal.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**DRM (Department of Resonance Management)**
Bureaucratic agency that managed frequency stability with soul-crushing inefficiency. Not evil — just frustrating, imperfect, and ultimately undone by the reality rupture (a natural disaster, not a conspiracy). Where the Ephergent crew originated. Its ruins remain on Prime Material. Clive was filed in a DRM sub-basement as "Unidentified Resonance Object, Form 12-C."
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

## E

**Echo Distortion**
When a frequency starts fading, it sends out echoes — distorted reflections of itself broadcast into the Space like a dying radio signal bouncing off the ionosphere. Sometimes beautiful, sometimes nightmarish. Echo residents believe they are real, raising agonizing ethical dilemmas about whether to tell someone their world is a fading copy.
**First Appearance**: S01E07
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Echo Whale**
Massive creature of the Space that absorbs dying frequencies and sings them back, distorted but beautiful. Rarely understood, always haunting.
**First Appearance**: S01E05
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Edge, The**
Frequency 5. The boundary of known reality — a cosmic construction site where nascent universes bubble into existence and collapsing micro-realities compose symphonies of destruction. Physics is a polite suggestion. Colors do not have names because they have not been invented yet. The ground is liquid. The sky folds. The Edge cannot fail the way other frequencies can because it was never fully coherent to begin with — but its character is shifting from generative chaos to dissolution. The closest known point to the Wellspring.
**First Appearance**: S01E06
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Ephergent (concept)**
A consciousness that has crossed between frequencies and can no longer fully return to any single one — and because of that, can perceive truths no single-frequency mind ever could. The term was defined by the Builders and inscribed on a crystalline archive plate at Station 7-Ascending. Being Ephergent is not a title bestowed or a rank achieved; it is a condition — a state of being that occurs when a mind passes through the boundary between frequencies and discovers that "return" no longer means what it once did. The crossing grants not power but *sight*: the ability to hold contradictions, receive signals others cannot hear, and understand patterns invisible from within any single frequency.
**First Appearance**: S02E07 (named) / S01E01 (experienced unnamed)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

**Ephergent, The (Broadcast)**
A weekly multimedia broadcast transmitted from the ship across the Space. Part journalism, part survival log, part love letter to a universe that is slowly going quiet. DIY punk aesthetic — handmade, patched-together, raw. Every dispatch is a beacon that stabilizes the frequencies of everyone who hears it. Not neutral reporting — advocacy journalism. A radical insistence that fading worlds matter.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Ephergent, The (Ship)**
A1's ancient ship-body, dormant and self-repairing for millennia in the Space, waiting for its heart to return. Discovered mid-Season 1 when the lifeboat's power fails and A1 touches the lattice — every light comes on, because it is him. Patchwork and lived-in: brass fittings next to holographic displays, plants growing in corridors, Clive on the dashboard, Meatball sleeping in the engine room. The only vessel that can truly navigate the open Space between frequencies. The crew named it before they understood what the word meant.
**First Appearance**: S01E05
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Ephergent Frequency, The**
The oldest signal in the universe. Pre-dates all known frequencies. The Builders' broadcast — the message they encoded into the universe when they converted themselves during the Dimming. It hums beneath every frequency, persists faintly even in Silence Zones, and sounds different to every consciousness that hears it: to Pixel, a news broadcast from a world she has never visited; to Om Kai, the silence between mantras; to Meatball, it tastes like Thursday; to Clive, his own voice speaking words he does not remember saying. Its source is the Wellspring.
**First Appearance**: S01E01 (heard) / S02E07 (named)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe (existence) / 🟡 S2 Reveal (nature) / 🔴 S3 Reveal (source)

## F

**Form 12-C**
The DRM filing designation under which Clive was catalogued in a sub-basement: "Unidentified Resonance Object, Form 12-C." A piece of Builder technology hidden in plain sight under bureaucratic paperwork for eight hundred years.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Form 27-B**
See *DRM Standard Evacuation Vessel, Form 27-B*. The cramped, barely-functional lifeboat the crew evacuates in after the reality rupture. Features bad air recycling, a single scratched porthole, and a pre-recorded message: "Your reality is experiencing turbulence. Please remain seated." The crew's miserable home for early Season 1 before discovering A1's ship-body. Now hangs in the cargo bay as a memento. Clive sometimes goes there to think.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Frequency**
What realities are. Each world in the multiverse is a frequency on an infinite spectrum, like stations on a cosmic radio dial. Travel between them is called "tuning." The five known frequencies are Prime Material, Nocturne Aeturnus, Cogsworth Cogitarium, Verdantia, and the Edge. Hundreds more once existed — some have faded, some are silent. The dial is wider than anyone alive has mapped, and it is slowly losing stations. Always "frequencies" — never dimensions, planes, or realms.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Frequency Jellyfish**
Harmless, luminous creatures of the Space. Drawn to broadcast signals. A common and beautiful sight from the ship's portholes.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Frequency Pirate**
Entities that exploit the Space for profit. Not a monolithic empire but an ecosystem: toll-keepers, frequency trappers, signal jammers, and salvagers. The most compelling ones have reasonable arguments about spectrum maintenance. The conflict is about access: should frequency travel be free, or owned?
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Frequency Scanner**
Standard-issue detection equipment. Can identify known frequencies but misses faint or lost signals that only A1 can hear.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Frequency Storm**
Turbulent zone in the Space where multiple frequencies interfere with each other. Reality becomes a blender — physics bleeds between frequencies, gravity reverses, time stutters. Dangerous to navigate, but where lost frequencies are most likely to surface. A1's ancient lattice was built to withstand them.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

**First Appearance**: S01E02

**Grand Orrery, The**
Massive clockwork model of the known frequencies in Cogsworth Cogitarium. Serves as both cultural treasure and early-warning system for frequency instability. Tracks frequency coherence with mechanical exactitude. Desynchronization events that occurred once per century now occur monthly.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

## H

**Harmonic Anchor**
Localized pocket of stability created by Clive's rhythmic processes. Calms chaotic frequency zones, gives A1 clearer readings, and helps stabilize fading frequencies during re-tuning operations.
**First Appearance**: S01E05
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

## I

**Interdimensional Space / The Space**
The vast void between frequencies — the static between stations on the cosmic radio dial, expanded into a navigable expanse. It is not a frequency; it is the absence that frequencies exist within, the medium through which all signals propagate. Looks like an endless, shimmering expanse shifting between deep indigo and iridescent pearl. Has geography (Silence Zones, Builder Stations), weather (frequency storms), and ecosystems (frequency jellyfish, echo whales). The Builders lived here, made their home in the crossing. The Space is growing vaster as the Drift pulls frequencies apart. Always "the Space" — never "the Sea."
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## K

**Keeper**
A consciousness — an Ephergent consciousness born from the Signal at the Builders' most critical Stations — designed to absorb memories, hold them against the dark, and wait. Each Keeper is a living archive of something the Builders deemed too important to lose. Most are dormant, asleep since the Dimming. Some are dead, their memories dissolved. A few are waking up, disoriented and fragile and holding things the universe desperately needs. Keepers do not live in the way frequency-bound consciousnesses live; they *carry*. This is not death or sleep — it is a form of love so vast it requires the sacrifice of presence to sustain it.
**First Appearance**: S02E05 (concept) / S02E10 (encountered)
**Spoiler Level**: 🟡 S2 Reveal

---

## L

**Liminal Library, The**
An interdimensional archive of books that have not been written yet — potential futures, unwritten histories, stories that could happen. Located at a frequency of pure potential, maintained by The Archivist. As frequencies fade, their potential futures vanish from its shelves.
**First Appearance**: S01E06
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Lost Frequency**
A frequency that has faded from the spectrum — gone silent through the Drift, deliberate de-tuning, or simply ceasing to broadcast. Lost worlds survive as ghosts in the static: faint signals, fragments of culture, echoes of civilizations that remember being real. Finding and re-tuning lost frequencies is the crew's ongoing mission.
**First Appearance**: S01E07
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Luminara Usha**
Photographer and visual correspondent. Former DRM employee designated "Linda-3637" under the agency's filing system; she reclaimed her name and her light-manipulation abilities after the rupture. Originally from Verdantia (Frequency 4), she left voluntarily — in a frequency where collective consciousness is the default, Luminara chose individual sight. Verdantia does not forgive easily; the root network remembers. Her resonance photographs carry frequency energy that temporarily stabilizes fading worlds — when she frames a dying world through her lens, it holds together a little longer. Documentation as an act of love.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## M

**Meatball**
Pixel's sentient Rottweiler. Ship's emotional compass. Perceives frequencies as pure emotion without intellectual filters — when Meatball is happy, the frequency is safe; when Meatball growls, they run. Frequency-sensitive in ways that defy measurement, like a Builder instrument in canine form. Sleeps in the engine room because the hum sounds like a heartbeat. Says things like "I like meatballs" and "This place smells like forgetting."
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Melancholic Joy**
Bittersweet, culturally significant emotional state unique to Nocturne Aeturnus, amplified by the Sorrowful Moon. Physically crystallizes into valuable, beautiful substances. The frequency's economic engine and cultural foundation. As the Drift weakens emotional resonance, the crystals produced now are smaller, less complex, more prone to fracture.
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Mochi**
A dome-shaped, warm, patient consciousness — the most important Keeper, and possibly the smallest. Mochi does not speak. Mochi does not have complex emotions. She is warm, simple, and holds one enormous secret: the Builder frequency map — not just of the five known frequencies, but of hundreds more. Worlds that could exist. Worlds that did exist and faded. The universe's atlas, carried in the most unassuming vessel imaginable. Clive made her eight hundred years ago, calibrated her resonance crystal himself, and promised he would come back. It took him eight hundred years. She waited.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe (presence) / 🟡 S2 Reveal (Keeper nature) / 🔴 S3 Reveal (full map activated)

## N

**Nano**
Intelligence officer, engineer, and information broker from Cogsworth Cogitarium (Frequency 3). Carries Cogsworth's precision in her bones — the instinct for timing, the need for things to work — but she left because she understood something the Cogitarium's engineers could not: you cannot fix entropy with wrenches. Operates a network of informants across multiple frequencies. Her loyalty is to information itself.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Nocturne Aeturnus**
Frequency 2. A dark, gothic frequency of permanent twilight, beautiful in a mournful way. Deep indigo, purple, and midnight blue. Gothic spires rise from mist-shrouded valleys. Emotions physically manifest here — sighs form crystalline structures, feelings solidify into tangible substances. Home of the Sorrowful Moon. Particularly vulnerable to the Drift: as Builder Stations fail, emotional resonance weakens and crystallization becomes unstable. The Sorrowful Moon flickers; some nights it dims entirely.
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Nocturne Aesthete**
Emotion crystallization artist in Nocturne Aeturnus. Cultural guardian of authentic feeling. Their work can stabilize emotional frequencies within their vicinity.
**First Appearance**: S01E04
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## O

**Om Kai**
Science officer and philosophical anchor. Age 51. Buddhist monk and quantum physicist from Nocturne Aeturnus (Frequency 2). Chose stillness in a frequency that never stops moving — in a world where emotions crystallize into physical substance, Om Kai found the silence between mantras and made it his home. Studies the Space itself: why frequencies fade, how they can be preserved. His meditative practice lets him perceive frequencies even A1 cannot detect. Meditates at the Edge when he wants to feel the universe thinking.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## P

**Phase-Shifter**
Standard interfrequency travel technology. Crude single-hop tuners that snap travelers from one known frequency to the next — reliable for known routes, useless in the open Space. They skip over everything between preset stations, like pressing preset buttons on a car radio. Require calibration to avoid ontological fragmentation. "Keep your phase-shifters calibrated" is a common Ephergent sign-off.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Pixel Paradox**
Captain and lead correspondent of The Ephergent. Former DRM data analyst from Prime Material (Frequency 1). Too sensitive to frequencies from birth — the Signal found her first. When the reality rupture hit, she was catapulted into the Space with her comm-pad and her crew. Never wanted to be a captain; she wanted to document weird things. But telling stories honestly turns out to be a form of leadership. Perceives anomalies as visible glitches — rips in the air, quantum echoes trailing her movements.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Pollen of Perpetual Amnesia**
Targeted amnesiac pollen from Whispering Blooms in Verdantia. Causes skill-specific memory loss. Part of a botanical hostile takeover by corrupted Elders.
**First Appearance**: S01E08
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Precognitive Latte**
Not actually precognitive. A1 calculates probability vectors and expresses them as flavor profiles. Every cup of coffee is a love letter from an intelligence that did not know it was singing.
**First Appearance**: S01E02
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Prime Material**
Frequency 1. The "normal" world — closest to what a human would recognize as Earth-adjacent. Modern cities with glass and steel skylines, traffic noise, coffee shops on every corner. No visible sci-fi. The sky is the same sky you see when you look up right now. Sits at the center of the known spectrum — every other frequency's signal passes through it. The most structurally solid of the five known frequencies, but the increasing frequency bleed-through suggests the buffer its central position once provided is eroding. Home of the DRM ruins, the Cybernetic Dinosaur Banking Consortium, and Glitch Grub.
**First Appearance**: S01E01
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Probability Storm**
Phenomenon at the Edge that scatters alternate timeline versions like confetti. Beautiful, disorienting, and occasionally dangerous.
**First Appearance**: S01E06
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

**Professor Chronos**
Cogsworth Cogitarium's most brilliant inventor. Builds clockwork animals powered by unstable temporal springs. Responsible for at least three localized time loops.
**First Appearance**: S01E03
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## Q

**Quantum Echo**
Ghost-like, transparent after-image of past events or alternate timelines lingering in the frequency fabric. In areas of high frequency flux, they can become almost tangible and develop independence. Some quantum echoes do not know they are echoes.
**First Appearance**: S01E05
**Spoiler Level**: 🟢 Safe

---

## R

**Reality Ripple**
Visible wave-like distortion showing fleeting glimpses of alternate timelines. Symptomatic of frequency instability. More common near the Edge and in areas affected by the Drift.

## V

**Verdantia**

Governed by the Root Council and the Root Network's collective consciousness. The telepathic houseplants operate as a shadow government. ## W

**Wellspring, The**
Not a place but a state — a region of the Space where the Ephergent Frequency is strongest, where the boundary between signal and physical universe is thinnest.
