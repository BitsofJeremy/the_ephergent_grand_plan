# The Ephergent — Universe One-Pager

## What Is It?

The Ephergent is a story told through free, browser-playable games — short, self-contained adventures released episodically, each one a chapter in a larger mystery. No downloads. No paywalls. You open a browser, you play, you discover what happens next.

## What Is the World?

The multiverse is a cosmic dial. Turn it, and you tune into a different frequency — a different reality. Five are known: one that looks almost like home, one locked in beautiful twilight, one made of clockwork, one where everything is alive and growing into everything else, and one at the edge where reality gets thin.

Between the frequencies is the Space — a vast, quiet dark where ancient stations drift. These stations were built by a civilization so old nobody remembers their name. They kept the frequencies stable, humming along in harmony. Eight hundred years ago, the stations started going dark. Without them, the frequencies drift apart. Slowly. Inevitably.

That drift is the clock ticking underneath everything.

## What Is the Story?

A crew of nine people and a very good dog fly a ship called *The Ephergent*. Their mission is simple to say and impossible to finish: keep the signal alive. Find the stations that still broadcast. Document what's being lost. Send it forward so someone, someday, knows what was here.

The ship's heart is A1 — an espresso machine at the center of a quantum navigation vessel. That's not a joke. The machine *is* the ship's intelligence. It built the vessel around itself. It expresses its emotions through the flavor of the coffee it makes. When the coffee goes cold, something is very wrong.

The crew includes a broadcaster who hears frequencies no one else can, a knee-high robot in a fedora who's been around for eight centuries and won't say why, a communications officer looking for a brother who crossed over and didn't come back, a scientist who left a living world that tried to grow back into her, and a Rottweiler named Meatball who smells things that don't have a smell yet.

They argue. They laugh. They make terrible decisions and brilliant ones, sometimes in the same afternoon. They care about each other the way people do when the universe is very large and they are very small and the only thing between them and the dark is that they showed up.

## What Makes It Worth Caring About?

The Ephergent is about what you do when you can't fix everything. The drift can't be stopped. Dead frequencies don't come back. The crew doesn't save the universe — they document it, broadcast it, make sure someone remembers. And somehow that's enough. That's everything.

It's warm. It's funny — the kind of funny that comes from people who've decided laughter is the correct response to impossible situations. It's melancholy without drowning in it. It's hopeful without pretending the hard parts aren't real.

Play a game. Meet the crew. Tune the dial.
