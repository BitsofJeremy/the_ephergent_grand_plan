# The Laughing Funeral

The Laughing Funeral is a point-and-click mystery set in Nocturne Aeturnus — a gothic frequency of permanent twilight where emotions crystallize into mineral and grief is the culture's most valued public art.

You control Baron Klaus: investigator, procurement specialist, a man who left Nocturne Aeturnus fifteen years ago under circumstances he has never explained. He is not a detective. He is a man who does not look away. In Nocturne, that is considered the same thing.

Someone died. A funeral was held. And at that funeral — in a place where laughter during mourning is a rupture in the social fabric, where grief is currency and sorrow is architecture — *everyone laughed*.

Klaus has been asked to find out why.

---

## Story Context

Nocturne Aeturnus operates on a simple premise: feelings are real, and they have weight.

Here, grief crystallizes into beautiful structures — sorrow spires, melancholy lattices, the famous Sorrowful Moon that hangs permanently over the capital and dims measurably during national mourning periods. Joy is rarer, more volatile, more dangerous. Laughter at a funeral is not merely in poor taste. It is a structural violation. The social fabric of Nocturne Aeturnus is literally woven from collective emotional output, and ungoverned laughter at a grief event creates a harmonic distortion that can cascade.

Which is why the fact that everyone at Thaddeus Mourning's funeral laughed — clearly, joyfully, without exception — has been classified as a "cultural anomaly requiring no further inquiry" by Nocturne's Ministry of Emotional Integrity.

Klaus knows what "no further inquiry" means. He knows because he was the last investigator in Nocturne who refused to stop asking questions. The Ministry made his position obsolete. He left. He took a job on a ship that runs cargo and occasionally saves the universe, and he does not talk about why.

The dead person's sister contacted him through a frequency he thought was secure. She said: *I cannot reconcile what happened with anything I know about my world. You were the last person who did not look away.*

He is going back.

## The Setting — Nocturne Aeturnus

Nocturne Aeturnus exists in perpetual twilight — a deep indigo sky that never fully darkens and never fully brightens, lit by the Sorrowful Moon's violet radiance and the ambient glow of crystallized emotion suspended in the atmosphere. The air smells of rain and old stone and the faint mineral tang of crystallized feelings.

The architecture is gothic — tall spires, flying buttresses, elaborate facades with emotional resonance visible as subtle luminescence in the stonework. Buildings in wealthier districts pulse with accumulated joy. Buildings in mourning districts dim as grief is processed through the structure. A building that has recently experienced laughter glows faintly golden for generations.

The funeral house is a temple of grief — three hundred years old, built during the Third Sorrow Renaissance, its walls encrusted with the crystallized tears of generations of mourners. It is the most respected funeral house in the eastern districts. Nothing has ever gone wrong here.

Until now.

## Featured Crew

**Baron Klaus** — Player character. Tall, angular, dressed in Nocturne mourning blacks even off-world. Voice like a man who has learned to say difficult things quietly. He left Nocturne because the Ministry made it impossible to investigate what he found. He is not bitter. He is patient. There is a difference. The Ministry did not understand this.

**Pixel Paradox** — Klaus checks in via transmission every evening. Pixel asks how it's going. Klaus says "fine." Pixel says "that's not what your signal sounds like." Klaus adjusts his collar. Pixel does not push. She will wait.

**Clive** — Clive cannot enter Nocturne Aeturnus. The frequency resonance interferes with his systems. He waits on The Ephergent, on a dock Klaus can see from his hotel window. Clive's glow pulses at 3 AM every night. Klaus does not ask why. He understands.

**Luminara Usha** — Former Nocturne resident, current Ephergent crew. She left years before Klaus. She does not say why. Klaus does not ask. They recognize each other immediately across the frequency. They do not discuss what they recognize.

## Barry's Presence

In the funeral house archive room — the place where records are kept, where grief events are catalogued, where the Ministry stores the documentation it hopes no one will ever request — Klaus finds a field note.

The handwriting is not Nocturne script. It is Prime Material. It is Barry Kowalski's handwriting.

*"I came here looking for a signal. I found a silence. The silence was louder. If you're reading this, you've found the same silence. Here's what I found: the signal was never here. The signal was waiting for someone to stop listening so it could be heard. Don't stop listening. The silence is the message."*

Klaus photographs it. He sends it to Pixel. He does not know what it means. He knows it matters.
