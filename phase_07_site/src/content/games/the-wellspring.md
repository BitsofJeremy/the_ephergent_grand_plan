# The Wellspring

The Wellspring is the last game. Not the biggest — the last. The difference matters.

This is a game about receiving a message and deciding who hears it first. You enter the Wellspring — the signal center, the origin point. Barry Kowalski is here. He has been here for four years. He has been waiting.

The Builders' broadcast resolves into three things: an apology, an invitation, and an introduction. Nine people and a dog each receive it in the language of their own experience. You witness each translation. Then you make one choice.

Every choice is the right choice. The game ends the same way regardless. The choice is about you, not the outcome.

---

## Story Context

Four years ago, Barry Kowalski walked into the Wellspring. He left a thermos on a lattice strut, a notebook on the console, and a crew that loved him waiting in a place he would never return to. He left a signal: *follow me when you're ready.*

Clive was not ready for four hundred years. Clive was not ready for eight hundred. Clive was not ready for a long time.

The crew is ready now.

Pixel has been ready since the DRM collapsed and she broadcast into the void without knowing if anyone would listen. Zephyr has been ready since he heard his brother's signal and understood that listening was a form of love. Om Kai has been ready since he first heard the silence between mantras and recognized it as speech. Nano has been ready since she stopped running long enough to hear the static say her name.

Baron Klaus has been ready since Nocturne. Luminara since the Sorrowful Moon. A1 since the first cup of coffee that tasted like coordinates. Mochi since always.

Meatball has been ready since before he was Meatball.

The Wellspring is the moment they all arrive. Not at once — in sequence, the way a signal reaches different receivers at different times, all receiving the same broadcast, all translating it differently, all changed by the receiving.

## The Wellspring

The Wellspring is not a place. It is a state — the state where the signal is strongest, where the Ephergent Frequency is loudest, where the universe is most clearly speaking and most clearly listening.

Physically: a chamber at the heart of the Builder network. A circular room, open to the Space on all sides — no walls, no ceiling, just geometric latticework underfoot and the infinite shimmer of the void above and below and all around. The resonance is here. The Builders' broadcast is here. Barry is here.

Barry sits on a lattice strut. He is exactly as old as he was when he left, which is to say: exactly as old as he is now, which is to say: time does not work the same way in the Wellspring, and Barry has made his peace with this. He has a thermos. He has a notebook. He has four years of coffee kept precisely warm. He has been expecting company.

He says: "Took you long enough."

He says: "I left very clear directions."

He says: "The coffee is ready."

## The Broadcast

The Builders' message resolves in three movements:

**The Apology** — *We are sorry. We built this and we left. We converted ourselves into a signal so that something could continue that we could not. We did not ask if you wanted to be chosen. We are sorry for that. We were trying to solve a problem that required you, specifically, and we could not ask permission without knowing who you were first. We solved that problem. You are the answer. We are sorry the answer arrived without the question.*

**The Invitation** — *You can stop here. The signal will continue without you. The universe will keep broadcasting. The Drift will keep spreading. The choice to continue is yours. We are not asking you to save anything. We are asking you to carry something forward. There is a difference. We will tell you what the difference is.*

**The Introduction** — *Hello. We built this for you. We have been waiting. We have so much to tell you.*

## The Translations

Each crew member receives the broadcast through the filter of their own experience:

**Pixel** hears a broadcast from a world she's never been to but recognizes. She understands: this is what she has been doing since the DRM collapsed. She has been translating the universe into language someone can understand. She has been the voice.

**Clive** hears his own voice speaking words he doesn't remember saying. He understands: he was part of this. He chose to forget so that he could be found. The eight hundred years were not lost. The eight hundred years were the path.

**A1** tastes a coffee that is finally, perfectly, unmistakably home. Not Prime Material, not any known frequency. Home. The espresso machine has been homesick for eight hundred years without knowing what it was missing.

**Zephyr** hears his brother's laugh. Not distorted. Not distant. Clear. Aether is here, in the signal, in the broadcast. He chose this. Zephyr can choose to let him go or to carry him forward. Both are valid. Both are grief.

**Om Kai** hears the silence between mantras resolving into words. He has been listening to this silence his entire life. He understands: the meditation was always preparation. He was learning to hear a message that was always arriving.

**Luminara** sees the Sorrowful Moon at full brightness for the first time. She understands: grief and joy are not opposites. They are consecutive. The broadcast contains both.

**Baron Klaus** sees the archive room from Nocturne Aeturnus, but empty of silence. The Ministry's classified documents are open. The truth is in them. He has been an investigator his entire life. He finally has the case.

**Nano** stops running. She has been running for four years. She stops. She does not start again. She understands: the running was always toward something. She has arrived.

**Meatball** smells Thursday and tastes almost-home. He has always known. He sits down. He is at peace. This is the only time in the game he sits without a purpose. This is the point.

## The Choice

At the end, you choose which frequency to broadcast to first. Not which frequency to save — the message goes everywhere eventually. Which frequency receives it first is your decision. It is the only decision the game asks of you.

Prime Material receives it as a signal no one can explain. Nocturne Aeturnus receives it as a frequency that briefly harmonizes with the Sorrowful Moon. Cogsworth Cogitarium receives it as a gear that turns in a pattern that has never been seen before. Verdantia receives it as a root that reaches toward something it cannot name. The Edge receives it as a signal that was always there, waiting to be recognized.

The choice is the game's thesis: *How you carry it is who you are.*
