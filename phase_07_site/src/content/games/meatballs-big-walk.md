# Meatball's Big Walk

Meatball is a very good dog. He has an excellent nose. He uses it to investigate every corner of The Ephergent — the ship that is also an espresso machine, the knee-high robot with the fedora, the snacks Pixel thinks she's hidden, and one mug that smells like it belongs to someone Meatball has never met but somehow recognizes.

This is a cozy point-and-click about a dog being a dog on a ship that defies physics. There are no enemies. No fail states. No timers. There is only: sniff, walk, sit, and occasionally bark at something important that the humans haven't noticed yet. They will notice eventually. Meatball has excellent judgment.

The game is set in The Ephergent's interior — corridors that grew around A1's quantum navigation architecture, now comfortable in ways no ship's layout should be. Meatball knows every shortcut. Meatball knows where Pixel hides the good treats. Meatball knows which closets contain things that are not treats but are interesting in a way treats are not.

Point. Click. Sniff. Walk into frame. Sit. Wait for the humans to catch up.

## Story Context

Meatball arrived on The Ephergent the way all the best crew members arrive: uninvited, certain he belonged, immediately in charge of morale.

He was Pixel's dog before the DRM collapsed. He was there when the frequency split. He rode in the lifeboat with her, A1, and Clive, and he did not panic, because Pixel was there and Pixel needed him not to panic. He has not stopped working since.

On the ship, Meatball has a job: supervise. He takes this job seriously. He inspects every new room thoroughly. He evaluates all visitors. He maintains a detailed mental database of who deserves treats and who does not. The crew does not know this database exists. They are aware of its effects.

Meatball's Big Walk is a day in that supervision. The player guides him through the ship in a loop that reveals the crew's routines, Barry's lingering presence, and the small kindnesses that hold a family together across impossible distances.

## Controls

- **Click to move** — Meatball walks to the location and stops
- **Click on objects** — Meatball investigates (sniffs, paws, sits next to, stares at)
- **Hover** — Cursor changes to indicate interactable objects
- **Meatball's internal states** — He reacts to the environment without player input: tail wags, ear perks, head tilts, occasional soft bark at nothing in particular

## Featured Crew

**Meatball** — The player character. A dog. A very good one. Golden retriever energy, border collie precision, the kind of intelligence that doesn't need words. He understands everything. He chooses not to comment. He sits when he's made his point.

**Pixel Paradox** — Captain, broadcaster, Meatball's person. She is trying to work. Meatball is nearby. She keeps glancing over. She is not annoyed. She will pat him in approximately four minutes.

**A1** — The ship. The espresso machine at the center of everything. Warm. Present. Making coffee that Meatball cannot taste but somehow knows the emotional temperature of. Meatball sits near A1 when Pixel is worried. He does not know why he does this. He just does.

**Clive** — Knee-high robot, glowing sphere head, fedora. Meatball treats Clive with the respect owed to a very old friend he has not met before. He lets Clive walk past without following. He waits for Clive to adjust the fedora. He has seen this gesture before, in a dream, or a smell, or a frequency he could not name. He does not question it.

## Barry's Presence

Barry Kowalski is not in this game. He is in every room.

The mug on A1's console. The charging alcove in the engine room that is the exact height of something small and glowing. The coffee ring stains on the console surface. The notch in the third corridor wall that someone carved with a small knife to mark a height measurement.

Meatball knows. Meatball does not have the vocabulary to say so. He sits next to the mug for a long time. He does not knock it over. He does not need to.

*"Smells like: looking for something. 23 years. Almost there."*
