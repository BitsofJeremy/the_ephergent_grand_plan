# Prime Material

**Frequency 1** — The "normal" world. Closest to what a human would recognize as Earth-adjacent.

Modern cities with glass and steel skylines, traffic noise, coffee shops on every corner, smartphones in every pocket. No visible sci-fi. The sky is the same sky you see when you look up right now. If anything seems slightly off — a shadow that lingers too long, a room that feels heavier than it should — people blame the weather.

## Signal

A steady, confident hum — the backbone note of the known spectrum. Strong, clear, utterly mundane to its inhabitants. They cannot hear it. To them, the world simply *is*. No one suspects that their reality is one station on an infinite dial.

## The Dimming

Prime Material sits at the center of the known spectrum — every other frequency's signal passes through it to reach the others. When the Builder Stations began to fail, Prime Material experienced the Drift as a slow increase in background frequency noise. Paranormal reports tick upward. Reddit threads about "glitches in the matrix" get longer. Thin spots — places where other frequencies brush close — are spreading.

## Stability

Stable — the most structurally solid of the five known frequencies, anchored by its central position in the spectrum. But stability is relative. The increasing frequency bleed-through suggests the buffer that Prime Material's position once provided is eroding. The center holds, but it holds less firmly each decade.
