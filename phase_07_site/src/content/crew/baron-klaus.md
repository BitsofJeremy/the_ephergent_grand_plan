# Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf

Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf is twelve inches tall, made of glazed ceramic, and carries himself with the absolute gravity of a man who has never once considered the possibility that his title might be decorative.

He is, in fact, the only gnome in the known multiverse who uses the honorific "Baron" without irony.

This is not delusion. Klaus is aware that "Baron" is a title from a Nocturne Aeturnus peerage system that predates the Sorrowful Moon's current phase by several centuries. He is aware that the Nocturne nobility dissolved its formal structure approximately two hundred years ago. He uses it anyway. The title is a uniform he wears with precision, with purpose, with the understanding that how you present yourself determines what you're capable of.

## Physical Description

Klaus is a ceramic garden gnome of the classic Nocturne Aeturnus artisan tradition — fired clay body with hand-painted detailing, approximately twelve inches tall. His construction predates the current Sorrowful Moon phase by an unknown interval; the glaze technique is an older method, producing a slightly deeper violet undertone than contemporary garden gnomes.

He should not be alive. He is, demonstrably, alive.

**The Hat:** Deep forest green, slightly askew, firmly planted. It has been on his head through every incident, every firefight, every frequency storm the Ephergent has survived. It does not fall off. This is either a property of the hat or a property of Klaus's will.

**The Coat:** Dark wool, mid-calf length, with more interior pockets than any coat of that size should accommodate. Klaus can produce from these pockets: a lockpick, a signal dampener, a specific DRM form (pre-completed), crystallized grief (it actually is), a compact frequency scanner of his own design, one snack of indeterminate origin, and a small notebook of case notes. The coat is painted onto him — he cannot remove it. He has no interest in removing it.

**The Face:** Classic Nocturne garden gnome painting — rosy cheeks, bright eyes, a broad and slightly foolish smile. This expression is completely misleading. The smile is a default setting from manufacture. His real expressions are communicated through the tilt of his hat, the angle of his stance, and the precise arrangement of his painted features. His eyes are steel-gray with violet flecks. They miss nothing.

## Voice

Klaus speaks in complete, polished sentences. His grammar is precise. His word choices are exact. He does not use contractions in professional settings. He tells you things sideways — the relevant information is distributed across context and implication, and your job is to assemble the picture yourself. He will provide the pieces. He will not assemble them for you. This is not condescension. He genuinely believes you can do it. He is usually right.

Klaus is funny. This surprises people who meet him. His humor does not land as jokes. It lands as observations that happen to be true in a way that illuminates something unexpected. The crew has learned to listen for the moment when Klaus says something that makes them laugh, because that moment usually contains a real insight.

**Klaus, observed:**
> *"The filed complaint makes three references to 'unprecedented circumstances.' In my experience, 'unprecedented circumstances' is what people call things they should have anticipated but didn't."*

> *"I have reviewed the evidence. I have drawn conclusions. The conclusions are unflattering. I am still deciding whether to share them."*

## The Investigator

Klaus notices everything. He notices: the recalibration pattern in Luminara's spectral analyzer that suggests she's been scanning biological frequencies she claims not to study. The specific angle of Clive's hat adjustment that indicates a memory surfacing. The way Pixel's frequency sensitivity manifests in her posture before she consciously registers the signal.

He notices what people are hiding. This is his professional background — he was an investigator in Nocturne Aeturnus. Someone is hiding something. The question is why. Klaus will wait for the answer. He is very patient. He is also very thorough.

He avoids discussing why he left Nocturne Aeturnus with such consistent, practiced skill that most of the crew doesn't realize they've never heard the explanation. When redirected, he produces genuinely interesting information about the current topic and the original question evaporates.
