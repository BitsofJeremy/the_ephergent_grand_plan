# Nano

Nano is fast. Not fast for an engineer, not fast for someone from Cogsworth where precision is religion and urgency is heresy. Nano is just *fast* — the kind of fast that makes people miss things, except Nano doesn't miss things because she moves so quickly through a space that she experiences it completely before anyone else has finished processing the first detail.

She is small, sharp, and constantly in motion even when she is standing still. She talks faster than anyone can track. She builds faster than anyone can follow. She processes the universe at a speed that should be incoherent but somehow isn't — because every word she says is precise, every technical reference is accurate, every leap in her logic is earned.

## The Running

Nano left Cogsworth because she understood something the frequency couldn't: precision without speed is a clock that shows the right time for a moment that has already passed. The universe is desynchronizing. The Drift is accelerating. You can calibrate a gear to perfect tolerance and by the time you've finished, the tolerance has shifted because the frequency itself has shifted.

She didn't wait. She moved. She became the fastest thing in the Space between frequencies, and she has been running ever since — not from something, but toward something she hasn't found yet.

The Ephergent found her four months into her running, sprinting along the hull of a derelict Builder Station at a speed that should not have been possible for a human body. Pixel hailed her. Nano didn't slow down. She couldn't. She had been running for so long that standing still felt like dying.

She joined because Pixel didn't ask questions. Nano will tell you she stayed for the engine room. She will not tell you the real reason: Pixel's broadcast was the first signal in four months that sounded like someone who had also been alone and was choosing to keep moving anyway.

## Physical Description

Nano is compact — wiry, low-centered, built for velocity rather than power. Weight forward, chin up, eyes already three steps ahead of her body. Her features are sharp, fox-like. Her eyes are amber — warm, bright, Cogsworth-calibrated — and they move constantly, tracking details, calculating clearances.

Her hair is short, practical, copper-red, and almost always sticking up in three different directions. She runs her hands through it while thinking, and she is always thinking, and she is always thinking about multiple things simultaneously.

She smells of machine oil and burnt sugar. The chaos of her hair is a side effect of the velocity of her cognition.

**When Nano's hands go still, something is wrong.** This is the only reliable diagnostic indicator for her emotional state. Her hands do not go still often.

## Voice

Nano talks fast. This is not a style choice. It is the only speed she has.

She picks up in the middle of a thought, assumes you've been following, and continues at a pace that requires active effort to track. She is not incoherent — every word is precise, every technical reference is accurate. She is just faster than the conversation expects her to be.

She says "— right, so —" a lot, bridging between thoughts she's already processed and thoughts she's explaining. She interrupts herself. She answers questions before they're finished because she's modeled where the question was going and arrived at the conclusion while the questioner was still formulating the words.

**Nano, observed:**
> *"— right, so the conduit's fused but that's not the actual problem, the actual problem is the conduit was fused by a feedback loop from the secondary relay which means someone recalibrated wrong and I'm going to find out who because this is a Cogsworth-standard relay and I know what Cogsworth-standard tolerances look like and this ain't it —"*

> *"I don't slow down. If I slow down I have to think about why I'm going fast. I know why I'm going fast. It's not the kind of thing you examine while you're still moving."*
