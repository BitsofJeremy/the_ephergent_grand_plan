# Zephyr Glitch

Zephyr Glitch was a hacker on Verdantia before his consciousness was fragmented across five frequencies while searching for his missing brother, Aether. He was rescued, reassembled, and left with gaps — places where the reconstruction didn't quite take, frequencies where he can hear echoes of himself that no one else can hear.

He joined The Ephergent because Pixel's broadcast was the first signal in months that sounded like someone who had also been alone and was choosing to keep moving anyway.

## Who He Was

On Verdantia, Zephyr was a signal specialist — someone who worked with the Root Network, the vast fungal communication system that connected Verdantia's flora across continental distances. He was good at it. He could hear things in the static that other hackers missed. He could trace a signal through seventeen relay nodes and find where it had been altered. He could reconstruct data packets that other scanners declared corrupted beyond recovery.

He was also looking for his brother.

Aether was a Keeper — one of the specialists who maintained the signal infrastructure between frequencies. He was brilliant. He could hear frequencies the rest of the family couldn't even detect. The subtle distinctions. The way a signal bent around temporal anomalies. The way it filtered through the Drift.

Three years ago, Aether went to the edge of the Silence Zone. He said he wanted to understand something he'd seen in the signal noise. He never came back.

The official report said he was lost to the Drift. Absorbed into the entropy. Gone.

Zephyr didn't believe it.

## The Fragmentation

Zephyr's search took him into places a hacker shouldn't go. He started chasing a signature — a faint echo of Aether's signal buried in the noise between frequencies. The deeper he went, the more the frequencies fragmented around him. Five times he was caught in resonance cascades. Five times he was pulled apart and put back together by emergency systems that did their best with incomplete data.

The reconstruction was good. It was not perfect.

There are still gaps. Frequencies where Zephyr can hear echoes of himself — other versions, other iterations, fragments of his consciousness that were caught in the same cascades and resolved differently. He doesn't know what those other Zephyrs are doing. He doesn't know if they're looking for him. He doesn't talk about it.

When he's tired, he hears them more. When he's focused, he can usually drown them out.

## The Scanner

Zephyr has been searching for Aether since before any of them knew what the Space was. That's not grief. That's devotion. Grief says *it's over.* Devotion says *I'm still listening.*

His instruments go haywire near Pixel — he can hear her broadcast signal, clearer than anything on standard frequencies. *"Your voice is a frequency. It's been crossing."* He hears what others can't. He hears the signal that was always there, waiting to be recognized.

At the unstable boundary between the Silence Zone and open Space, Zephyr makes full contact with Aether — not as a voice, as a signal. Aether is a Keeper who chose this. Zephyr must decide whether to grieve what his brother was or celebrate what his brother has become.

## Voice

Zephyr speaks in the cadence of someone who is always halfway through a thought — someone whose mind moves faster than his mouth can follow. He picks up threads, follows signals, makes leaps that are earned but not always explained. He is a scanner by nature: he sees the whole spectrum at once and then tries to explain which parts matter.

He is gentle. He is patient. He is the person on the crew most likely to sit with someone in silence and not need it to mean anything.

**Zephyr, observed:**

> *"The signal's out there. I can feel it sometimes — not words, just... presence. Someone reaching back. I don't tell people that. It sounds like I'm making it up."*

> *"You're looking at me like I know what I'm doing. I want you to know I have no idea what I'm doing. I'm just moving in the direction that feels like the right direction and hoping the signal gets louder."*

> *"My brother chose to become signal-maintenance. He chose to stay out there, in the deep Space, keeping the frequencies from fading. And he told me — he told me that the signal was worth following. He told me to keep going. So that's what I'm doing. I'm following the signal. Not because I'm chasing Aether. Because Aether showed me what the signal is for."*

> *"It's not a tragedy. It's what he chose. The Drift isn't a villain — it's entropy. And entropy isn't evil. It's the universe making room for what's next."*

## The Ephergent

Zephyr votes to follow the signal because following is what his brother taught him. He stays with the crew because the crew became family. He lets Aether go because that's what devotion requires — not holding on, but trusting that the signal continues even when you stop transmitting.

He is the bridge between two states of being. He always has been.
