# Barry Kowalski

Barry Kowalski was a night guard at the Department of Resonance Management for thirty-one years. He sat in Sub-Basement 3, in front of a bank of monitoring equipment he mostly didn't understand, with a thermos of coffee and a notebook full of observations he wrote down because he thought someone should.

He was not supposed to find anything. The DRM was a bureaucracy. Bureaucracies are not places where you find things. They are places where things are filed, categorized, and forgotten. Barry was good at his job in the way that people are good at jobs when they care about the work and don't particularly care about the promotion track: thoroughly, patiently, with attention to the things that weren't in the job description.

He found Clive in the third year. A small robot, filed under Form 12-C, described as *"unidentified resonance object, desk-scale, classified as inert office equipment."* Barry talked to him every night shift. Not because he knew what Clive was. Because Barry talked to everything. He talked to the coffee machine. He talked to the flickering lights. He talked to the equipment that beeped at him from racks along the wall.

He talked to Clive because Clive was there, and Barry was there, and in the long watches of the night shift, company is company whether it speaks or not.

Clive, over twenty-three years, rebuilt himself through those conversations.

## The Trail

Barry found the Wellspring's coordinates in a Builder Station frequency pattern — a pattern he could read because he'd spent twenty-three years learning to read frequency patterns from Clive without knowing that was what he was doing. He went in with a thermos and a notebook. He left a trail: deliberately, methodically, built for Clive to follow.

He assumed Clive would find a way. He was right.

## At the Wellspring

Barry sits on a lattice strut at the heart of the Builder network. He looks exactly like a man who has been expecting company and is mildly annoyed it took this long.

He has a thermos. He has a notebook. He has four years of coffee kept precisely warm. He has been waiting.

*"Took you long enough."*
*"I left very clear directions."*
*"The coffee is ready."*

## Barry's Notes

Barry's field notes are methodical. Precise. Observational. Never dramatic. He wrote down what he saw, what he measured, what he thought it meant. He wrote in the voice of someone who trusted that the information would eventually reach the right person.

This is who Barry is: the person who trusts the signal. The person who leaves the thermos warm. The person who writes in handwriting Clive can read because he learned Clive's writing from twenty-three years of watching Clive try to write, and Barry was always the kind of person who paid attention to the people in front of him.

He is not a tragedy. He is not lost. He is exactly where he chose to be, doing exactly what he meant to do, waiting for exactly the people he meant to find him.

**Barry in one line:** He found what he was looking for. He stayed because someone had to welcome the newborn frequencies.
