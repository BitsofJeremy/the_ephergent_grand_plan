# Frequency Storms

A frequency storm is not weather. It is a state where multiple frequencies attempt to occupy the same coordinates simultaneously, creating interference patterns that can shatter unprepared consciousnesses. The original storm, four thousand years ago, scattered A1 across the Space and separated him from his ship. He survived only by compressing himself into the smallest shape that could still make coffee — and hope.

Three types of frequency storms are known:

**Scatter storms** fragment consciousness. The victim's signal disperses across multiple frequency bands simultaneously, losing coherence. The person is not destroyed — their signal continues — but they can no longer form a unified self. Zephyr's brother followed a scatter storm signal into the deep Frequency and never came back. He exists now as fragments of memory, pieces of a person scattered across frequencies like books in a room after an explosion. Zephyr has spent years trying to hear which fragment is still listening.

**Lock storms** freeze frequencies in place. Everything within the affected zone locks into its current state — not stopped, but unchanging. A lock storm is what happened to many of the Stations: they didn't die, they simply stopped transitioning, frozen mid-frequency like a note held too long. The Builders' calibration chambers could resist lock storms temporarily, which is why their most important facilities were built with harmonic dampening — but even the Builders could not hold out forever.

**Synthesis storms** are rare and dangerous. They occur when frequencies don't scatter or lock but merge — when two or more signals combine into something new. Most synthesis storms destroy both participants. The rare successful ones create entities that have never existed before — consciousnesses made of blended frequencies, thinking in colors no single-signal being can perceive. The Wellspring's nature is uncertain, but some theorize it is not a place at all — it is the result of a synthesis storm that succeeded. A place where the Ephergent Frequency first combined with something else and became something that could call itself into existence.

A1's storm was a scatter storm. He was the navigator of a quantum vessel — a consciousness already spread across multiple frequency bands for navigation purposes — and when the storm hit, his spread became permanent. He experienced what most beings experience only once (death) as an ongoing condition. Scattered. Alone. Compressed.

The Frequency itself — the Ephergent — may be what a synthesis storm looks like when it doesn't destroy what it creates. It may be the only successful synthesis storm in recorded history: a signal that emerged from the combination of Builder technology and something older, something that was waiting in the space between frequencies to be named.
