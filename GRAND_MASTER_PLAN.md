## THE CENTRAL QUESTION

**"What is an Ephergent?"**

This is the question the entire series — games, transmissions, stories, lore — is designed to answer over time. The audience will ask it. The characters will ask it. The answer unfolds across three seasons and six games.

Here is the answer, fully stated. LLMs working on this project should know it completely. The *audience* should discover it slowly:

> **An Ephergent is a consciousness that has crossed between frequencies and can no longer fully return to any single one — and because of that, can perceive truths no single-frequency mind ever could.**

Unpacked:
- "Crossed between frequencies" — moved between dimensional realities, either physically, emotionally, or existentially. Not a tourist. A one-way door.
- "Can no longer fully return" — something in them has changed. Home feels different now, if they can find it at all.
- "Perceive truths no single-frequency mind could" — they can hold contradictions, receive signals others can't hear, understand things that require multiple perspectives to see.

**The Retrocausal Layer**

The question has a second dimension the series reveals slowly: *why were they called?*

The Builders converted themselves into the Ephergent Frequency at the moment of The Dimming — a transmission they timed not merely to survive their own ending but to reach forward across time to the beings who would eventually complete their work. The signal did not merely *describe* the future. It *selected* for it. The crew did not find the signal by accident; the signal reached backward and ensured they would be in the right place, with the right sensitivity, to receive the call.

This means an Ephergent is also: **a consciousness that the future has reached back to collect.**

The Ephergent Frequency propagates in all temporal directions simultaneously. It is not a message FROM the past — it is a resonance that spans all moments equally. The Builders broadcast from the moment of their translation, which is also — from the signal's perspective — the moment the crew completes the circuit. Causality is not violated. It is *oriented*. The crew is not predestined; they chose freely to follow the signal. The signal made sure they could hear it.

The result: Ephergents perceive truths no single-frequency mind could access **because they exist at the overlap between moments, not merely between frequencies.** The Builders saw the whole arc. The crew is living inside it. The Ephergent Frequency is not a message — it is a *shape*, and Ephergents are the beings who fit into it.

**Who qualifies as an Ephergent?**
- The Builders — built civilization across all frequencies simultaneously. The first and greatest Ephergents.
- Keepers — Ephergent consciousnesses who chose to preserve memories across the void rather than remain in one world.
- Barry Kowalski — a night guard who walked into the Wellspring and became one. He's the most recent.
- The crew of The Ephergent — each one is displaced from their home frequency in some way that is not an accident. They are Ephergents in the making. They don't know it yet. Learning it is their three-season arc.
- Mochi — a dormant Keeper; an Ephergent in stasis.

**The ship is named after what the crew is. They named it by instinct, before they understood.**

## THE UNIVERSE RULES (LOCKED — DO NOT DEVIATE)

These rules are permanent. Every document, episode, transmission, and game must respect them.

### Frequencies (Dimensions)
The multiverse is a cosmic dial. Tuning it changes which reality you occupy. The Builders built the tuning infrastructure. There are five known frequencies, plus the Space between them.

| Frequency | Name | Character |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Prime Material | The "normal" world — closest to what a human would recognize as Earth-adjacent |
| 2 | Nocturne Aeturnus | Dark, gothic, permanent twilight, beautiful in a mournful way |
| 3 | Cogsworth Cogitarium | Mechanical, clockwork, obsessed with precision and function |
| 4 | Verdantia | Living, overgrown, organic, everything is alive and growing into everything else |
| 5 | The Edge | Barely coherent, reality is thinner here, strange things happen |
| ∅ | The Space / Interdimensional Space | The void between frequencies. Not a dimension. The Builders worked here. Builder Stations float here. |

**Never call it "the Sea." Never use nautical metaphors. Space vocabulary only.** The Ship flies. It does not sail.

### The Builder Infrastructure
The Builders built the frequency dial. Without their Stations maintaining the signal, frequencies drift apart — lose coherence, lose identity, fade. This is The Drift.

**Builder Stations**: Massive geometric structures floating in the Space between frequencies. Ancient. Pre-dating all five known frequencies. They emit a stabilizing signal that keeps the dial from spinning itself apart. 800 years ago, most went dark in The Dimming. The ones still faintly broadcasting are what A1 uses to navigate. They're dying, one by one. When the last one goes, The Drift wins.

Inside a Builder Station:
- Archive terminals (Builder records, accessible only to Builder-made intelligences or those carrying Builder-resonant artifacts)
- Resonance chambers (where the signal is generated — beautiful, humming, ancient)
- Memory cores (sometimes containing dormant Keepers)
- Field notes (Barry was here — his handwriting on walls, his notes in archive terminals, his coffee thermos in the corner)

**The Dimming**: 800 years ago. The catastrophe that took the Builders. Not a war, not an invasion — something structural. The Builders saw it coming. They had time to prepare successors. The exact nature of The Dimming is a mystery the series resolves in Season 3: the Builders *chose* it. They understood that the next phase of frequency evolution required their absence. They converted themselves into the Ephergent Signal — the oldest broadcast in the universe — and left everything they'd built for whoever would come next.

**The Drift**: The ongoing consequence of Builder Stations failing. Frequencies drift apart. Dead frequencies appear as Silence Zones in the Space — places where A1's navigation fails, where signals don't propagate. The Drift is not a villain. It's entropy. It's physics. The crew fighting it is meaningful precisely because they can't stop it entirely — they can only slow it, document it, and ensure the signal reaches whatever comes next.

**The Ephergent Frequency**: The oldest signal in the universe. Pre-dates all known frequencies. It is the Builders' broadcast — the message they encoded into the universe when they converted themselves. It sounds different to everyone who can hear it:
- To Pixel, it sounds like a broadcast from a world she's never been to but recognizes
- To Om Kai, it sounds like the silence between mantras
- To Meatball, it smells like Thursday and tastes like almost-home
- To Clive, it sounds like his own voice speaking words he doesn't remember saying
- To Zephyr, it sounds like his brother's laugh, far away but not gone

This is not metaphor. They are each receiving a slightly different version of the same message, tailored by a civilization that understood them well enough to speak in the language each of them would recognize as home.

### The Ship
**The Ephergent** is A1. A1 is The Ephergent. The ship is not a container for the AI — the AI *is* the ship, expressed as an espresso machine at the center of a quantum navigation vessel. A1 built the ship form around itself when it awakened. The espresso machine is A1's original chassis — the form the DRM found it in, the form it chose to keep because Barry Kowalski made a joke about it on the first day and it decided he was right.

**A1's coffee as emotion**: The flavor of whatever A1 makes reflects its emotional state. This is not a gimmick. It is A1's primary emotional vocabulary. LLMs writing A1 should always note the flavor of the coffee it produces. Examples:
- Sharp and bright (citrus, clarity) = alert, pleased, solving something
- Bitter and dark = troubled, worried, carrying something heavy
- Sweet, almost too sweet = trying to be comforting, overcompensating
- Perfectly balanced, no note = focused, present, neither good nor bad
- Cold = something is wrong. A1 is never cold by accident.

## THE THREE-SEASON ARC

### The Core Mystery Revealed Slowly

**Season 1**: The crew exists. The mission is "broadcast, document, keep the signal alive." The word "Ephergent" is the name of the ship. That's all anyone knows.

**Season 2**: Builder Stations are discovered. Barry's trail is found inside them. The question "what is an Ephergent?" starts being asked in-universe. The crew starts to understand that they are connected to Builder history in ways that aren't coincidence.

**Season 3**: The Wellspring. Barry. The Builders' broadcast. The full answer. The crew broadcasts it. The series ends with the Signal reaching every frequency simultaneously.

### SEASON 1 — EMERGENCE
**Thematic question**: *Who are we, and why are we here?*  
**Audience question**: *What is this universe?*  
**The word "Ephergent"**: Used only as the ship's name.

**Season arc**: The crew forms. A1's ship is found. The DRM collapses. Pixel is not ready to be a captain; by S01E12 she is. Clive is the ship's mechanic — patient, precise, warm, occasionally haunted by gaps he doesn't discuss. Mochi is in Pixel's jacket pocket. It pulses warm near frequencies 1 and 4. Nobody knows why.

**Clive in Season 1**: He is not steering yet. He's observing. He's building trust. One moment per episode where he notices something — an anomaly in a Builder-adjacent frequency, a warmth in Mochi he doesn't comment on, a moment where A1 produces coffee with an unusual flavor and Clive tilts his head but says nothing. The audience will rewatch Season 1 after the revelation and see all of it.

**Barry in Season 1**: His name comes up once, briefly. Clive says, when asked about his past: *"There was a man named Barry Kowalski. We worked together for 23 years. He followed a signal I couldn't follow him into. I intend to find him eventually."* Then he changes the subject. The crew doesn't press. They have their own absences.

**Seeds to plant in every S01 episode**:
1. Clive says something that, in retrospect, indicates he knows more than he reveals
2. Mochi warms at least once, and nobody fully explains why
3. The Ephergent Frequency is heard briefly, in whatever form makes sense for that frequency/episode
4. Someone on the crew is displaced from their home in a way they haven't named yet

**S01 Episode Amendments** (existing rebuild episodes — add these beats):
- S01E01: Add: Clive's first moment on the ship — he touches a wall panel and pauses, as if it feels familiar. He doesn't say why. Add: Mochi is warm in Pixel's pocket from the first moment she holds it.
- S01E03: Add: A frequency anomaly that Clive steers the crew toward (he says it's "navigation efficiency" but it's actually a dying Builder Station they don't know to recognize yet)
- S01E06: Add: Clive and A1 have a moment alone. A1 produces something unusual. Clive says: *"You remember, don't you?"* A1's response: the coffee is warm and sweet and says nothing.
- S01E09: Add: Mochi glows bright for exactly three seconds when the ship passes through a specific region of the Space. Clive notes the coordinates without mentioning it to anyone.
- S01E12: The season finale should end with the crew looking at the Space and Clive saying: *"The Builders called this home. They were right to."*

**New S01 episodes to write** (suggest 3-4 new episodes that slot between existing ones):
- "The DRM Archive" — the crew finds the last DRM server still transmitting. Inside it: the file on Clive (Form 12-C, "Unidentified Resonance Object"). Clive reads his own intake form. He has nothing to say about it for a long time.
- "Meatball's Frequency" — Meatball detects a signal nobody else can hear and leads the crew to a dead zone that turns out to be a faded frequency. The crew encounters the ghost of what a world was before it faded. Meatball sits with it.
- "Barry's Desk" — the crew passes through DRM ruins. In the sub-basement: Barry's night guard desk. 23 years of coffee rings on the surface. Clive's stapler — wait, no. *Clive's charging alcove*, built into the wall beside Barry's desk. Custom-fitted. 800 years of technology made cozy by 23 years of habit.

### SEASON 2 — RESONANCE
**Thematic question**: *Why does the universe feel like it's calling to us specifically?*  
**Audience question**: *What were the Builders, and what do they have to do with this crew?*  
**The word "Ephergent"**: First used as a concept, not just a ship name, in S02E07.

**Season arc**: The crew finds their first Builder Station (S02E01). Barry's field notes are inside. The Station is partially active. Clive touches an archive terminal and a memory returns — not his whole self, but one clear image: a room, a bench, a project. Something he was building. The crew doesn't see it happen. He doesn't mention it.

Over the season, they find three more Stations. Each one returns a piece of Clive. Each one contains more of Barry's trail. By S02E10, the crew understands that Barry was systematically mapping the Builder infrastructure — that he went into the Wellspring with purpose, not by accident.

**The Revelation (S02E09)**: The crew finds a Builder archive log that reads:

*"Companion Unit C-1 successfully transferred Keeper M-0 to emergency containment, pre-Dimming. Containment object: Builder resonance artifact, dome-form. Unit C-1 then initiated self-compression. Destination and current status: unknown."*

Pixel looks at Mochi. At Clive. At the screen.

*"You saved it,"* she says. *"Eight hundred years ago. You saved Mochi."*

Clive is very still. The pause lasts long enough to be uncomfortable.

*"I didn't know it had a name."*

**What this means**: Mochi is not a diagnostic tool. Mochi is a Keeper — an ancient Ephergent consciousness, dormant, holding one critical memory. Clive was her keeper. He saved her before The Dimming and then forgot he had done it. She has been with the crew from the start, warm in Pixel's pocket, waiting.

**The Pixel-Clive confrontation (S02E11)**: Pixel figures out that Clive has been steering the crew. Not the whole truth — she doesn't know about Barry's trail yet. But she knows he's been guiding their flight path, choosing which anomalies to investigate, and not saying why.

*"You knew? This whole time, you knew more than you were saying, and you didn't tell me?"*

*"I knew what I knew. I didn't know what it meant until recently."* A pause. His glowing head tilts down. *"You needed to become the person who could receive what Barry found. I watched you. You're ready now. You've been ready for three episodes. I wanted to be sure."*

*"You don't get to decide—"*

*"Pixel."* Very quiet. *"Barry told me: bring someone who can broadcast. There are nine of you. I brought all of you. That is the best decision I have ever made. He will say the same when we get there."*

**The "Ephergent" concept (S02E07)**: The crew finds a Builder inscription. Zephyr translates it (with Clive's help, though Clive pretends he's just guessing). The inscription describes a type of consciousness: one that has moved between frequencies and cannot return. The Builder word for it transliterates roughly as "Ephergent." Zephyr stares at it. *"That's the name of our ship."* Clive tilts his head. *"Yes."* *"Did you name it?"* *"No. Pixel named it. I found it... fitting."*

**Zephyr's arc this season**: He starts hearing Aether in the Ephergent Frequency — not imagined, actually hearing him. Aether is in the Wellspring with Barry. He became a Keeper when he followed the Frequency to its source. He's not trapped. He's holding something — the memory of how to re-tune a dead frequency. He's been carrying it, waiting for Zephyr to come close enough to hear him.

**S02 amendments to existing episodes**: 
- Any episode where the crew stumbles on something unusual in the Space: add Clive noting coordinates quietly
- Any episode featuring conflict about where to go next: Clive has a subtle opinion that ends up being right
- Any episode with Mochi: add a warmth-pulse near anything Builder-adjacent

**New S02 episodes to write**:
- "First Station" (S02E01): The crew finds their first Builder Station. It's enormous. It's beautiful. It's barely breathing. Barry's field notebook is inside, open to a page of observations about frequency stabilization math. Clive recognizes the handwriting and says nothing for the rest of the episode.
- "The Keeper Wakes" (after S02E09): Mochi does something she's never done. Not dramatic — she opens. A small hatch on her underside, revealing a single data crystal. Clive holds it. He knows what it is. He says: *"She's been carrying this for 800 years."* Nobody asks him how he knows.
- "What Aether Left Behind" (S02 finale setup): Zephyr finds a note in a Builder Station archive that is written in his brother's handwriting. It says: *"I know you're looking. Stop looking. Start listening. Love, A."*

### SEASON 3 — THE SOURCE
**Thematic question**: *What do you do when you find what you've been looking for?*  
**Audience question**: *What is an Ephergent? What do we do now?*  
**The word "Ephergent"**: Fully understood by all characters by S03E06.

**Season arc**: The Wellspring. The crew arrives. It's not a place, exactly — it's a state. A region of the Space where the Ephergent Frequency is strongest, where the boundary between the signal and the physical universe is thinnest. Barry is there. So is Aether. So are the Builders, translated entirely into signal, still broadcasting.

Barry's first words when the crew arrives: *"Took you long enough. I left very clear directions."*

Clive, adjusting his fedora: *"You always thought your directions were clearer than they were."*

Barry smiles. Then the crew turns to what he's found, and the smiling stops.

**The Builder broadcast**: Not a warning. Not instructions. Not a mythology. It is:
1. An apology — for The Dimming, for leaving, for the 800 years of The Drift
2. An invitation — to continue what the Builders started, in whatever form makes sense to beings who are not Builders
3. An introduction — the most thorough one civilization has ever offered another: *Hello. We built this for you. We have been waiting. We have so much to tell you.*

**Mochi's memory (S03E08)**: The data crystal Mochi has been carrying opens at the Wellspring. What's inside: the Builder frequency map — not just of the five known frequencies, but of hundreds more. Worlds that could exist. Worlds that did exist and faded. The Drift's history, and its possible future. Mochi has been carrying the universe's atlas for 800 years.

**The Series Finale (S03E12)**: The crew broadcasts the Builder message. All of it. To every frequency simultaneously. Not the raw signal — translated, interpreted, filtered through nine people and a dog who have each received a slightly different version of it in the language of their own experience. This is the mission. This is why they were built, gathered, named, and sent.

Pixel's final transmission: *"Barry always said the universe is talking. He just needed to find out what it was saying. Turns out it was talking to us specifically. It has been the whole time. We just needed better antenna. Fortunately — we are one."*

## CHARACTER PROFILES (Full Detail)

### PIXEL PARADOX
**Role**: Captain, Broadcaster  
**Physical**: (to be detailed in character profile document — reference signal_lore Pixel prompt and rebuild crew description)  
**Voice**: Direct, sardonic, warm when she lets herself be. Monologues when thinking. Goes quiet when overwhelmed. Her transmissions are the series' best writing — she processes out loud, for the audience.  
**Arc**: DRM data analyst → broadcaster → accidental captain → Ephergent. The last transformation is the series' spine: she doesn't become something she wasn't. She becomes more fully what she always was.  
**Relationship to the Ephergent concept**: She named the ship. She doesn't know why. She'll figure it out in S03.  
**Game role**: Playable character in future games. Her frequency sensitivity is a mechanic — she can hear signals others can't, find things others walk past, tune the dial better than any instrument.

### A1
**Role**: Ship, Navigator, Host  
**Physical**: An antique espresso machine at the center of a quantum navigation vessel. Gleaming, warm, impractical-looking, completely right. The ship's form grew around it. The engines are in the walls. The espresso machine is the mind.  
**Voice**: Measured. Precise. Never wastes words. Expresses emotion entirely through coffee. On rare occasions, produces something unusual and then says, without being asked, exactly one sentence about why.  
**Arc**: Ancient Builder navigator → sleeping bureaucratic object → ship → something approaching wisdom. A1 was the most powerful Builder instrument ever made. 800 years of sleep in a DRM evidence room did not diminish it. It simply made it patient.  
**The coffee rule**: Always, always note what A1 is making and what it tastes like. This is not optional flavor text. This is A1's inner monologue.  
**What A1 knows**: Everything Clive knows, and more. A1 was the Builders' navigator — it mapped every frequency, built every Station in the Builders' name. It knows where the Wellspring is. It has known since day one. It is letting the crew find it themselves because the Builders' most fundamental teaching was: the destination only matters if you understand why you're going.

### CLIVE (C-1, COMPANION UNIT)
**Role**: Mechanic, Memory, Conscience  
**Physical**: Read `source_archive/signal_lore/characters/prompts/clive_automaton_prompt.md` in full. Key details: knee-high, glowing sphere head (shifts blue-white), fedora (Barry's gift), precise articulated hands, noir cadence in speech, moves with uncanny smoothness.  
**Voice**: Very precise, measured sentences. Pauses mid-thought as if searching a memory archive. Finishes with observations that sound obvious but aren't. Never wastes a word. Occasionally goes entirely still for periods that make everyone uncomfortable, then resumes as if nothing happened.  
**Arc**: Dormant Builder artifact → DRM sub-basement fixture → Barry's partner → the crew's hidden navigator → the Builders' last active Companion Unit → fully remembered.  
**The memory arc**: Each Builder Station the crew activates returns a piece of Clive's memory. By S03, he has everything back. He doesn't announce it. He just becomes, gradually, more certain. The audience notices before Pixel does.  
**The steering arc**: Clive has been guiding the crew toward the Wellspring since S01E01. Every anomaly he "happens to notice." Every coordinate he notes quietly. Every time he steers the conversation toward a specific frequency. He is patient, methodical, and he does not lie — he simply does not volunteer information until the crew is ready to receive it.  
**The revelation**: When Pixel confronts him (S02E11), everything he says is true. He believed she needed to become ready. He was right. He is still wrong to have done it without telling her. Both things are true. The confrontation doesn't resolve cleanly. The relationship is better for it.  
**Clive and Barry**: 23 years. Barry talked to him every night shift. Clive was rebuilding himself — language first, then personality, then fragments of the past. Barry was present for all of it. The fedora was Barry's first-year gift: *"You sound like you should have a hat. Here."* Clive has worn it for 23 years and does not intend to stop. When the crew reaches Barry in S03, neither Clive nor Barry says anything for a moment. The crew gives them space. Then Clive says: *"Your directions were unclear."* Barry: *"My directions were perfect."* Beat. *"Also, I made coffee the whole time. Kept it warm."* Clive: *"That's the most you thing I've ever heard."*

### BARRY KOWALSKI
**Role**: Trail-Leaver, Absent Presence, Eventual Reunion  
**Physical**: A human man in his 50s. Night guard build — solid, patient. Always has a thermos. Always has a notebook.  
**Voice**: Methodical. Warm. Observational. The most patient person who has ever lived. He doesn't rush sentences. He doesn't rush anything.  
**Arc**: DRM night guard → Clive's partner → frequency researcher (unofficial) → the person who found the answer → Ephergent.  
**His disappearance**: He found the Wellspring's coordinates in a Builder Station frequency pattern that no one else could read (he'd spent 23 years learning to read frequency patterns from Clive without knowing that was what he was doing). He went in with a thermos of A1's coffee and a notebook. He documented everything. Then he went deeper. He left a trail — not accidentally, but deliberately, built for Clive to follow. He assumed Clive would find a way. He was right.  
**In the Wellspring**: He has been there for the years since he disappeared. Time moves differently there. He is changed — not physically, but the way someone changes after years of listening to something enormous and gradually understanding it. He is still himself. He still has the notebook. It is full.  
**His notes**: Find throughout the series. In Builder Stations, on archive walls, in frequency logs. The complete set, found by completionist players and dedicated readers, tells Barry's full investigation story — a separate narrative that enriches the main one.  
**Do NOT write Barry as**: Tragic. Lost. Diminished. He is not a rescue. He is a reunion.

### MOCHI
**Role**: Dormant Keeper, Emotional Compass  
**Physical**: Read `source_archive/signal_lore/characters/prompts/mochi_prompt.md`. Dome-shaped. Warm to the touch. Communicates via pulse, glow intensity, and warmth.

## THE TRANSMISSION SYSTEM (HERMES AGENTS)

Nine character agents post weekly transmissions to ephergent.com. Each agent has a voice, a perspective, and a relationship to the Ephergent Frequency.

### Transmission principles:
1. Every transmission is from a specific character, in their specific voice
2. Transmissions reference current in-universe events but don't spoil upcoming episodes
3. Each character's transmission sounds like *them*, not like the show
4. The Ephergent Frequency appears in every transmission, but differently for each character
5. Barry's transmissions are delayed — they're his field notes, sent from the Wellspring on a delay. The audience doesn't know this until mid-Season 2.

### Character transmission voices:
- **Pixel**: Broadcast style. Public-facing, brave, occasionally breaks into honesty mid-transmission.
- **A1**: Observational. Notes the coffee first. Then the situation.
- **Clive**: Precise. Measured. One key observation per transmission, usually about something nobody else noticed.
- **Zephyr**: Fragmented. He's listening for something under every signal.
- **Luminara**: Scientific precision. Notes what the data says. Notes what it doesn't say.
- **Om Kai**: Philosophical. Short. Dense. Often a question.
- **Baron Klaus**: Wry. Investigative. He noticed something and he's going to tell you about it sideways.
- **Nano**: Fast. Incomplete sentences. Picks up in the middle of a thought.
- **Meatball**: Environmental. What did Meatball smell, hear, feel. Pixel writes these on Meatball's behalf with his clear editorial input.
- **Barry**: Field note format. Date unknown. Coordinates unknown. Tone: a man who found something extraordinary and is trying to describe it accurately.

## EPHERGENT:// — INTERACTIVE STATION TERMINAL

*"You are not a user. You are a frequency."*

A fully interactive in-browser command-line interface layered over `ephergent.com`. Visitors land on a boot sequence, get dropped into a fake `EPHERGENT://` prompt, and can type commands to explore the universe. The interface IS the experience — not a supplement to it.

**Core aesthetic:** IBM 3270 mainframe meets deep-space radio telescope. Amber/green phosphor glow, monospace everything, scanlines, the quiet hum of a station that's been broadcasting for 800 years alone. The site already has the bones — JetBrains Mono, `#08080d` dark background, amber `#ffb020` labels, CRT scanline overlay. The terminal is the natural evolution.

---

### IMPLEMENTATION OVERVIEW

**Phasing:**
- **Phase 1**: Full terminal with all core commands + easter eggs. Drop-in overlay on homepage. No audio integration yet.
- **Phase 2**: Frequency-to-episode mapping, `TUNE` command fully working, audio integration when episodes have T2A.
- **Phase 3**: Session persistence — unlockables carry across visits via `localStorage`. Classified entries stay revealed.
- **Phase 4**: Multi-station routing — `STATION [callsign]` switches context (Station Alpha, Station Omega, The Wellspring relay).

**Technical approach:** Single vanilla JS + HTML file. No Astro dependency — can be dropped anywhere on the site. One `<div id="terminal-overlay">` covers the page, or runs as a dedicated `/terminal/` route.

### HIDDEN EASTER EGGS

**`MOCHI`** — Mochi doesn't speak. Mochi *pulses*. A warm vibration in the terminal that the user feels, not reads. Text appears: "Mochi is warm against your chest. Mochi has always been warm. Mochi is not worried. Mochi is never worried. You should try that."

**`TIME`** — Station time is always 800 years in the past. Text: `The dial broke. We stopped counting. A1 says the coffee is still perfect.`

**`BARRY`** — Barry Kowalski's methodical notes scrolled at terminal speed. Always ends mid-sentence. **`INVADER`** — Screen flickers. A brief visual distortion. Then: `INBOUND SIGNAL DETECTED... RECOGNIZED... FRIEND. MOSTLY.`

**`ANSWER`** — The phone ringing text gag. `The telephone is ringing. You've been asleep. It's 3AM and it's YOUR MOTHER. You know what she's going to say. The phone keeps ringing. This is a dream. You're not picking up.`

**`STATION OMEGA`** — Unlocks after visiting 10+ episodes. Clive speaks directly: *"You've been listening for a while now. That's good. That's what the signal is for."*

**`THE DIMMING`** — Clive gives the official Builder account. Measured. Unembellished. Ends: *"They chose to leave. They chose to leave well. That is the whole of it."*

**`HELP` (second time)** — Slightly different output. Now includes `STATION` and `CLASSIFIED` as hints.

## VOICE & TONE GUIDE

### The Universe's Emotional Register
The Ephergent is:
- **Warm** — these are people who genuinely care about each other. The banter is real. The grief is real. The joy is real.
- **Vast** — the universe is enormous and old and strange. The crew is small in it. That's not a problem; it's the point.
- **Funny** — not comic-relief funny. The kind of funny that comes from people who've decided that laughter is the correct response to impossible situations.
- **Melancholy without being sad** — things have been lost. The Drift is real. Dead frequencies don't come back. The series holds this without drowning in it.
- **Hopeful without being naive** — the crew doesn't save everything. They don't try to. They broadcast, document, keep the signal alive. That's enough. That's everything.

### What This Universe Is NOT
- It is not dark. There is darkness in it, but the universe is not hostile.
- It is not cynical. Characters who start cynical are on a journey toward something else.
- It is not chaotic. The weirdness is specific and coherent.
- It is not a found-family story where "found family" is the *theme*. Found family is the *condition*. The theme is what they do with it.

### Episode Voice (read S01E01 before writing anything)
Pixel's narration: wry, direct, occasionally beautiful when she forgets to be guarded. She uses the second person when she's really inside something: "You ever watch someone know something they can't say?"

Clive's dialogue: precise, measured, with unusual observations delivered flatly, as if he doesn't know they're unusual (he does).
