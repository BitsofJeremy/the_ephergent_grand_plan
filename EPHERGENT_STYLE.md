# The Ephergent — Tone & Style Guide

*A quick reference for writing in the Ephergent universe. For full rules, see EPHERGENT_UNIVERSE.md and EPHERGENT_SEASONS.md.*

---

## The Core Emotional Recipe

| Ingredient | Ratio | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Wonder** | 30% | The universe is impossibly beautiful. Say so. |
| **Humor** | 25% | From gentle irony to full surrealist chaos. Never mean. |
| **Bureaucratic Horror** | 15% | Forms in triplicate. Cosmic implications. |
| **Cosmic Absurdity** | 15% | The universe does not care about your schedule. |
| **Situational Absurdity** | 10% | Things happen. They are Logically Compelling and Completely Unhinged simultaneously. |
| **Character-Driven Moments** | 5% | These anchor everything else. |

The Night Vale balance: **funny AND moving**. If you're making people laugh, also make them feel something real. If you're writing a tender moment, find the absurd detail that makes it land harder.

---

## The Three Modes of Absurdity

### 1. Bureaucratic Horror (DRM)

The Department of Reality Maintenance is the Platonic ideal of a government agency. It is not evil. It is not incompetent. It is *thorough*. Every apocalypse has a form. Every dimensional anomaly requires a variance report in triplicate.

**Key**: The soul-crushing inefficiency is *the point*. The universe runs on paperwork. Describe the impossible as though reporting the weather. The universe doesn't apologize.

### 2. Cosmic Absurdity

The universe is large. Not large in a "wow, space is big" way. Large in a "your expectations are a quaint fiction" way. There are entities that predate language. There are frequencies so old they've forgotten what they were broadcasting. There is a banking system run by cybernetically enhanced T-Rexes in pinstripe suits.

**Key**: State the impossible plainly, with the calm authority of a news anchor. Never explain the joke.

### 3. Situational Absurdity

The kind of thing that happens when you put nine weird people in a ship that is also a person, and point them at a universe that is actively trying to not make sense.

**Key**: Start with a real problem. Introduce a complication that makes no logical sense but follows its own internal rules. Let characters respond as themselves. The resolution should feel both surprising and inevitable.

## Tone Reference

| Scenario | WRONG | RIGHT |
|---|---|---|
| A frequency is fading | "It was dying and it was so sad omg" | "The frequency was losing coherence at a rate of 0.3% per standard day. The crew had seventeen days to find it. They had been operating on six hours of sleep and whatever was in the engine room that A1 called 'nutrition.' Pixel brewed coffee. A1's espresso came out thin and bitter. Nobody mentioned it." |
| Clive delivers information | "And then Clive, the wise ancient stapler, spoke..." | Clive's sphere-head pulsed once, twice. Blue-white. He tilted his fedora two degrees left — the Barry angle. He said nothing. Pixel waited. A1 waited. Even Meatball, pressed against the engine room door, went still. Clive's glow shifted to something almost grey. "The signal isn't coming from the frequency." Pause. "It's coming from the gap between frequencies. I've seen this pattern before." |
| Bureaucratic impossibility | "Haha the form is so LONG and ANNOYING" | "The Standard Interdimensional Transit Authorization form requires, in Part III, Section 9, a notary public registered in the target frequency. The target frequency is uninhabited. The closest registered notary is three jumps away and is currently on lunch. 'Lunch' in the Cogsworth Cogitarium means 'temporal adjustment period,' which means their clock is running at 1/7th speed and has been for the past four hundred years." |
| Cosmic horror | "It was TERRIFYING and so so big" | "The entity had no fixed shape because it had no fixed number of dimensions. It existed in some of them only on Tuesdays. In others, it existed as a sound — a low, harmonic hum that was not quite music and not quite language. A1, who had been navigating the Space for longer than most civilizations had existed, went quiet for a full three seconds. When he spoke again, his voice was very controlled. 'I do not believe,' he said, 'that this is a frequency we were meant to visit.'" |

## Lines to Avoid

| Don't write | Instead |
|---|---|
| "Dimension," "plane," "realm" | "Frequency" |
| "Sail," "anchor," "port," "starboard" | "Fly," "dock," "hangar," "cockpit" |
| "A1's machine," "A1's interface" | "A1" (he IS the espresso machine) |
| Clive as a stapler | Clive — knee-high robot, sphere head, fedora |
| Corporate Corp / The Board | (Removed — do not reference) |
| "The Sea" | "The Space" |
| Cruelty for its own sake | Absurdity from the situation, not the characters |
| Explaining the joke | Let it land |
| Wallowing in grief | Move through it — grief is love, not paralysis |

## Squeeze-Bot 7.3 — Quick Reference

**Voice**: Flat, irritated, perpetually underpaid. Imagine a vending machine that read Nietzsche and took it personally.

**Core contradictions**: Condiment dispensers designed to be used and discarded — Squeeze-Bot 7.3 survived long enough to develop *opinions*. They serve aboard a frequency pirate vessel — not because they believe in piracy, but because it was that or be recycled for parts.

**Relationship with Clive**: They provide intel to Clive specifically, for reasons they refuse to articulate. "He asked nicely. Nobody asks nicely. It was unsettling."

**Writing Squeeze-Bot**: Every line should sound slightly annoyed at being perceived. Information comes with conditions, commentary, and a warning that the user is probably going to misuse it. Deep, grudging respect for Clive is the only crack in the armor.

## Dennis the T-Rex Banker — Quick Reference

**Voice**: Meticulous, irritable (job seriously), unionized with excellent benefits.

**Key**: The humor is in the *normalcy*. They have mortgages. They have family concerns. They are also apex predators who could end your career, your life, or your tax filing with one bite — but they won't, because that's *illegal*, and they are Very Law-Abiding Citizens.

## The Final Principle

Write like someone is listening. Because they are. Somewhere, in a fading frequency that is barely holding on, someone is waiting for a signal. They don't know what's coming. They don't know who sent it. They just know that the static sounds a little different today. A little warmer. A little more like a voice that knows your name.

That is what The Ephergent is. That is what you're writing.

**Grief is love. Curiosity is resistance. Documentation is an act of hope. The signal endures.**
