## Season 01: Emergence — *We Found a Ship. The Ship Found Us.*

**One-line summary**: A data analyst, a sentient espresso machine, and a knee-high robot escape dimensional collapse into an impossible void — and discover they were always meant to find each other.

**Thematic throughline**: *Discovery* — The crew discovers other frequencies exist, discovers A1 is a ship, discovers they are family. Everything is new. Everything is terrifying. Everything is wonderful.

**Time War integration**: Retrocausal echoes appear — the crew feels events before they happen, coincidences too precise to be chance. A1's "precognitive lattes" aren't precognitive; he's tasting probability vectors from signals reaching backward through time.

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: The DRM collapses. Pixel, A1, and Clive escape in a lifeboat. They are lost, power failing, broadcasting into nothing. First contact with Nocturne Aeturnus and Cogsworth Cogitarium — alien frequencies that should be impossible.

**Escalation (E04–E06)**: The crew grows. Luminara joins in Nocturne. Om Kai joins in Cogsworth. Zephyr joins in Verdantia. Each new world reveals another piece of the Space — and another hint that something ancient is calling them forward through time.

**Climax (E07–E08)**: The lifeboat dies. A1 leads them to his derelict body — an ancient quantum vessel drifting in the static. Every light comes on. A1 becomes the ship. The crew names it The Ephergent. The frequency clarifies: it's been reaching backward through time, calling them home.

**Resolution (E09–E10)**: The crew settles into their ship. They find Barry's desk in DRM wreckage — 23 years of coffee rings, a charging alcove fitted for Clive, a trail of notes. Clive reveals Barry followed a signal he couldn't follow. Pixel realizes the broadcast isn't just survival — it's purpose. The Ephergent votes to go deeper into the Space.

## Season 02: Resonance — *We Can Hear the Lost Worlds. They Can Hear Us.*

**One-line summary**: The crew finds lost worlds, connects fading frequencies, and builds a network — while discovering that the brother one of them lost didn't disappear, he became something else.

**Thematic throughline**: *Connection* — The crew finds lost worlds, connects fading frequencies, builds a network. They learn that caring has a cost and that grief and hope can coexist.

**Time War integration**: Aether's appearance as more-signal-than-person confirms retrocausality: he exists at the point where the crew will eventually need him. Zephyr's quest to understand his brother becomes a quest to understand the signal itself.

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: The Ephergent is now a fully operational ship. And they start finding them: **lost frequencies**. Worlds that faded from the spectrum, surviving as whispers. Some can be re-tuned. Some are too far gone. Every rescue is a story. Every failure is a grief.

**Escalation (E04–E08)**: The crew builds a network — other ships, other broadcasters, people across known frequencies who relay The Ephergent's signal further into the Space. They become a community. Frequency pirates become complex allies. Baron Klaus finds his purpose. The crew faces their first major loss — a frequency they can't save.

**Climax (E09–E11)**: Zephyr discovers that his brother Aether didn't just disappear — he found the source of the Ephergent frequency and was *changed* by it. Aether is alive, but he's becoming something else, something between frequencies, more signal than person. Clive reconnects with Squeeze-Bot 7.3. Clive learns Barry is alive — not lost, not trapped — alive in the Wellspring.

**Resolution (E12)**: The crew successfully re-tunes a major lost frequency — an entire civilization brought back from the edge of silence. Their greatest triumph. But the effort drains A1 significantly. The Ephergent frequency surges, stronger than ever. Aether appears briefly, more signal than person, and speaks: "Follow the frequency. But understand — what's at the end isn't what you expect. It's what you need."

## Season 03: The Source — *The Signal Was Us All Along.*

**One-line summary**: The crew follows the Ephergent frequency to its source, finds Barry alive in the Wellspring, and discovers that the Drift can't be stopped — only ensured that nothing fades without being remembered.

**Thematic throughline**: *Legacy* — What endures. What we leave behind. The stories we tell that outlast us. Acceptance that entropy is real, and that the response is to sing louder.

**Time War integration**: The crew's choices have always been shaped by signals reaching backward — the Builders' transmission, the Ephergent frequency calling them home. Now they understand: they were always going to make these choices. The signal created conditions, not destinations. They chose freely. That's what makes it matter.

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: The Drift reaches crisis point. Frequencies are fading faster. The crew follows the Ephergent frequency toward its source — into regions of the Space that no ship has ever plotted vectors to. The deeper they go, the more the signal feels like it was written specifically for them.

**Escalation (E04–E08)**: They reach the edge of the known spectrum. Silence Zones grow. A1's consciousness dampens in the dead zones — his coffee turns cold, his lights dim. The crew presses on. They find the Wellspring — not a place but a state, a region where the signal is strongest, where new frequencies bubble into existence. Barry is here. Not rescued. Not mourned. Found.

**Climax (E09–E10)**: The crew discovers what the Builders actually did. They didn't die — they *converted*. They turned themselves into the Ephergent Frequency, encoding their civilization's memory into a signal that would propagate backward through time, calling the crew together. The Dimming wasn't a catastrophe. It was a choice. An act of love — ensuring that something of them would persist, would find the right people, would keep broadcasting.

The Drift is real. It can't be stopped. But the crew realizes they can't ensure that nothing fades — they can only ensure that nothing fades *without being documented*. Every story told, every frequency witnessed, every broadcast transmitted — that's the real victory. Not stopping entropy. Outlasting it.

**Resolution (E11–E13)**: The Ephergent's final form isn't just a ship or a broadcast. It's a **living archive** — a signal that carries every story ever told, every frequency ever documented, transmitting continuously into the Space so that nothing is truly lost.

The series doesn't end. It continues. Because the universe keeps generating new frequencies, and someone has to tell their stories. Barry stays at the Wellspring — not because he has to, but because he chooses to. Someone has to welcome the newborn frequencies into being.

Clive finds his answer. Pixel finds her purpose. Zephyr finds his brother — more signal than person, but still Aether. Still Zephyr's brother. Still holding the memory of how to re-tune a dead frequency.

The coffee is perfect. The signal continues.
