---

## Transmission Calendar Rules

1. **Every transmission is in-character.** The voice, cadence, and concerns are the character's — never the show's.
2. **Transmissions reference current events but never spoil future episodes.** They react to what has happened, not what will.
3. **The Ephergent Frequency appears in every transmission differently** — each character perceives and reports it through their own lens.
4. **Characters join the transmissions as they join the crew.** Nobody transmits before they exist in the story.
5. **Barry's transmissions are delayed field notes.** Date unknown. Coordinates unknown. The audience does not know they originate from the Wellspring. They read as dispatches from someone who found something extraordinary and is trying to describe it accurately. They begin appearing around Week 7.
6. **Meatball's transmissions are written by Pixel with Meatball's editorial input.** This is stated openly. Tail wags confirm. Grumbles dispute.

## Transmission Principles — Season 2 Handoff

By the end of Season 1's transmission calendar:

- **The audience knows Barry before the crew does.** His field notes have been arriving all season. The crew may or may not be receiving them — this is ambiguous by design.
- **Klaus is already investigating.** His two transmissions establish that he will uncover what the season has planted.
- **Luminara's Unified Frequency Hypothesis** gives Season 2 its scientific framework.
- **Zephyr has connected his brother's trail to the Ephergent Frequency.** Season 2 follows both.
- **Every character's voice is established.** The audience can identify a transmission's author from the first sentence.
- **The Ephergent Frequency has been named, measured, felt, mourned, and followed** — but never explained.

Season 2 transmissions begin with the crew in deep water, following a signal that Barry placed decades ago, toward a place he is still carefully documenting, one field note at a time.
