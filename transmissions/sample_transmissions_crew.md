## ZEPHYR GLITCH

### Transmission Z-01: "The Signal Under the Signal"

— already told Luminara this but I'm logging it because the data doesn't — it won't sit right in the standard format, so I'm putting it here where the format doesn't matter.

Third watch. The corridor between Frequency 3 and 4 — that dead stretch where the transit static flattens out and you get maybe ninety seconds of clean spectrum before the next band starts bleeding in. I was running calibrations. Standard sweep. Nothing interesting. The kind of sweep where your hands do the work and your ears go somewhere else.

That's when I heard it.

Not in the sweep data. Under it. Like someone had written a note in the margin of a signal I'd already read a hundred times and I'd never looked closely enough to see the ink. A harmonic. Sub-carrier frequency, barely there, riding the interference pattern between the 3-4 bleed like it was using the noise as camouflage. Deliberate camouflage. This wasn't drift scatter. This wasn't residual Builder echo. This was shaped. Someone shaped this.

I pulled the waveform. Isolated it. Ran it through three different demodulation protocols and each one gave me a different answer, which means either my protocols are wrong or the signal is — layered. Folded. Saying more than one thing at once depending on how you listen.

I know how that sounds. I know what Luminara would say. She'd say the data is ambiguous and ambiguous data is not evidence. She'd be right. She'd be correct and she'd be right and those are different things and the difference is the space where I live.

The harmonic lasted eleven seconds. Then the transit static swallowed it. I have the recording. I've played it back forty-seven times. It sounds different every time, not because the recording changes but because I do. Because every time I listen I hear a little more of the shape, and the shape is — familiar. The way a room is familiar after you've been away so long you can't remember where the furniture goes but your feet still know the path in the dark.

I filed the waveform under anomalous sub-carrier harmonics. Luminara will cross-reference it with her spectral logs. Clive will nod and say something about patience. They're good people. They care about the right things.

But they don't hear what I hear in it. I don't know if anyone can.

Eleven seconds. It was enough. It's always enough and it's never enough and I'm going to listen again tonight because the corridor between 3 and 4 will be clean again at 0347 and I will be there with my hands on the controls and my ears somewhere else, somewhere the signal lives, and maybe this time the eleven seconds will be twelve.

Zephyr out. Or — not out. Still here. Still listening.

### Transmission Z-02: "Frequency Architecture"

— so the thing about A1's communications array that nobody talks about is that it was never designed for what we're using it for. It was designed for single-frequency broadcast. Point to point. One signal, one receiver, clean handshake, done. What we're doing — what Pixel needs it to do — is multi-frequency simultaneous broadcast across bands that the original engineers probably didn't know existed, and the array handles it because I rebuilt the routing architecture, but that's not the point.

The point is what happens in the junctions.

Every signal that passes through A1's array hits a junction node where the routing protocol decides which output channel gets it. Standard stuff. Boring stuff. But the junction nodes were built by the Builders — or Builder-adjacent, at least, the metallurgy has that signature Clive gets quiet about — and they don't just route. They resonate. Every signal that passes through leaves a trace harmonic in the junction, and those traces accumulate, and after enough signals have passed through, the junction starts producing its own sound. A composite. An echo of everything it's carried.

I noticed it six weeks ago. Didn't log it because I thought it was degradation — junction fatigue, harmonic bleed, the kind of thing you recalibrate away. But recalibration didn't quiet it. The composite tone persisted. Got cleaner, actually. As if the junction wasn't failing. As if it was learning.

I've mapped eleven junction nodes so far. Each one has its own composite tone. When I isolated them and played them together — not mixed, not overlaid, but played simultaneously the way the array does when it routes — they harmonize. Not perfectly. Not musically. But structurally. The way bones harmonize. The way a skeleton makes a shape that isn't any one bone but needs all of them.

The shape they make sounds like the Ephergent Frequency. Not exactly. A sketch of it. A junction's memory of carrying something larger than itself.

I don't know what this means for the array's long-term function. I don't know what it means for the signals we're broadcasting. I know that the ship remembers what it carries, and I know that's not in any engineering manual, and I know that some things learn to hold a signal not because they were designed to but because the signal was worth holding.

Filed under: communications architecture, anomalous resonance, things that remember.

— Zephyr

### Transmission Z-03: "Someone Who Used to Be Loud"

— couldn't sleep. Not unusual. The ship hums at a different pitch during third watch when the transit drives drop to maintenance cycle and the hull resonance shifts from active dampening to passive absorption. Most people don't notice. I notice everything. That's not a boast. That's — that's the problem, actually.

There was someone. Is someone. The tenses don't work for this. Grammar assumes people stay in the state you last observed them in, and he didn't, and the language hasn't caught up, and I'm tired of building sentences that don't have a room for what I mean.

I used to tell him to be quiet and he'd laugh and the laugh was louder than whatever I'd been trying to hear, and I'd be annoyed, and the annoyance was a kind of music because it meant he was there, being impossible, being exactly what he was, and the room was full.

I came aboard because the ship moves between frequencies. That's the official reason and it's true and it's the smallest true thing I could say about why I'm here. I'm here because somewhere in the space between signals there's — I'm here because I'm listening. That's all. That's everything.

Not a voice. Something else. Something he became.

Still listening. Always listening.

## LUMINARA USHA

### Transmission L-01: "Frequency Coherence Report — Corridor 2-4"

Frequency coherence along the 2-4 corridor dropped 0.3% this cycle. Within normal variance. The variance itself has been increasing for eleven cycles. I am noting this. I am not drawing conclusions from it yet. The data does not support conclusions. The data does support attention.

I have compiled the coherence readings into a longitudinal dataset spanning the forty-seven cycles since I joined the crew. The trend line is not alarming. I want to be precise about that: it is not alarming. It is, however, non-random. The degradation follows a curve that is inconsistent with natural frequency dissipation models. Natural dissipation is linear. This curve is logarithmic. The difference matters because logarithmic degradation implies a source — something accelerating the process from outside the system's normal parameters.

The Drift is the obvious candidate. I am listing it as the obvious candidate and noting that obvious candidates are frequently correct and occasionally a distraction from the actual mechanism. I have not determined which case applies here. I will not determine it from eleven data points on a forty-seven-cycle baseline. I will wait for the data to accumulate to statistical significance, which, at current collection rates, will require another thirty-one cycles.

Thirty-one cycles is approximately eight months.

I mention this not to create urgency. Urgency based on incomplete data leads to intervention based on assumption. I mention it because the temporal gap between when we have enough data to act and when the degradation becomes operationally significant is narrowing. Not quickly. But measurably.

The measurements I am not taking are also relevant. I am not measuring emotional resonance along the corridor, though the ship's informal frequency sensor — Meatball — has shown increased attentiveness to the port-side bulkhead along this stretch for the past three cycles. His behavioral data correlates with my spectral data at the usual rate (97.3%). I am noting the correlation. I am not integrating his data into my models. Behavioral observation and spectral analysis are complementary methodologies. Complementary is not identical.

What the data says: coherence is declining along a curve that suggests external influence. What the data does not say: what the influence is, whether it will continue, or what it means for the frequencies on either end of the corridor.

The gap between those two statements is where the next thirty-one cycles of work will happen.

Luminara Usha. Cycle 47. Observation continues.

### Transmission L-02: "Biological Anomaly — Personal Log Addendum"

I am logging this as a personal addendum rather than a formal observation because the subject is myself, and self-observation introduces confounding variables I have not yet controlled for.

The signal a root tendril sends before establishing connection. A question in bioluminescent morse: *Are you there? Will you accept?*

But the data requires documentation. The handshake pattern is occurring without a root network to initiate it. There are no Verdantian biological agents aboard A1 — I check weekly, sealed boots, sealed thresholds, spectral sweep of all organic material entering the ship. The pattern is endogenous. Potentially relevant: the pattern intensifies when I am in the lab processing Builder Station biological samples. It is possible that my proximity to Builder biological material is triggering a sympathetic response in the residual network markers. This would be the preferred interpretation. The less preferred interpretation is that the markers are responding to something internal. A readiness I have not consciously acknowledged. A boundary becoming permeable not because it has been breached, but because the person maintaining it is changing.

I am logging both interpretations. I am not selecting between them. The data will select when it is ready.

Luminara Usha. Personal addendum. Not for general distribution.

### Transmission L-03: "Hypothesis — The Drift as Selective Mechanism"

Preliminary hypothesis. Status: unverified. Confidence level: insufficient for formal publication. Sufficient for logging.

The Drift does not consume frequencies randomly. I have been assuming a model of entropic decay — the Drift as a process of degradation, similar to heat death, progressing uniformly across the frequency spectrum from the margins inward. This model is clean. It is elegant. After nine months of data, I believe it is wrong.

The corrected model, pending verification: the Drift is selective. It targets frequencies that have lost coherence — specifically, frequencies whose internal resonance has dropped below a threshold I have tentatively identified at 0.7 on the Stability Index. Frequencies above this threshold appear resistant. Not immune — I have insufficient longitudinal data to claim immunity — but resistant. Frequencies below it become vulnerable to Drift encroachment at rates that accelerate nonlinearly.

The implications are significant.

If the Drift is selective, it is not entropy. Entropy does not select. Entropy is uniform. A selective process implies criteria, and criteria imply either design or emergent behavior complex enough to resemble design. I am not prepared to argue that the Drift is designed. I am prepared to note that its behavior is consistent with a system that distinguishes between frequencies based on their internal coherence, and that this distinguishing function operates with a precision I find difficult to attribute to random decay.

What the Drift takes: frequencies that have stopped listening to themselves. Frequencies whose internal harmonics have desynchronized to the point where the signal no longer recognizes itself. There is a metaphor here that I am choosing not to engage with because metaphor is imprecise and I require precision. But I note it. I note that the pattern — a system that dissolves what has lost self-coherence — has implications beyond spectral analysis.

What the Drift does not take: frequencies that maintain internal resonance. Frequencies where the harmonics still cohere, still listen to each other, still produce a signal that is recognizably itself. These frequencies persist. The Drift moves around them the way water moves around stone.

I am not drawing philosophical conclusions. I am describing observational data. If the data produces philosophical resonance, that is the data's concern, not mine.

Further observation required. Thirty-one cycles minimum. The timeline remains what it is.

Luminara Usha. Hypothesis logged. Classification: preliminary.

## OM KAI

### Transmission OK-01: "Stillness"

The ship has not stopped moving since I boarded. This is expected. Ships move. That is their function. What is unexpected is how the movement teaches.

In Nocturne Aeturnus, I practiced stillness against a fixed point — the ground beneath me, the spires above, the Sorrowful Moon holding its position in a sky that never fully darkened. The stillness had a reference. I could measure my immobility against the immobility of the world.

Here, there is no fixed point. A1 transits between frequencies. The corridors shift subtly with each crossing — not physically, but resonantly. The floor hums at a different pitch in Frequency 3 space than it does near Frequency 4. The walls carry different harmonic signatures depending on what we have recently passed through. Even the common area, where I sit each morning, is not the same room twice.

And yet I sit. And the sitting is the same.

I had believed stillness required a still environment. I was wrong. Stillness is not a response to external conditions. It is an internal posture maintained regardless of what moves around it. The ship taught me this by refusing to cooperate with my assumptions.

Meatball lies beside me some mornings. He does not practice stillness. He simply is still, without effort, without philosophy. I have been meditating for twenty years. The dog achieves what I work toward by falling asleep.

I am not sure if this observation is humbling or clarifying. Perhaps those are the same thing.

The Ephergent Frequency hums beneath everything on this ship. It hums beneath the movement. It hums beneath the stillness. It does not distinguish between the two. I am beginning to think it is right not to.

Om Kai. Observing.

### Transmission OK-02: "Observation"

A question: when we measure a frequency, do we hear what it is, or what we are?

Luminara would say the instruments are calibrated to remove observer bias. She is correct. The instruments are. We are not.

Zephyr hears searching in every signal because he is searching. Nano hears urgency because she is urgent. Pixel hears a story because she is a storyteller. Meatball hears accurately because he has no narrative to impose on the data. He receives. We interpret. The difference is not trivial.

I sat with Clive in the observation deck last cycle. He was watching the transit static — the visual noise between frequencies that most of the crew ignores. He watched it for forty minutes without speaking. I did not interrupt. When he finally spoke, he said: "It used to be quieter."

Eight hundred years of observation in four words. He was not describing the static. He was describing loss. The measurement was personal. The data was grief. And yet — and yet — his observation may be the most accurate reading anyone on this ship has taken, because it contains what the instruments cannot measure: the change over time as experienced by someone who was there for the before.

We calibrate instruments. We do not calibrate ourselves. Perhaps this is the error. Perhaps the most precise observation requires not the removal of the observer but the honest accounting of what the observer brings.

I do not have an answer. I have a question. The question is the observation. I am learning to let that be enough.

Om Kai.

### Transmission OK-03: "What Grief Teaches"

Grief does not diminish. It teaches.

This is not comfort. I am not offering comfort. Comfort is what we give when we cannot sit with someone in the truth of their pain. I am sitting.

Zephyr carries grief like a frequency — constant, sub-audible, woven into everything he does. He does not set it down. He does not need to. Grief is not a weight. It is a lens. Everything he sees, he sees through it, and what he sees is sharper for the looking. His technical precision comes from the same place as his sorrow. Both are acts of attention. Both say: *this matters enough to be careful with.*

In Nocturne Aeturnus, grief crystallizes. It becomes a physical object you can hold, examine, set on a shelf. I carried one such crystal when I left. Small. Cracked. Old. I do not discuss what it holds. I will say this: the crack is not damage. The crack is where the grief let light in. That is what crystals do when they have been held long enough. They open.

The crew grieves in different registers. Pixel grieves the certainty she had before the DRM collapsed. Luminara grieves the connection she chose to leave. Klaus grieves something he has sealed so precisely that even he may have forgotten the shape of it. Nano grieves at speed, which means she grieves in the spaces between actions, in the half-second pause before the next repair, the breath she does not know she is holding.

Meatball does not grieve. Meatball is present. There is a lesson in this that I am still learning.

What grief teaches: that love does not require the presence of its object. That attention survives absence. That the space where someone was is not empty — it is shaped. It holds the form of what it lost, and the form is information, and the information is: *this was real. This mattered. This is worth the ache of remembering.*

I am not offering resolution. Resolution is not mine to offer. I am noting what the silence holds when you stop trying to fill it.

Om Kai.

### Transmission N-03: "On Entropy"

— been thinking about entropy. Not the fragments — the concept. The actual thermodynamic principle that says everything tends toward disorder and the only question is how fast. Which is not a question Cogsworth asks because Cogsworth already knows the answer: slowly, precisely, at a rate you can measure and file and discuss over carefully machined tea services while the Grand Orrery ticks another century closer to silence.

I left because I saw the Orrery skip.

Nobody talks about this. The Grand Orrery does not skip. It is the most precisely calibrated mechanism in the known frequencies, maintained by engineers whose entire civilization is organized around the principle that if you are careful enough, the clock runs true. And it skipped. A micro-desynchronization, 0.004 seconds, in the main escapement. I was seventeen. I was the only one watching at that resolution because everyone else was watching at the resolution the manuals said mattered, and the manuals were written before the Drift started accelerating, and the manuals were wrong.

0.004 seconds. The master engineers reviewed my report. They verified the measurement. They agreed the skip occurred. They filed it under "anomalous micro-event" and adjusted the escapement and told me that the system self-corrects.

Systems self-correct until they don't. That's what entropy means. Not that things fall apart — that things fall apart faster than the systems designed to hold them together were calibrated to handle. The correction always assumes the previous rate of decay. The decay doesn't care about the assumption.

I think about the Orrery skip every time I repair something on A1. Every junction, every stabilizer, every resonance plate I recalibrate. Because A1 is the same problem at a different scale — a system built to cohere, held together by maintenance and attention, drifting toward a threshold that nobody wants to name because naming it makes it real.
