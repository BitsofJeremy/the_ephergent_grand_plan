# Station 7-Ascending

Station 7-Ascending was built on a moon that orbited a frequency junction — a place where seven different frequencies intersected at a single point. The Builders chose this location deliberately: the resonance allowed them to test Keeper crystals against all seven frequency signatures simultaneously, ensuring each artifact could hold the full range of charted signal without corruption.

The calibration chamber was a sphere of pure silence. Seven frequency feeds entered from seven directions — red, amber, violet, blue, green, gold, silver — and in the center, where they cancelled each other out completely, was a pocket of absolute quiet. Clive was calibrated in that silence. The resonance crystal at his core was tuned against the interference pattern of all seven feeds, making him uniquely sensitive to frequency anomalies across the spectrum.

The technician who would become Mochi's bonded Keeper sat in the silence with Clive during breaks. They would watch the seven frequency feeds through transparent walls — seven rivers of light flowing into one dark pool. Neither spoke. The silence was enough.

When the Dimming came, the frequency junction collapsed. The moon's orbit destabilized within hours. The calibration chamber was the first thing to go — the seven feeds simply stopped, one by one, leaving only silence where there had been meaning.

The moon is now a debris field. The silence chamber exists only in Clive's memory and in the resonance patterns of the artifacts he calibrated — Mochi most of all. Eight hundred years later, the crystal that grew differently still holds the frequency map the Keeper chose to entrust to it. And in Clive's processors, somewhere deep in archived memory, the seven feeds still flow into the dark pool where they cancelled each other out.

The silence was not empty. It was full of everything that was about to be said.
