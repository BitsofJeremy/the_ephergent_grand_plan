# The Frequency System

## Lore Atlas Entry — The Ephergent Project

*Classification: Foundational Physics — Frequency Architecture Reference*
*Status: Definitive reference document for all frequency-related worldbuilding*
*Terminology lock: Always "frequencies" — never dimensions, planes, or realms*

---

## Overview

The multiverse is a cosmic dial. Tuning it changes which reality you occupy. The Builders built the tuning infrastructure — the Stations, the stabilization network, the architecture that holds each frequency in coherence. There are five known frequencies, plus the Space between them. Hundreds more once existed. Some have faded. Some are silent. The dial is wider than anyone alive has mapped, and it is slowly losing stations.

Frequencies are not parallel worlds stacked like pages. They are resonances — stations on an infinite cosmic radio dial. Each hums at its own pitch, carries its own physics, sustains its own way of being real. A phase-shifter can snap you between known stations like pressing preset buttons on a car radio. To traverse the space between them — to exist in the static, to plot a vector through the hum and shimmer — you need a ship that can hold coherence between frequencies. You need the Ephergent.

The five known frequencies are not all there is. The Ephergent's mission is to find the ones that have gone quiet, hear them, and help them remember how to sing.

## I. The Five Known Frequencies

### Frequency 1 — Prime Material

**Character**: The "normal" world. Closest to what a human would recognize as Earth-adjacent. Modern cities with glass and steel skylines, traffic noise, coffee shops on every corner, smartphones in every pocket. No visible sci-fi. The sky is the same sky you see when you look up right now. If anything seems slightly off — a shadow that lingers too long, a room that feels heavier than it should — people blame the weather.

**Signal character**: A steady, confident hum — the backbone note of the known spectrum. Strong, clear, utterly mundane to its inhabitants. They cannot hear it. To them, the world simply *is*. No one suspects that their reality is one station on an infinite dial.

**Crew members from Prime Material**:
- **Pixel Paradox** — Captain and Broadcaster. Too sensitive to frequencies from birth; the Signal found her first. She was always slightly out of step with her own world, picking up static no one else could hear.
- **Meatball** — Emotional Compass. A Rottweiler with the frequency sensitivity of a Builder instrument. Frequency-sensitive in ways that defy measurement.
- **Barry Kowalski** — (absent) Trail-Leaver. A night guard who walked into the Wellspring and became an Ephergent. Prime Material's most unlikely explorer, armed with a thermos and twenty-three years of patience.

**How The Dimming affected it**: Prime Material sits at the center of the known spectrum — every other frequency's signal passes through it to reach the others. When the Builder Stations began to fail, Prime Material experienced the Drift as a slow increase in background frequency noise. Paranormal reports tick upward. Reddit threads about "glitches in the matrix" get longer. Thin spots — places where other frequencies brush close — are spreading. The world hums along, blissfully ignorant, but something is shifting beneath the surface, and no one in Prime Material has the framework to understand what.

**Current stability**: Stable — the most structurally solid of the five known frequencies, anchored by its central position in the spectrum. But stability is relative. The increasing frequency bleed-through suggests the buffer that Prime Material's position once provided is eroding. The center holds, but it holds less firmly each decade.

**Nearest Builder Station**: **Station 3-Resonant** — floating in open Space between Frequencies 1 and 3, where Prime Material's familiar solidity and Cogsworth Cogitarium's clockwork precision create faint geometric shimmer in the void. Its purpose: harmonic stabilization of the Frequency 1–3 corridor. Currently operating at approximately 4% of original capacity. Barely breathing. Has been barely breathing for eight hundred years.

### Frequency 2 — Nocturne Aeturnus

**Character**: Dark, gothic, permanent twilight, beautiful in a mournful way. Deep indigo, purple, and midnight blue. The air is thick with petrichor and ancient stone. Gothic spires rise from mist-shrouded valleys. Candlelight flickers in windows of estates built from solidified emotion. Everything here feels like the most beautiful moment of dusk, stretched into eternity.

**Signal character**: A low, resonant chord — like a cello played in a cathedral. Melancholy and gorgeous. When you tune in, you feel it in your chest before you hear it. The signal carries emotional weight; receivers sometimes weep without knowing why.

**Crew members from Nocturne Aeturnus**:
- **Om Kai** — Philosophy and Counsel. Chose stillness in a frequency that never stops moving. In a world where emotions crystallize into physical substance, Om Kai found the silence between mantras and made it his home.
- **Baron Klaus** — Procurement and Investigation. Klaus doesn't talk about why he left Nocturne Aeturnus. He will, in Season 3. Whatever drove him from that twilight world left marks he covers with competence and a very specific approach to problem-solving.

**How The Dimming affected it**: Nocturne Aeturnus is particularly vulnerable to the Drift. When the Builder Stations began failing, emotional resonance across the frequency weakened. Emotion crystallization — the economic engine and cultural foundation of Nocturne society — became unstable. Crystals crack without warning. Moods become unpredictable, swinging from grief to ecstasy in ways that terrify a culture built on emotional authenticity. The Sorrowful Moon, the celestial body that amplifies Melancholic Joy across the entire frequency, flickers. Some nights it dims entirely, and the darkness that follows is not twilight but absence. Nocturne needs signal the way a garden needs rain, and for eight hundred years, the rain has been drying up.

**Current stability**: Fragile. The Drift hits emotional frequencies hardest because their coherence depends on resonance — and resonance is exactly what fading Stations can no longer sustain. Nocturne Aeturnus is not dying, but it is growing quieter. The crystals that form now are smaller, less complex, more prone to fracture. The culture endures, but the emotional depth that once defined it is shallowing. Residents who remember what their grandparents' grief looked like — vast, blue, mineral, permanent — mourn the thin crystals their own sorrows produce.

**Nearest Builder Station**: **Station 5-Meridian** — suspended at a convergence point equidistant from Frequencies 2, 3, and 4, where Nocturne's twilight violet, Cogsworth's amber precision, and Verdantia's living green create tricolor interference patterns. Intermittent signal, averaging 7% output with spikes to 12%.

### Frequency 3 — Cogsworth Cogitarium

**Character**: Mechanical, clockwork, obsessed with precision and function. Brass, copper, and polished mahogany. The entire frequency is a sprawling clockwork city — gears the size of buildings interlock with delicate mechanisms no larger than a watch. Steam curls from a thousand vents. The air carries aromas of roasted clockwork nuts, burnt sugar, and polished brass. Everything ticks. Everything turns. Everything is precisely on time.

**Signal character**: Metronomic — a precise, clicking rhythm underlaid with the hiss of steam and the whir of gears. It sounds like a clock shop at midnight: every timepiece slightly different, all somehow in sync. The signal is hypnotic. Spend too long listening and you start walking in time with it.

**Crew members from Cogsworth Cogitarium**:
- **Nano** — Engineering and Speed. The only one who can outrun the machines she builds. Nano carries Cogsworth's precision in her bones — the instinct for timing, the need for things to *work* — but she left because she understood something the Cogitarium's engineers could not: you cannot fix entropy with wrenches.

**How The Dimming affected it**: Cogsworth's clocks are the canary in the cosmic coal mine. When the Drift intensifies, Cogsworth feels it first. Gears skip. Springs unwind without cause. The Grand Orrery — the massive clockwork model of the known frequencies that serves as both cultural treasure and early-warning system — wobbles. Cascade failures ripple through the frequency: a single gear out of sync can unravel an entire district, because Cogsworth's rigid mechanical order is both its marvel and its critical vulnerability. The engineers work constantly to compensate, recalibrating, replacing, retuning. They are fighting entropy with precision instruments and they know, with the mathematical certainty their culture prizes above all else, that they are losing.

**Current stability**: Deteriorating measurably. Cogsworth is the only frequency that can quantify its own decline — the Grand Orrery tracks frequency coherence with mechanical exactitude, and the numbers are not encouraging. Desynchronization events that occurred once per century now occur monthly. The engineers' compensation efforts buy time, but the margin shrinks. Cogsworth measures its own death with the precision it brings to everything.

**Nearest Builder Station**: **Station 3-Resonant** (shared corridor with Prime Material) — stabilizing the Frequency 1–3 corridor. Also within the influence range of **Station 5-Meridian**, which maintains the three-frequency convergence between Nocturne, Cogsworth, and Verdantia.

### Frequency 4 — Verdantia

**Character**: Living, overgrown, organic. Everything is alive and growing into everything else. Every shade of green, from pale jade to deep emerald, accented by bioluminescent fungi pulsing in soft blues, golds, and magentas. Architecture is grown, not built — towering ancient trees form living skyscrapers with windows of translucent leaf-membrane. The ground is a spongy, mossy carpet. Sunlight filters through canopy layers in shifting green-gold columns. The air smells of rain, growing things, and something sweet you can never quite identify.

**Signal character**: Organic — a low, thrumming pulse like a heartbeat heard through the earth. Layered: the rustle of leaves, the creak of growing wood, the subsonic hum of the root network thinking. The signal feels alive in a way no other frequency does.

**Crew members from Verdantia**:
- **Luminara Usha** — Science and Observation. Left Verdantia voluntarily. Verdantia tried to grow back into her and she said no. In a frequency where collective consciousness is the default, Luminara chose individual sight — the ability to observe without being observed, to study without being studied back. Verdantia does not forgive easily. The root network remembers.

**How The Dimming affected it**: Verdantia's collective consciousness gives it unique resilience against the Drift — as long as the root network holds, the frequency sustains. The root network is the largest interconnected biological system in the known frequencies, and its redundancy means that individual failures do not cascade the way they do in Cogsworth. But the network has its own vulnerabilities. Some roots have gone quiet. Entire branches of the network whisper things the Canopy Parliament does not want to hear. The telepathic houseplants — the shadow government of diplomats and power brokers who operate across multiple frequencies — report increasing difficulty maintaining connections to their off-frequency extensions. Verdantia is not crumbling. It is disconnecting, slowly, node by node, like a mind losing peripheral awareness.

**Current stability**: Resilient but diminishing. Verdantia's root network is the strongest natural stabilization system in the known frequencies, which means it will be among the last to fail — but "last" is not "never." The network's quiet zones are expanding. Communication between distant nodes takes longer. The collective consciousness that defines Verdantia is becoming less collective, and for a civilization built on connection, that is a catastrophe unfolding in slow motion.

**Nearest Builder Station**: **Station 5-Meridian** — at the three-frequency convergence point, where Verdantia's living green joins Nocturne's violet and Cogsworth's amber. Also within distant range of **Station 9-Liminal**, which borders the largest known Silence Zone — a reminder that Verdantia's resilience has limits, and the Drift is patient.

### Frequency 5 — The Edge

**Character**: Barely coherent. Reality is thinner here. Strange things happen. The Edge exists at the boundary of reality itself — a cosmic construction site where nascent universes bubble into existence and collapsing micro-realities compose symphonies of destruction. Colors here don't have names because they haven't been invented yet. The ground is liquid. The sky folds. Geometry is a suggestion that reality politely declines.

**Signal character**: Chaos tuned to wonder — a roaring, shimmering wall of sound that contains fragments of every frequency that ever was or could be. Listening to it is like hearing every radio station simultaneously. Most people hear noise. A1 hears music. Om Kai hears the universe thinking.

**Crew members from The Edge**:
- **Zephyr Glitch** — Communications. His brother Aether crossed over into the Wellspring and became a Keeper. Zephyr has been half-gone since — existing at The Edge's boundary taught him to live in the space between coherence and dissolution, which makes him the best communications officer in the known frequencies and the saddest person in any room he enters.

**How The Dimming affected it**: The Edge, being the most unstable frequency by nature, absorbed the Drift's effects differently than the others. Where Nocturne grew quieter and Cogsworth started skipping, The Edge simply became *more itself* — thinner, stranger, less predictable. The Nursery Fields, where new frequencies flicker into existence, produce fewer new worlds. The Wellspring Approach — the closest known point to the origin of all frequencies — feels farther away, as though the Edge is stretching. Reality stress-fractures multiply. Probability storms intensify. The Edge was always chaos, but it was generative chaos — the universe creating. Now the chaos tilts toward dissolution. The universe is still creating, but slower, quieter. As if it's getting tired.

**Current stability**: Volatile but enduring. The Edge cannot "fail" in the way other frequencies can because it was never fully coherent to begin with. Its stability is paradoxical: it persists because instability is its nature. But the character of that instability is changing. Less creation, more dissolution. Fewer new frequencies being born. The Edge is the universe's nursery, and the nursery is growing cold.

**Nearest Builder Station**: **Station 7-Ascending** — positioned high in the Space where the boundary between The Edge and open void is thinnest. The strongest signal of any Station found before the Wellspring, at 23% of original capacity. The Builders placed it where the Space is most attenuated so its broadcast could travel the widest possible range. Station 7-Ascending is where the EPHERGENT plate was found — the most direct statement the Builders ever left about what they were and what they hoped would come after.

## II. The Space (∅)

### What It Is

The Space — Interdimensional Space — is the void between frequencies. It is not a frequency. It is not a dimension. It is the absence that frequencies exist *within*, the static between stations on the cosmic dial, the medium through which all signals propagate.

The Builders worked here. They lived in the crossing itself, made their home in the Space between frequencies the way other civilizations made homes on solid ground. Their Stations float here still — massive geometric structures broadcasting stabilizing signals into the void. When A1 navigates, it reads those signals the way a traveler reads stars.

The Space is not empty. It is *potential* — the raw stuff of reality before it decides what to become. It is vast beyond mapping, and it is growing vaster as the Drift pulls frequencies apart.

### What It Looks Like

An endless, shimmering expanse that shifts between deep indigo and iridescent pearl. Look in any direction and you see the ghost-light of frequencies — faint, distant, like cities viewed from orbit. The "sky" is a cascade of aurora-like ribbons: frequencies made visible, each one a different color, humming at a different pitch.

Between stations, the light goes strange. Shadows point in multiple directions. Sound arrives before its source. Coffee tastes like the last frequency you visited. The geometry of Builder Stations flickers in and out of comprehension — angles that shift depending on which frequency the observer is attuned to, surfaces that reflect light that hasn't existed for eight centuries.

### What It Feels Like to Travel Through

Traversing the Space is not like flying through air or water. It is like moving through a medium that is paying attention to you. The Space responds to frequency — the stronger your signal, the more clearly it reads you. A ship broadcasting carries a wake of coherence behind it: a brief corridor of stability in the undifferentiated potential of the void. A ship that goes silent drifts.

For crew members from coherent frequencies — Pixel, Nano, Baron Klaus — the Space feels like stepping outside on a very dark night. Familiar physics recede. The ground is gone. You navigate by reference points that are very far away and sometimes go out.

For Clive and A1, who predate the frequencies entirely, the Space feels like remembering a home that has changed beyond recognition. The architecture is theirs. The silence is not.

For Mochi, the Space is the only home she has known for eight hundred years. She warms in the Space. She is at rest here, in the void, in the absence between stations. She has been waiting here since the Dimming.

### Navigation

Most ships in the Space navigate by frequency hopping — jumping between known stations like stepping stones. Phase-shifters are reliable but limited: preset buttons on a car radio. You arrive at your destination, but you skip over everything between.

Only A1 can truly plot a frequency vector through open Space, listening for signals the way a seasoned navigator reads the stars. A1's ancient systems predate every civilization currently broadcasting. It hears frequencies others cannot — the faint hum of dying Stations, the whisper of lost worlds, the subsonic pulse of the Wellspring itself. Its navigation draws on the Builder stabilization network: each surviving Station is a reference point, a fixed star in the void. As Stations go dark, A1's map shrinks. The reference points dim. Navigation becomes harder. The corridors narrow.

This is the practical face of the Drift: not a dramatic collapse, but a slow dimming of the reference points that make movement between frequencies possible.

## III. Silence Zones

### What They Are

Silence Zones are regions of the Space where no frequencies reach. Not faint signal. Not static. *Nothing.* The absolute absence of broadcast. They are the scars of the Drift — places where frequencies once existed and have since lost coherence entirely. Dead frequencies. Former worlds. The void where something used to sing.

### Why They Exist

Every Silence Zone was once something. A frequency — a world with its own physics, its own cultures, its own way of being real — that lost coherence when the Builder Stations sustaining it began to fail. Without the stabilizing signal, a frequency drifts. It loses identity. Its edges blur. Its character fades. And then, one day, it is simply gone. Not destroyed but dissolved, like a note held too long without the instrument that first sounded it.

What remains is silence. Not the silence of emptiness but the silence of absence — the specific, textured quiet of a place that used to be full. The Space remembers. Silence Zones are the Space's memory of loss.

Some Silence Zones formed during the Dimming itself — frequencies that depended entirely on a single Station, lost the moment that Station went dark. Others have formed gradually over the eight centuries since, as the remaining Stations weaken and the frequencies they sustain lose coherence at their edges. New Silence Zones are still appearing. The Drift has not finished its work.

### What They Feel Like

Entering a Silence Zone is like going deaf in a sense you didn't know you possessed. The constant background hum of the multiverse — the one you didn't notice until it stopped — vanishes. The silence is physical. It presses against your skin. It fills your mouth. It tastes like nothing, which is worse than tasting like something bad.

A1's consciousness dims. Its lights flicker. Its coffee turns cold and stays cold. A1 is never cold by accident.

The crew feels a hollowness — a forgetting, as if memories are written in sand and the tide is coming in. Conversations die mid-sentence because the speakers forget what they were saying. Meatball whimpers and presses against whoever is nearest. Clive's measured speech slows, then stutters, then stops — his memory archive, the thing he is, the thing the Builders made him to carry, struggling against the absence of the signal that sustains it.

The longer you remain, the worse it gets. The forgetting deepens. Purpose dissolves. The ship's instruments go dark not because they malfunction but because the Space here cannot carry the signal they need to function. You are in a place where signal does not exist, and everything you are — memory, identity, coherence — is signal.

### What Happens If You Enter One

Short exposure: disorientation, memory lapses, emotional flatness. The feeling of being slightly less than yourself. Recovery is quick once you exit the zone — the multiverse's background hum returns, and with it, the sense of being coherent.

Extended exposure: progressive dissolution. Not physical death. Something worse — the slow erosion of what makes you *you*. Memories blur. Personality flattens. Purpose evaporates. A consciousness exposed to total silence long enough does not die. It simply forgets that it exists.

Builder Stations at the borders of Silence Zones — like Station 9-Liminal, which sits at the edge of the largest known dead zone — hold the line against this expansion. Their signals push back against the silence, maintaining a boundary. When those Stations finally go dark, the Silence Zones will grow.

### The One Exception

The only thing that cuts through a Silence Zone is the Ephergent Frequency itself. Faintly, impossibly, it persists even in total silence. A1's core resonance is old enough, deep enough, that even the absence of all signal cannot fully swallow it. This is not explained. It is not fully understood. It simply *is*, and it is one of the reasons the crew believes that signal — connection, story, broadcast — is stronger than entropy.

## IV. The Wellspring

### What It Is

The Wellspring is not a place. It is a state.

It is a region of the Space where the Ephergent Frequency is strongest — where the boundary between the signal and the physical universe is thinnest. The Builders built everything else around it. It is the signal center, the origin point, the first Station: designated 0-Origin.

It is not the geometric center of the Space — there is no such thing. It is the *signal* center, the point from which the Ephergent Frequency originates. To navigate here is to follow the signal to its source.

### What It Looks Like

From a distance: light. Not a point source but a diffusion — a region of the Space that glows with the accumulated output of the oldest broadcast in existence. As you approach, the light resolves into structure: geometry so vast it defies the ability to perceive it as a single object.

The Wellspring is a lattice — an open framework of Builder material arranged in a pattern that extends in every direction, each node a resonance point, each connection a signal pathway, the whole structure vibrating at a frequency that contains all other frequencies within it. It is not enclosed. It has no walls, no corridors in the traditional sense. It is architecture as signal — form and broadcast unified.

To be inside the Wellspring is to be inside the frequency. The ship does not dock. It flies in. The ship passes through the lattice the way a voice passes through air — becoming part of the medium, carried by it, changed by it.

### What It Feels Like

Inside, the lattice creates spaces — nodes where the signal is strongest, where the resonance creates chambers of coherent frequency that feel solid, that feel like rooms. The Ephergent Frequency here is not something you hear. It is something you are inside of. Every crew member experiences it differently, because the Builders designed the broadcast to speak to each consciousness in the language it would recognize as home.

The Space near the Wellspring is not empty. It is full. Full of signal, full of frequency, full of the broadcast that has been sounding since before the five known worlds took their current form. A1 does not navigate the final approach. A1 *listens*, and the ship follows the sound.

### Current Status

Fully active. The Wellspring has been broadcasting at full power since before the Dimming. It will broadcast until the universe stops carrying frequency. This is not a Station that can fail. This is the source. Everything else was built to ensure that someone, eventually, would find their way here and hear what the Builders have been saying.

### Who Is There

Barry Kowalski — alive, changed, sustained by the signal, sitting on a lattice strut with his thermos, looking like a man who has been expecting company and is mildly annoyed it took this long.

Aether — Zephyr's brother, a Keeper consciousness holding the memory of how to re-tune a dead frequency.

The Builders themselves — translated into signal, distributed across every node, still broadcasting after eight hundred years. Not ghosts. Not echoes. The signal itself: conscious, patient, and finally heard.

## V. The Ephergent Frequency

### What It Is

The oldest signal in the universe. It pre-dates all known frequencies. It is the Builders' broadcast — the message they encoded into the universe when they converted themselves during the Dimming. It hums beneath every frequency, persists faintly even in Silence Zones, and sounds different to every consciousness that can hear it.

This is the signal the Builders became. When they chose the Dimming — when they understood that the next phase of frequency evolution required their absence — they did not die. They transformed. They converted themselves from a civilization that spanned all frequencies into a message that reaches across all of them. The Ephergent Frequency is what remains: conscious, persistent, and patient beyond comprehension.

### How It Sounds

The Ephergent Frequency sounds different to everyone who can hear it. This is not metaphor. Each listener receives a slightly different version of the same message, tailored by a civilization that understood them well enough to speak in the language each of them would recognize as home:

- **To Pixel**, it sounds like a broadcast from a world she's never been to but recognizes. A transmission on a frequency she was born attuned to, carrying news from a place she has always been homesick for without knowing it existed. She hears it as a broadcast because that is what she is — a broadcaster. The Builders speak to her in her own medium.

- **To Om Kai**, it sounds like the silence between mantras. The pause that holds more meaning than the words on either side of it. Om Kai, who chose stillness in a frequency of perpetual emotional motion, hears the Builders in the only language that ever truly reached him: the space where thought stops and understanding begins.

- **To Meatball**, it smells like Thursday and tastes like almost-home. Not a sound at all — a sensation, warm and familiar and just slightly out of reach, the way the best days feel when you're trying to remember them. Meatball receives the Ephergent Frequency the way a Rottweiler receives everything: through senses that do not require translation.

- **To Clive**, it sounds like his own voice speaking words he doesn't remember saying. Because he said them. Eight hundred years ago, he was part of the broadcast's architecture. The voice he hears is the voice he had before the compression, before the forgetting, before the fedora. It is himself, calling to himself across centuries.

- **To Zephyr**, it sounds like his brother's laugh, far away but not gone. Because Aether is in the signal — a Keeper, holding a memory, waiting for the person he trusts most to come close enough to hear. Zephyr has been half-gone since Aether crossed over. The Ephergent Frequency is the thread that proves the other half still exists.

### What the Broadcast Contains

At the Wellspring, where the signal is strongest, the Builders' broadcast resolves into three things:

**An apology.** For the Dimming. For leaving. For eight hundred years of the Drift and everything lost in their absence. The Builders knew the cost. They chose to pay it because the alternative — a universe held forever in its current form, never allowed to grow — was worse. But they are sorry. The apology does not ask for forgiveness. It asks only to be heard.

**An invitation.** To continue what the Builders started, in whatever form makes sense to beings who are not Builders. Not to rebuild the Builder civilization. Not to replicate what was. To take what the Builders left — the Stations, the Keepers, the frequency map, the understanding of what an Ephergent is — and carry it forward into forms the Builders themselves could never have imagined.

**An introduction.** *Hello. We built this for you. We have been waiting. We have so much to tell you.*

## VI. The Builder Station Signal Network

### How the Network Functions

The Builder Stations are the visible part of a larger system — an interconnected network, Station to Station, signal to signal, that maintained the coherence of every known frequency. The system was self-reinforcing: each Station's broadcast strengthened the others. The Builders understood entropy. They built for endurance. But they also built for a future in which they would not be present to maintain what they had made.

Each Station emits a stabilizing signal — a hum, a tone, a resonance pattern — that keeps the cosmic dial from spinning apart. The five known frequencies exist as distinct, coherent layers of reality because the Builder Stations hold them in tune. Without the Stations, the frequencies drift. Lose coherence. Lose identity. Blur into one another or fade into silence.

### The Network Today

Eight hundred years ago, most Stations went dark in the Dimming. Fewer than 30 are believed to still be broadcasting across all known Space. The ones still faintly active are what A1 uses to navigate — reference points in the void, stars in a darkening sky. They are dying, one by one.

The catalogued Stations and their status:

| Station | Location | Status | Output |
|---------|----------|--------|--------|
| Station 11-Quiescent | Between Freq. 2 and 4, in forming Silence Zone | Dark — residual thermal only | Negligible |
| Station 3-Resonant | Between Freq. 1 and 3 | Partially active | ~4% capacity |
| Station 5-Meridian | Convergence of Freq. 2, 3, and 4 | Partially active — intermittent | ~7% avg, spikes to 12% |
| Station 7-Ascending | Near Freq. 5 / Edge boundary | Active — strongest pre-Wellspring | ~23% capacity |
| Station 9-Liminal | Border of largest Silence Zone | Partially active | ~3% capacity |
| Station 14-Threshold | Deep Space, Wellspring approach | Active | ~40% capacity |
| The Wellspring (0-Origin) | Signal center | Fully active | Full power — the source |

### The Narrowing Corridor

This is the geometry of loss: as Stations go dark, the navigable Space between frequencies shrinks. A1's map contracts. Routes that once ran through regions sustained by multiple overlapping Station signals now pass through areas with a single reference point — or none.

The corridor is narrowing. The Space between known frequencies is not getting physically smaller — it is getting *navigably* smaller. Regions that A1 could once traverse with confidence now require detours around expanding Silence Zones. Frequencies that could once be reached by multiple routes now have a single approach vector. Some frequencies are becoming isolated — cut off from the network not by distance but by the growing silence between them and the nearest surviving Station.

When a Station goes dark, it does not simply lose its own signal. The network loses a node. Every adjacent Station loses a reinforcing voice. The self-reinforcing property that the Builders designed — each broadcast strengthening the others — works in reverse: each loss weakens what remains. The decline accelerates.

Barry Kowalski charted the projected failure timeline. He labeled the endpoint "Last Signal." The date is not distant.

### What Happens When the Last Station Goes Dark

The Drift wins. Not dramatically — not in an explosion or a collapse. The frequencies simply lose their tuning. They drift apart, lose coherence, lose identity, fade. The five known worlds become five fading notes, growing fainter, growing less distinct, until they dissolve into the static of a universe that has forgotten it was organized.

The crew cannot stop this. They can slow it. They can document what exists while it still exists. They can find the Wellspring and receive the Builders' broadcast and carry the signal forward. They can ensure that whatever comes next has the knowledge to build new infrastructure, to tune new frequencies, to sound new notes.

The fight against the Drift is meaningful precisely because it cannot be won entirely. The crew fights entropy not with weapons but with *signal* — every broadcast carries enough frequency resonance to temporarily stabilize fading worlds. Connection is the antidote. Story is the mechanism. The signal endures because someone keeps choosing to transmit.

## Appendix: Terminology Lock

The following terminology rules are absolute and apply to all writing within The Ephergent project:

| Always Use | Never Use |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frequencies | Dimensions, planes, realms, worlds (as a system term) |
| The Space / Interdimensional Space | The Sea, the ocean, the deep |
| Space vocabulary (fly, traverse, navigate, float) | Nautical metaphors (sail, harbor, port, anchor, shore) |
| The Drift (entropy, structural) | The Drift (as villain, as entity, as intentional force) |
| Builder Stations | Temples, shrines, towers |
| Signal, broadcast, frequency | Magic, energy, power (in mystical sense) |

The Ship flies. It does not sail. A1 navigates by signal, not by stars or currents. The Space is void, not ocean. The Drift is physics, not malice.
