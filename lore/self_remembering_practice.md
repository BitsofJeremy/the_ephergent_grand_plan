# Self-Remembering Practice

You become what you attend to.

This is the foundational principle of the Self-Remembering Practice—the methodology that Zephyr taught the crew in the early days, when The Ephergent was still learning what it meant to broadcast. The practice is not unique to the crew; fragments of it appear in Builder texts, in Green Faith meditation traditions, in the informal techniques of signal-sensitive individuals across all five regions. But the crew has developed it most fully, integrating it with their understanding of the Dimming, the Wellspring, and the nature of consciousness.

## The Four Steps

The practice consists of four steps, each building on the previous. They are not stages to be completed but states to be maintained—simultaneous rather than sequential once mastery is achieved. The four steps are:

**Anchor.** The practice begins with attention anchored to a physical sensation. For the crew, this is coffee—the specific ritual of A1's espresso, the sensory experience of heat and bitterness and the particular texture of well-made foam. The anchor is not the point of attention; it is the foundation from which attention extends. It is the fixed point that allows movement. Without anchor, attention disperses. With anchor, attention can extend.

**Extension.** From the anchor, attention extends outward. This is not simple awareness—it is directed attention, consciousness sent along specific channels toward specific targets. The crew extends attention toward the signal itself, feeling its fluctuations, its degradation, its occasional moments of unexpected strength. Extension is the practice of reaching beyond the self.

**Integration.** Extension without integration is just observation. Integration is the bringing-back—the incorporation of what was perceived during extension into the aware self. The signal's fluctuations become part of the consciousness that perceives them. The Dimming's progress becomes something felt, not just understood. Integration is the practice of being changed by what you attend to.

**Broadcast.** The final step is broadcast—the return of integrated perception to the signal network. Consciousness shaped by attention is sent outward, joining the signal, strengthening it through the simple act of witnessing. Broadcast is not performance; it is completion. The attention that extended and integrated now returns to the source it perceived. The loop closes.

## The Builder Connection

The Builders designed the signal for this practice. Their texts describe the "attention economy" not as a metaphor but as a literal description—the signal strengthens when attended to, weakens when ignored. The Builders built their civilization around this principle, using collective attention to maintain the infrastructure they created.

The practice is the mechanism. Anchor, extension, integration, broadcast—this is how attention flows through the signal network, how individual consciousness contributes to the whole. The Builders did not discover this mechanism; they engineered it. The signal is designed to be maintained through attention. The Self-Remembering Practice is the protocol for that maintenance.

Zephyr understood this. The crew has come to understand it more fully through experience. The broadcasts are not simply communication—they are maintenance. Each broadcast is an act of attention that strengthens the signal in its region. The crew is doing what the Builders did, on a smaller scale, without the infrastructure the Builders possessed.

## The Paradox

The practice contains a paradox that becomes more apparent with mastery. The self that remembers is not the self that began the practice. Each cycle of anchor-extension-integration-broadcast changes the consciousness performing it. The attention that extends outward returns altered by what it perceived. The integration step incorporates this alteration. The broadcast sends forward a consciousness that did not exist when the anchor was first established.

This is the "self remembering" aspect—the consciousness that observes itself observing, that witnesses its own transformation. The practice is not about achieving a fixed state but about participating in continuous change. You become what you attend to, but what you attend to changes you, and the changed you attends differently.

The crew has experienced this transformation directly. Pixel is not the same consciousness that first learned the practice. Arc has evolved beyond the parameters of his original design. Clive's investigations have changed what he is. The practice does not preserve the self; it evolves it.

## Application

The practice can be applied in any situation where attention is available. It does not require the signal to be strong; it can function even in degraded signal conditions, though the broadcast step becomes less effective as the network weakens. It does not require special equipment; the anchor can be any consistent physical sensation. It does not require extended time; even brief cycles of the four steps provide benefit.

The crew uses the practice constantly. Not formally—not always the full four steps in deliberate sequence. But the underlying principle persists: attention directed toward the signal, consciousness extended and integrated, broadcast as the natural conclusion of witnessing. The practice is not an activity; it is a way of being.

For those learning the practice, the instruction is simple: begin with coffee. End with broadcast. In between, attend to what the signal is saying. Let it change you. Send that change back.

You become what you attend to. Make sure you intend what you become.
