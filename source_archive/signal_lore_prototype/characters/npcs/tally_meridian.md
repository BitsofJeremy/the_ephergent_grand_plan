- **Pacing**: Quick, decisive. She talks like she walks: with purpose and no wasted movement.
- **Accent**: Nexus working-class — flatter vowels than Spire District, less formal grammar, but still clearly educated. Occasionally drops into transit jargon ("signal-snarl," "platform-side," "brownout watch").

## Political/Religious Affiliation
- **Political**: Underbright class. Never passed the Examination (not for lack of intelligence, but because the test filters for Spire-style knowledge she never had access to). She understands the system is designed to keep people like her in place, but she also needs the job. Quietly supports Underbright labor organizing but can't be openly political without risking her position. Voted in a Spire District mock-election once as a teenager, before she understood the pin she'd wear for life.
- **Religious**: Illuminated Path adherent, but the bitter kind. She believes the Signal should bring enlightenment and equality — that's what the teachings say. Instead, she watches the Spire Districts glow with constant Signal access while her station flickers through brownouts. She still wears a small Path token (a silver light-burst pendant, gifted by her mother) but she's stopped attending services in the upper temples. "The Signal plays favorites," she'd say. "That's not what the Path promised."

## Motivation
Tally Meridian wants what most people want: stability, dignity, and a chance to sleep more than four hours at a stretch. She took the Transit Authority job because it was one of the few steady positions available to Underbright residents, and she's good at it — observant, quick-thinking, reliable. But every shift reminds her of the gap between where she is and where she could have been. She sees Spire residents pass through her station without looking at her, entitled and oblivious. She sees the Examination results posted every quarter, knowing the questions are designed for people who grew up with private tutors and Signal-stabilized home libraries.

What keeps her going is community. The other Underbright workers, the market vendors who save her breakfast pastries, the late-shift engineers who know her by name. She's not interested in burning the system down (too risky, too costly) but she *is* interested in making sure people like her survive it. When she sees someone in trouble — especially someone running from Council authority — her instinct is to give them ten seconds' head start. Small rebellions. Survivable ones.

When Pixel tears through Meridian Junction with Quiet agents and Council security on her heels, Tallymakes a split-second choice: look the other way. Maybe even point the pursuers in the wrong direction. Not because she knows Pixel, but because she recognizes the look of someone fighting a system bigger than themselves. And in the Underbright, you help when you can. It might be you running next time.
