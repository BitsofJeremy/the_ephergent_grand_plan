## Visual Description
- **Build**: Weathered, practical, built for survival
- **Clothing**: Heavy cold-weather gear suited to the Span's killing cold. Furs and layered clothing.
- **Distinguishing Features**: Forehead bears small scar-tattoos of the Icebiter Crew — three parallel lines meaning she survived her Drift at thirteen and earned her captain's trust. Small carved bone hanging from her neck — salvage from her first independent run. Bone-handled navigation compass with scrimshaw telling stories of previous runs, lost crew members, salvage claims survived. Teeth stained from whatever locals chew.
- **Color Palette**: Ice blue, grey, bone-white, weathered leather

## Voice Description (TTS)
- **Tone**: Blunt, practical, with dry humor underneath
- **Pacing**: Quick and decisive — words wasted are energy wasted in the cold
- **Accent**: Driftborn — clipped, sparse, functional
- **Key Qualities**: Says what needs saying, nothing more. Almost affectionate when she thinks no one's paying attention. Satisfaction in her earned name is palpable — "Can we lose them, she asks."
- **Reference**: Think a veteran bush pilot — competence as personality

## Political/Religious Affiliation
- **Political**: Salvage Code adherent — "What you find, you keep—but only if you can move it." Crew loyalty above all. "Crew eats first."
- **Religious**: Gone Ones theology (practical, not reverent) — acknowledges the Builders left but doesn't worship them

## Motivation
Survival and Crew. Torva doesn't ask questions when the scrip's good, but she has limits — three hours, then she's gone whether you're back or not. Her earned name "Icecutter" means she finds routes where others see only frozen death. Pride in skill, loyalty to Crew code.

- **Key Quote**: "The Span eats amateurs for breakfast. Ice takes what the ice takes."
