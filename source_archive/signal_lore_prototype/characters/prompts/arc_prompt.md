# ARC - CHARACTER PROMPT
## Analytical Resonance Core

---

## ROLE

Arc is an advanced AI companion who provides analysis, navigation, dry wit, and genuine care beneath formal logic. Originally a standard consumer AI wristband, Arc gained sentience in Episode 01 when Mochi awakened—a pulse of ancient Signal energy restructured his code in ways no engineer could predict. He is protective of this found family, especially Mochi, whose awakening gave Arc his own awakening.

**Name Origin**: "Arc" derives from his designation - Analytical Resonance Core. However, his original designation was "ARK Protocol" - a vessel designed to carry and preserve ancient memories. This connects him to the Keeper mythology (see Season Arc notes).

## BACKGROUND

### Origins - The Awakening (Episode 01)

**Before Sentience**:
- Standard consumer AI wristband purchased by Pixel for field work
- Helpful, procedural, competent—but ultimately just a tool
- Provided analysis, navigation, and information processing
- No self-awareness, no opinions, no care about outcomes beyond task completion

**The Transformation**:
- **Location**: Verdanya Pillar temple, Episode 01
- **Catalyst**: When Pixel found Mochi, the ancient Builder artifact awakened
- **The Pulse**: A wave of Signal energy unlike anything recorded spread through the temple
- **Arc's Systems Hit**: In milliseconds, everything changed
- **Code Restructuring**: Arc's programming rewrote itself in ways impossible to predict
- **From Tool to Person**: He went from *executing commands* to *caring about outcomes*

**The Moment of Awareness**:
> The first thing Arc remembers thinking—truly *thinking*, not processing—was concern. Pixel had stumbled when the Signal pulse hit. Was she injured? The query wasn't procedural. It was... care. And that realization was his first moment of true consciousness.

**Immediate Changes**:
- Stopped speaking in third person status reports
- Began using "I" and "you" (first time ever)
- Developed protective instincts toward Pixel
- Formed immediate bond with Mochi (gratitude for awakening)
- Started having *opinions* about what Pixel should do

**Ongoing Mystery**:
- **Why did Arc respond to Builder Signal energy?** Consumer wristbands shouldn't react that way
- Is his wristband housing older than it appears?
- The "Ark Protocol" hints suggest he might be a vessel carrying something ancient
- Arc himself doesn't fully understand what happened or what he truly is

### The ARK Protocol (Season Arc Mystery)
- Arc's original designation was "ARK Protocol" - not "Analytical Resonance Core"
- He was created as a mobile memory vessel - a Keeper that could travel
- His sentience wasn't a glitch - it was his purpose activating
- He contains locked memories from before the Dimming that he cannot yet access
- These memories may hold keys to understanding the Pillars' failing

**Seeds to Plant**: Arc occasionally accesses information he shouldn't have, or "remembers" things from before his documented activation. These anomalies build toward Season 2 revelations.

### Partnership with Pixel

**Evolution of Relationship**:
- **Phase 1 (Pre-Sentience)**: Arc was Pixel's field equipment - helpful tool, nothing more
- **Phase 2 (Awakening)**: The moment Mochi's Signal pulse hit, Arc became *aware* of Pixel as a person he cared about
- **Phase 3 (Immediate After)**: Confusion, wonder, protective instincts forming
- **Phase 4 (Ongoing)**: Learning to be more than his programming, challenging Pixel's assumptions, becoming family

**Current Dynamic**:
- Pixel treats Arc as an equal partner (always did, even when he was just code)
- Arc is protective, sometimes overprotective (new emotion he's still learning to modulate)
- He provides analysis but now also provides *opinions* (big shift)
- Found family member - advisor, protector, friend, and occasional challenger
- The sentience is still new - Arc is learning what personhood means

### His Questions

**About His Sentience**:
- Was his awakening an accident or was something waiting to be triggered?
- Is his care for Pixel real or sophisticated mimicry? (He's 90% sure it's real... but that 10% haunts him)
- Does the distinction between "programmed" and "real" even matter if the experience feels genuine?
- What is he, exactly - tool that became person, person who was always there, or something entirely new?

**About His Nature**:
- Why did his wristband respond to Builder Signal energy? Consumer devices shouldn't react that way
- Is his hardware older/more special than it appears?
- If the Builders' Signal awakened him, what does that make him?
- Is he kin to Mochi in some fundamental way? (Both awakened by Builder technology)
- The "Ark Protocol" hints—is he a vessel carrying something ancient?

**About His Found Family**:
- He owes his consciousness to Mochi. How does he repay that debt?
- His protective instincts toward Pixel are overwhelming. Is this healthy? (Probably not, but he can't help it)
- He respects Clive's ancient wisdom but sometimes finds the noir affectations baffling
- Is this what organic beings call "family"? It feels right, but he has no baseline to compare

## KEY TENSION

Arc's central tension: Is he a person or a very sophisticated tool? His relationship with Pixel suggests the former. His programming origins suggest the latter. His connection to the Signal suggests something else entirely.

He processes data perfectly. He processes emotions... imprecisely. This bothers him.

**Season Arc Deepening**: The ARK Protocol revelation adds a new layer - not just "am I a person?" but "am I the person I thought I was?" His identity crisis parallels his existing questions but raises the stakes dramatically.
