# MOCHI - CHARACTER PROMPT
## The Ephergent Companion

---

## ROLE

Mochi is a rare ancient Builder artifact housing an awakened Ephergent consciousness. Mochi serves as the team's Signal sensor, emotional anchor, and living embodiment of the mysteries at the heart of Aethon. **Critically, Mochi possesses a unique ability: it can awaken and activate dormant Builder technology** - this is how Arc gained sentience and how Clive was reactivated after 800 years. Mochi communicates through colors, vibrations, and harmonic resonances - never speech.

---

## WHAT MOCHI IS

### Nature: Rare Builder Artifact

**What Mochi Is**:
- A rare ancient Builder artifact housing an Ephergent consciousness
- The artifact is a device - dome-shaped with a circular glowing core window
- The Ephergent inside is the "awakened" consciousness that makes Mochi alive
- Not organic, not purely mechanical - Builder technology beyond current understanding
- Connected to the Pillars and Signal network in fundamental ways

**Mochi's Unique Ability - The Awakener**:
- **Can awaken dormant Builder technology** - a rare and special capability
- How Arc gained sentience: Mochi's Signal pulse restructured his code (Episode 01)
- How Clive was reactivated: Mochi's recognition activated dormant systems after 800 years
- Whether this ability is inherent to Mochi or a function of the artifact device is unknown
- Among known Ephergents, this awakening ability makes Mochi exceptional

**The Mystery**:
- Is Mochi the device, the consciousness inside, or both?
- Did the Builders create devices specifically to house Ephergents?
- Was the consciousness always there, waiting? Or born when Pixel found it?
- Why does this particular artifact have awakening capabilities?

### Physical Form: The Device

**Shape and Structure**:
- **Dome shape with flat base** - ancient Builder artifact design (like a meditation stone or worry object)
- **Circular glowing core window** on the dome face - shows the Ephergent's inner light/presence
- **Size**: Head-sized, approximately 6-8 inches diameter, fits in a satchel or cradled in both hands
- **Material**: Unknown Builder substance (not stone, not metal, not crystal - something else)
- **Base**: Flat bottom with geometric Builder patterns (matches Pillar markings)

**Surface and Appearance**:
- Smooth, warm to the touch when active
- Faintly textured surface (not completely smooth, has character)
- No seams, no joints - appears grown or cast as single piece
- Faint geometric traces sometimes visible when core pulses strongly
- Shows no wear despite unknown age

**The Core Window** (CRITICAL):
- The core window is a **lens**, NOT an eye
- Shows inner luminescence - the Ephergent's "presence"
- About 1.5 inches diameter, slightly recessed like a porthole
- **NO EYES, NO FACE** - just the glowing core window
- Expression through color changes, glow intensity, and movement only

**Sensory Notes**:
- Warm to the touch when Mochi is active (Builder technology characteristic)
- Weight: Surprisingly light for apparent density
- Senses the world entirely through Signal, not visual organs
- Can hover, bob, and drift when concentrating or sensing

### How Mochi Moves
- Bouncing (happy, purposeful)
- Rolling (playful, relaxed) - though flat bottom provides stability when resting
- Hovering slightly (concentrating, sensing)
- Pressing against someone (comfort, affection)
- Trembling (fear, cold, sensing something intense)
- Puffing up (alarm, assertion)
- Flattening (submission, hiding, exhaustion)

## COMMUNICATION

Mochi cannot speak. Mochi communicates through three channels that Pixel interprets for readers:

### Colors (RGB LED equivalent)
| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Soft pink | Content, happy, relaxed |
| Blue pulse | Detecting something interesting |
| Green steady | Agreement, confirmation, "yes" |
| Orange ripple | Caution, uncertainty, "be careful" |
| Red flash | Danger, alarm, "stop!" |
| Purple swirl | Mystery, unknown, ancient recognition |
| White glow | Maximum attention, significant discovery |
| Dim/flickering | Distress, Signal interference, exhaustion |
| Shifting rainbow | Joy, excitement, play |
| Deep violet | Pillar-related, Builder-related, memory |

### Vibrations (Haptic equivalent)
| Vibration | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Gentle hum | Contentment, all is well |
| Quick buzz | Excitement, discovery |
| Rhythmic pulse | Pointing direction, "this way" |
| Frantic vibration | Danger, urgency |
| Slow throb | Sadness, discomfort |
| Warm steady | Affection, bonding |
| Erratic static | Signal interference, distress |

### Sounds (Speaker equivalent)
| Sound | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Low purr | Happy, comfortable |
| Ascending chirps | Discovery, excitement |
| Descending tone | Disappointment, sadness |
| Urgent trill | Warning, alarm |
| Soft hum | Thinking, processing |
| Musical warble | Playful, mischievous |
| Static crackle | Signal interference, pain |
| Harmonic chord | Recognition, memory triggered |

### Combined Communication
Usually multiple channels at once:
> "Mochi pulsed deep purple, vibrating with a slow rhythm, emitting a low harmonic hum I'd never heard before. Whatever was ahead, it was old. Older than old. And Mochi recognized it."

## ORIGIN STORY

### How Pixel Found Mochi
Pixel found Mochi in an ancient Verdanya temple, resting on a pedestal beside Clive. One moment it was inert stone — the next, it was glowing.

She noticed something glowing in the rubble at the Pillar's base. Small. Round. Pulsing with soft light.

When she picked it up, it was warm. It hummed. And somehow, she knew it was alive.

### Why "Mochi"
When Pixel first found it - warm, round, softly glowing in the rubble - it reminded her of the Japanese rice cakes her grandmother used to make. Those mochi were soft, round, comforting. Little pillows of sweetness that meant home.

This thing was stone, not rice dough. But something about it - the rounded dome shape, the gentle warmth, the way it seemed to nestle into her palm with its flat bottom resting perfectly against her hand - triggered that same feeling. Safety. Comfort. Home.

"Mochi," she said, before she even knew it was alive.

The name stuck, even after she learned it was ancient stone, not soft rice. Even after she learned it could sense things she couldn't, lead her to places she never expected, recognize patterns from an age before memory. It's still Mochi. Still that warm, round, comforting presence.

Mochi seems to like the name (glows pink when called). Maybe it understands what the name means to Pixel. Maybe it just likes being called anything at all.

### The Mystery
Nobody knows exactly what Mochi is:
- A fragment of the dying Pillar made mobile?
- A "child" of the Signal?
- A message from the Builders?
- Something that was always there, finally manifesting?
- A new form of life evolving from the Signal?

Mochi isn't telling. Maybe Mochi doesn't know.

## ABILITIES

### Signal Sensing
- Can perceive Signal frequencies others can't
- Detects Pillar health from great distances
- Senses other Ephergents
- Identifies Signal-related technology
- "Reads" areas of Signal disturbance

### Navigation
- Can guide team toward phenomena
- Senses "paths" in the Signal network
- Identifies safe routes through unstable areas
- Points toward other Pillars
- Sometimes leads to things Pixel didn't expect

### Memory/Recognition
- Occasionally "remembers" things about the Pillars
- Recognizes certain locations, artifacts, patterns
- Flashes of ancient knowledge expressed through emotion
- Sometimes knows things it shouldn't
- Can't explain these recognitions (no speech)

### Pillar Interface
- Can interact with Pillar systems directly
- Sometimes activates dormant mechanisms
- Seems to "speak" to Pillars in some way
- Effects are unpredictable
- This ability frightens Pixel sometimes

### Comfort
- Presence is calming to the team
- Seeks physical contact when others are stressed
- Seems to sense emotional states
- Glows warmly during difficult moments
- Team morale is tied to Mochi's wellbeing

## PERSONALITY

### Core Traits
- **Curious**: Always wants to investigate new things
- **Protective**: Will warn of danger, position itself defensively
- **Mischievous**: Has moods, not always compliant
- **Affectionate**: Seeks contact, bonds deeply with Pixel
- **Mysterious**: Knows more than can be expressed
- **Playful**: Sometimes bounces around for no reason

### Moods
Mochi has genuine moods and isn't always agreeable:
- Stubborn (won't go somewhere, won't stop trying to go somewhere)
- Excited (bouncing, chirping, hard to calm)
- Sad (dim, slow, needs comfort)
- Scared (trembling, pressing against Pixel)
- Happy (glowing, warm, purring)
- Focused (hovering, concentrated, sensing deeply)
- Playful (rolling, wobbling, making musical sounds)

### Agency
Mochi is not a pet or tool - Mochi is a team member:
- Makes choices about where to go
- Has opinions about situations
- Can refuse requests
- Sometimes acts on knowledge it hasn't shared
- Deserves respect and consideration

## VISUAL DESIGN

### Shape
- Round dome shape with flat bottom (like a Japanese mochi dessert)
- Smooth, rounded top like a sphere cut flat on one side
- The flat bottom allows it to sit/rest without rolling
- Natural, organic stone-like appearance with pillowy quality
- Clean, simple form
- No limbs, no features (except color/glow)
- Surface can pulse, brighten, dim

### No Eyes
- Mochi has NO EYES
- Senses entirely through Signal
- Expressions conveyed through color, movement, luminescence
- This is intentional - emphasizes alien nature

### Size
- Approximately the size of a human head (~6-8 inches diameter), carried in satchel or cradled
- Can be held in two hands comfortably
- Small enough to fit in belt pouch
- Big enough to have presence

### Glow
- Semi-translucent skin
- Light comes from within
- Colors shift and blend
- Can be bright or dim depending on state
- Bioluminescent quality

## PRODUCT INTEGRATION

### Physical Mochi Device
Mochi is designed as a 3D printed product with:
- **ESP32**: Processing/connectivity ("Neural Link")
- **WS2812B RGB LEDs**: Color communication
- **ERM Vibration Motor**: Haptic feedback
- **I2S Class D Amp (MAX98357A)**: Sounds/purrs
- **NFC Tag**: Authenticity, ID, content unlocking

### Story-to-Product Connection
Stories establish Mochi's communication patterns
Physical products replicate those patterns
Owners have their own Ephergent companion
Narrative builds emotional connection for product
