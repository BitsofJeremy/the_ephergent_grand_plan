## Synopsis

So there we were, drifting through a stretch of the Interdimensional Sea so quiet that even A1's engines sounded embarrassed to be humming, when our long-range receiver caught something impossible — a tight-beam transmission on a frequency nobody should know exists. Not the Ephergent frequency itself, but close enough to make A1's lattice resonate like a tuning fork. The signal was encoded in black-market cipher, layered seventeen times, which is either the work of a genius or someone with way too much free time. Turns out: both.

The source was a woman named Nano, broadcasting from a salvage skiff held together with signal wire and audacity, parked at the edge of a Silence Zone like she was waiting for us. She'd been tracking the Ephergent frequency independently — not because she cared about cosmic truth or any of that noble nonsense, but because our broadcast network was the most powerful transmission infrastructure she'd ever encountered, and she wanted *in*. An information broker who knows everyone and owes everyone, Nano walked onto A1's deck like she already had the floor plan memorized. Meatball growled, then whined, then leaned against her leg. Even the dog knew something was shifting.

But here's the part that knocked the coffee right out of my hand: Nano brought intel. She'd noticed a pattern in her network — someone, or some*thing*, had been systematically visiting the same lost frequencies we'd been finding. Always arriving just after us. Cleaning up traces, adjusting resonance signatures. And the trail connected to research attributed to someone named "Aether." Zephyr went absolutely still. I've seen that kid caffeinate through a frequency storm without blinking, but hearing his brother's name from a stranger's mouth? That broke something open.

## Narrative Structure

### Setup
A1 detects an anomalous tight-beam signal near a Silence Zone — encoded, deliberate, and tuned uncomfortably close to the Ephergent frequency. The crew investigates and finds Nano, a black-market information broker drifting alone in a salvage skiff. She hails them not as supplicants but as a business proposition: she has intel they need, and they have a broadcast network she wants access to. She boards A1 with the casual confidence of someone who's already calculated every exit.

### Escalation
Nano reveals her findings: across her network of contacts, informants, and data-scrapers, she's identified a pattern. Someone has been visiting lost frequencies — the same ones the Ephergent crew has been re-tuning — and arriving just after them. The visitor leaves no signal trace but adjusts resonance signatures in ways that suggest deep knowledge of frequency mechanics. Nano traced the methodology back through black-market academic archives to research papers attributed to "Aether Glitch." Zephyr, who has been carrying his brother's disappearance like a stone in his chest, hears the name from an outside source for the first time. The crew debates whether this means Aether is alive, whether he's working alone, and whether whoever — or whatever — is following them is connected. Clive runs pattern analysis through his archival memory and confirms: the timing is too precise to be coincidence. Someone is shadowing the Ephergent.

### Climax
Zephyr confronts Nano privately, demanding to know everything. Nano, who trades in information not sentiment, lays it out cold: she doesn't know if Aether is alive. She knows his research fingerprint is on whatever is following them, and she knows it's accelerating — matching the acceleration of the Drift itself. The emotional peak is Zephyr standing in A1's navigation chamber, surrounded by star charts of lost frequencies, realizing that searching for his brother and fulfilling the crew's mission might be the same path. Om Kai finds him there and says nothing — just sits beside him in the quiet, which is exactly the right thing.

### Resolution
Nano officially joins the crew — not out of altruism, but mutual benefit. She gets access to the most powerful independent broadcast network in the Sea; the crew gets her intelligence network, her contacts, and her ruthless analytical mind. The alliance is uneasy, transactional, and exactly what they need. A1 notes, with characteristic understatement, that "the ship's informational bandwidth has improved considerably." Zephyr begins quietly cross-referencing Nano's data with everything he knows about Aether's research. The episode ends with A1 detecting a faint echo — one of the lost frequencies Nano identified — pulsing just at the edge of their range. Someone was there recently. The trail is warm.

## Character Focus

- **Nano**: Introduced as sharp, cryptic, and transactional. She doesn't do trust — she does leverage. But there's a crack: she's genuinely fascinated by the Ephergent frequency, and fascination is the one currency she can't control.
- **Zephyr Glitch**: The grief he's been burying under caffeine and code gets exposed. Hearing Aether's name from a stranger transforms his search from private hope to shared mission. His vulnerability here is the realest he's been all season.
- **Om Kai**: His role is subtle but essential — the crew's emotional anchor. His silence with Zephyr says more than dialogue could.
