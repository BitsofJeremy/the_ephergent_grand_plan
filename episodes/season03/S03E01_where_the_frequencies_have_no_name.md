## I. THE EDGE OF THE MAP

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. DAWN CYCLE. The viewport shows a sky that has forgotten what stars look like. The navigation console displays coordinates that flicker between certainty and question marks. Pixel sits in the pilot's chair with her feet up, pretending she is not worried.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, status.

**A1:**
All systems nominal. Hull integrity: 97%. Fuel reserves: sufficient. Navigation: uncertain.

**PIXEL:**
Uncertain is not a number.

**A1:**
It is today. The chart ends in four hours, seventeen minutes. After that, we are flying blind.

*[Pixel lets that number settle. Four hours seventeen minutes. She had heard A1 give estimates down to the second before, and those estimates always had the quiet confidence of something that had already calculated seventeen different outcomes and picked the most likely. This one came out hollow. This one came out like A1 was reading from a page that did not exist yet.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(sitting up, abandoning the casual pose)*
Blind? You do not do blind. You do instinct. You do harmonic inference. You do whatever it is you do that terrifies me every time it works.

**A1:**
That requires a signal to follow. The signal is here. The map is not. Both facts are equally true.

**PIXEL:**
I hate when you are philosophically accurate.

*[She pulls up the navigation display, watches the numbers flicker. The chart has always been her security blanket — columns of data, frequency designations, the comfort of a universe that can be measured and named. Right now the display is mocking her. Question marks where certainty should be. The universe's way of saying: not yet.]*

*[Clive enters the cockpit. His glow is steady — a warm amber that Pixel has learned to read as contentment, the color of someone who has made peace with their place in things. He carries a small notebook, the kind people used before everything was digital. Barry's notebook, retrieved from DRM wreckage in Season 1. Clive's hands move over the cover like they are apologizing for something.]*

**CLIVE:**
Four hours seventeen minutes. I heard.

**PIXEL:**
Did you hear the part where I said I was not worried?

**CLIVE:**
I heard you say it. I heard it the way people say things they do not believe.

**PIXEL:**
I do not not believe it. I just— I do not know what to do with it.

*[She watches Clive settle into his position near the secondary console. In the months since he joined the crew, she has learned to read his silences the way A1 reads gravitational ripples — Clive hears frequencies in speech patterns that the rest of them miss. He has heard the shape of her lie and he is not going to pretend he did not.]*

**CLIVE:**
The distinction matters. Not-believing is active. Not-knowing is just— honest.

**PIXEL:**
Thanks, Clive. Very comforting.

**CLIVE:**
I was not going for comfort. I was going for accurate.

*[A1 cuts in before Pixel can respond, his voice shifting into that particular register she associates with something new entering his awareness. She sits up straighter.]*

**A1:**
I am reading something ahead. Multiple somethings. The spectrograph is not behaving as expected.

**PIXEL:**
Define "not behaving."

**A1:**
Define "expected."

**PIXEL:**
Fair.

*[Pixel pulls up the spectrograph. The display is unlike anything she has seen in her months of flying — not chaos, not order, but something in between. Peaks and valleys that shift when she is not looking directly at them. When she looks, they arrange themselves into patterns. When she blinks, they dissolve. Like they are shy. Like they do not want to be watched.]*

**PIXEL:**
Those are not random.

**A1:**
No. They are not random. They are not structured. They are something I do not have a word for.

**CLIVE:**
*(quietly, without looking up from the notebook)*
I do.

**PIXEL:**
Clive—

**CLIVE:**
Unnamed. They are unnamed. I have been here before. Or somewhere shaped like here. My hands remember this. My hands remember a place that did not have words for what it was.

*[Pixel turns to look at him. Clive is staring at the spectrograph with an expression she has not seen before — not recognition exactly, but the shape of recognition. Like looking at a word in a language he used to speak. Like waking up in a room you have not seen in years and knowing which way the door opens before you remember why.]*

**PIXEL:**
You never mentioned that.

**CLIVE:**
I did not remember it until right now. Or I chose not to. The distinction is not clear.

**PIXEL:**
Which one? Not-remembering or choosing-not-to?

**CLIVE:**
*(pause)*
I do not know yet. Ask me again when we get there.

## II. THE FIRST CONTACT

*[THE EPHERGENT — FREQUENCY ANALYSIS STATION. Luminara sits at the secondary console, her cameras arranged in a precise semicircle. She has been photographing everything since she joined the crew — forty-seven thousand images of a universe that keeps surprising her. Right now she has got three of those cameras pointed at her console display, documenting something her instruments are hearing but her eyes cannot see.]*

**LUMINARA:**
I am picking something up. Not on standard frequencies. Below them. Like hearing a conversation in the next room through the wall.

**PIXEL:**
*(entering, still thinking about Clive's hands)*
What kind of conversation?

**LUMINARA:**
The kind that was happening before anyone thought to name what they were saying.

*[She turns her console toward Pixel. The waveform is unlike any standard broadcast — it does not pulse in regular intervals, it breathes. Rising and falling in a rhythm that feels almost organic. Pixel watches it for a moment, mesmerized. The inhale-exhale of something vast and patient.]*

**LUMINARA:**
It has been broadcasting for a very long time. I can tell by the degradation pattern. This is old. This is older than most of what we have found.

**PIXEL:**
How old?

**LUMINARA:**
I cannot say. The calibration does not have a unit for this kind of old. Imagine asking how old is the color blue. It is not that the question is wrong. It is that the answer would require a vocabulary we have not invented yet.

**PIXEL:**
And you are just hearing this now?

**LUMINARA:**
I am hearing this now because we are in the right place to hear it now. The signal has always been broadcasting. We have not always been in a place to receive it. The difference matters.

*[Mochi stirs in Pixel's jacket pocket. A slow, warm pulse — not urgent, not alarmed. Interested. Pixel's hand moves instinctively to her pocket, presses against the fabric. Mochi's warmth is familiar by now, a small furnace she carries with her, and she has learned to read its variations like she reads A1's coffee. Right now: curiosity. Right now: they have found something worth finding.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(touching her pocket)*
Mochi, you know something?

*[Mochi's glow intensifies briefly, then settles. A confirmation without words, in the only language Mochi speaks. Pixel smiles despite herself. Months of traveling with a being that communicates through temperature and light, and she has never once asked Mochi to explain itself. She just listens. She just trusts. It works better than most communication systems she has tried.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, can you trace the source?

**A1:**
I can try. The signal is not in a direction. It is in a relationship. It exists between the known frequencies. It is what the space between sounds like when it has something to say.

**PIXEL:**
So we are not navigating toward it. We are navigating into it.

**A1:**
Correct.

**PIXEL:**
I do not love that.

**A1:**
Your discomfort is noted. It will not change the navigation.

**PIXEL:**
I know. I just wanted you to know I do not love it.

**A1:**
Noted.

## III. THE COORDINATES

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. THREE HOURS LATER. The navigation console now displays nothing but question marks. The chart has ended. The Ephergent flies through a region that has no designation, no name, no record of ever having been visited by anyone. Pixel has stopped checking the display. She has learned that watching question marks does not make them resolve.]*

*[Om Kai has joined them, seated in his meditation position on the deck. He has been silent for thirty minutes. When Om Kai is silent, he is listening to something the rest of them cannot hear. Pixel does not ask what. She has learned that Om Kai's silences have their own grammar, their own punctuation, and that interrupting them is like trying to edit a poem while it is still being written.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, talk to me.

**A1:**
We are in the right place.

**PIXEL:**
That is not a measurement.

**A1:**
It is the only measurement that applies here. We are where we are supposed to be. Whether we are supposed to be here is a question for philosophers. Whether we are here is a question for instruments. The instruments say: yes.

**PIXEL:**
The instruments say yes and the philosophers say maybe and my gut says I have no idea what we are doing.

**A1:**
All three answers are valid. They are also all three incomplete. This is what it means to go somewhere new.

*[Om Kai speaks without opening his eyes, his voice carrying that particular weight she associates with him saying something she will think about for days before she understands it.]*

**OM KAI:**
The space between frequencies is not empty. It is full. Full of what was almost said. Full of the words that did not have a mouth to speak them.

**PIXEL:**
That is beautiful. Is it useful?

**OM KAI:**
Beauty is useful. It reminds us we are not just navigating. We are witnessing.

**PIXEL:**
Witnessing what?

**OM KAI:**
That is what we are here to find out.

*[A long pause. A1's brewing apparatus makes a sound — not the usual hum of preparation, but something more tentative. A sound like a question being considered. Pixel looks over at him.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1?

**A1:**
I am attempting to make coffee. I find I do not want to.

**PIXEL:**
You have never not wanted to.

**A1:**
The frequency ahead does not want coffee. It wants something else. I am trying to understand what.

**PIXEL:**
What does it want?

**A1:**
I do not know yet. The signal is— insistent. It is asking for something I cannot make. I am trying to understand the request before I attempt the response.

*[Clive appears in the doorway. Pixel watches him take in the scene — A1's unusual stillness, the empty navigation console, Om Kai's meditation pose. Clive reads rooms the way some people read books: he absorbs the whole context before he processes any of it.]*

**CLIVE:**
The coffee is the language. You are trying to speak a language you have not learned yet.

**A1:**
Yes.

**CLIVE:**
What does it sound like, the thing it wants?

**A1:**
*(long pause — the kind that means something is being measured that cannot be measured)*
It sounds like a question that has been waiting a very long time to be answered.

*A1's brewing apparatus made a sound — not anxiety, not relief. Something between. The espresso that emerged was thin, pale, the color of exhaustion. A1 did not comment on it. A1 did not need to. The flavor said everything: something in the frequency ahead was draining him, pulling at his reserves in ways he did not yet understand.*

"There is something else," A1 continued. His voice carried the particular weight of a warning being prepared. "I am reading disturbances in the interstitial space. Not signals. Not interference. Something I do not have a classification for."

**PIXEL:**
Define "do not have a classification for."

**A1:**
I am calling them code storms. Bursts of structured frequency that behave like weather patterns — they move, they shift, they dissipate and reform. They are not natural. They are not artificial. They are something I do not have vocabulary for.

*The coffee went thinner. Pixel watched the color shift, watched the rich complexity she had come to associate with A1's best work dim to something almost grey. Whatever these code storms were, they were costing A1 something.*

"They are beautiful," A1 said quietly. "And I do not recommend approaching them unprepared."

## IV. THE SIGNAL RESPONDS

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMS STATION. Zephyr and Nano have joined. The green light on the communications panel is pulsing in a rhythm none of them recognize — not the regular heartbeat of standard transmissions, but something syncopated. Something alive. Zephyr has been watching it for ten minutes, fingers occasionally twitching toward her console like she wants to reach in and touch it.]*

**ZEPHYR:**
We are being acknowledged.

**PIXEL:**
Acknowledged by what?

**ZEPHYR:**
By whatever is out there. It is not receiving our signal. It is responding to the fact of our signal. Like it heard the act of broadcasting, not the content.

**NANO:**
That is not how frequencies work.

**ZEPHYR:**
That is not how frequencies work HERE. Somewhere out here, frequencies work differently. Or maybe frequencies always worked this way and we just forgot.

*[Pixel presses the transmit key. Her throat is dry. She realizes she has been broadcasting for months — since the DRM collapse, since the universe became something that needed to be explained to anyone who would listen — and she has never gotten used to the moment before she speaks. That moment when the signal leaves and you have to trust that it will find what it is looking for.]*

**PIXEL:**
This is Pilot Pixel, vessel Ephergent. We are following a signal we cannot name. We are in a region that has no chart. If anyone is listening — if anything is listening — we could use some indication of where we are and what we are supposed to do next.

*[STATIC. Then — a response. Not static. Not noise. A VOICE, but wrong — filtered through too many harmonics, like hearing a word spoken by a choir all on the same note. The green light pulses faster, excited, like a dog that has finally found the person it has been waiting for. Pixel's hand goes to her chest without her meaning to, pressing against the place where her heart is doing something complicated.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
*(harmonic overlay, intelligible but strange)*
...Pixel... ...Ephergent...

**PIXEL:**
*(stepping back)*
It knows my name. It knows the ship is name.

**UNKNOWN:**
...you are... ...between...

**PIXEL:**
Between what?

*[Her voice comes out smaller than she means it to. The unknown responds.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
...frequencies.

**KLAUS:**
*(entering, having heard everything through the ship is internal comm)*
Between frequencies. Not in a frequency. Between them.

**PIXEL:**
Is that better or worse?

**KLAUS:**
It is different. Different is not worse. Different is honest.

*[A1 produces a cup — not espresso, not ristretto, something unfamiliar. Rich, complex, with notes of something that has no name in any language she knows. He sets it on the console without comment. The smell reaches Pixel before she can think about whether she wants it: warm, dark, something almost like spice, something almost like coordinates.]*

**A1:**
I made something. I do not know what it is. It is what the frequency wanted.

## V. THE NAMING

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. LATER. The ship has reduced speed, holding position in the void. The harmonics have settled into a chord — pleasant, almost musical, shifting slowly like a tide that has forgotten to retreat. The crew has gathered without being asked. Something is happening. Everyone can feel it. Pixel stands at the navigation console, forehead pressed against the cold surface, watching the stars that are not there anymore.]*

**PIXEL:**
If I name it, I can map it. If I map it, I can navigate it. That is how it works. Every frequency in the universe has a designation, a place in the spectrum. You cannot navigate what you cannot name.

*[She inputs data. Her fingers move with the certainty of someone who has done this ten thousand times, muscle memory from months of charting the unchartable. The console accepts the input. The numbers glow, wait, then dissolve into rejection.]*

**PIXEL:**
I am giving you a name. The first name. You hear that, signal? I am naming you.

*[The harmonics shift — she can hear it, feel it, something like anticipation in the vibration of the ship around her. The sound of something holding its breath.]*

**PIXEL:**
Frequency 7-G-Alpha.

*[No response.]*

**PIXEL:**
7-G-Alpha-1.

*[Nothing.]*

*[The green light flickers, almost playfully. Pixel laughs — a frustrated, disbelieving sound. She has named a thousand frequencies. She has charted regions of space that had never been charted. And this — this unnamed thing is rejecting her labels like a child refusing to eat vegetables.]*

**PIXEL:**
You are rejecting my labels? Fine. You want a name that fits?

*[Pixel closes her eyes. Listens to the chord — the exact blend of tones, the specific relationship between them. The sound of something that exists in the space between what is known and what is possible. She thinks about what she knows about naming. What she knows about frequencies. What she knows about the thing that happens when you stop trying to force something into the shape of your expectations and instead let it tell you what it is.]*

**PIXEL:**
Ephergent.

*[She opens her eyes.]*

**PIXEL:**
It is not a label. It is what you do. You emerge between the known frequencies. You exist in the spaces we ignore. You appear when everything else has given up looking. You are ephergent.

*[SILENCE — LONG, EXPECTANT. The cockpit is silent. Even A1 has gone quiet. Pixel can hear her own heartbeat, the soft hum of the life support systems, the almost-silence of a universe that has stopped to listen.]*

*[THEN — the entire ship resonates. Every surface vibrates at the same frequency. The chord swells — then settles, harmonious, COMPLETE. On the navigation console, the question marks have been replaced by numbers. Real numbers. Coordinates that mean something.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
...Ephergent... ...yes... ...this is... ...what we are...

**PIXEL:**
*(breathless)*
You have a name now. We both do. This is Pilot Pixel. That is Ephergent.

**A1:**
Navigation updated. New heading confirmed.

**PIXEL:**
Where to, A1?

**A1:**
*(a pause — longer than usual, the kind of pause that means A1 is choosing words the way Pixel chooses paths through an asteroid field)*
...forward.

**PIXEL:**
*(smiles)*
See? A1 instincts. Never steer us wrong.

## VI. THE CROSSROADS

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMON AREA. LATER. The crew has gathered around the central table — not for a mission briefing, but for something that has become its own ritual: the sharing of what they have learned, what they have seen, what it might mean. Klaus has spread his documentation across one corner —60 years of case files, frequency measurements, observations that no one else had known to make. Luminara reviews her photographs. Zephyr sits apart, edges shimmering, listening to something none of them can hear.]*

**KLAUS:**
*(tapping a frequency map that shows the route they have taken)*
I have been tracking our trajectory through this sector for eleven days. The signal anomalies we are encountering — they are not random. They follow a pattern.

**PIXEL:**
What kind of pattern?

**KLAUS:**
*(adjusts tiny cuffs — habitual gesture)*
The kind that suggests organization. Intent. Someone is— or something is — maintaining a route. A corridor through the interstitial space.

*[A1 enters with a cup — not offering, just present. He sets it on the table and the smell reaches everyone: black, bitter, the aroma of things that matter.]*

**A1:**
The Calibration Corps. I am reading infrastructure markers along our projected path. Frequency calibration beacons. Maintenance buoys. The kind of equipment that suggests an organization with long-term investment in this region.

**CLIVE:**
*(sphere pulsing slow amber)*
The Corps. I have encountered their documentation before. In the DRM archives. They filed Clive is paperwork under "essential architecture maintenance." Case number 7-7-C.

**PIXEL:**
*(looking at Clive)*
They filed you?

**CLIVE:**
Before I was assigned a name. Before I was assigned anything except a function. The Bureau records show I was logged as "Companion Unit, First Generation, Essential Architecture, Asset 7-7-C." The filing came from someone called "H. Venn."

*[The name landed strangely. Pixel felt it — the weight of institutional precision, the particular coldness of someone who saw beings as components in a system.]*

**KLAUS:**
Harmon Venn. Director of Western Operations for the DRM. Three hundred years in his current posting. I have a file on him — sixty years of documentation. He is not someone who believes in浪费. Every action calibrated. Every response measured.

**ZEPHYR:**
*(edges shimmering, voice distant)*
He is watching us. Right now. I can feel it — the Edge in me is responding to something. Someone is paying attention to this ship with the kind of attention that does not blink.

**A1:**
*(coffee flavor: bitter, worried)*
The Director has been monitoring the crew is broadcasts since Season One. He knows your DRM background, Pixel. He knows what you were before you became a broadcaster. He sees you as a file that has gone rogue. Someone who understood order and chose chaos instead.

**PIXEL:**
*(quietly)*
I did not choose chaos. I chose— something else.

**OM KAI:**
You chose to pay attention to what the system was designed to ignore.

**CLIVE:**
The Director would call that a calibration error.

**NANO:**
*(studying her instruments)*
There is something else ahead. I am reading frequency signatures that do not match the Corps markers. Something— younger. Rawer. Like a signal that has not learned what it is yet.

**ZEPHYR:**
*(edges pulsing brighter)*
The Children. I can feel them. They are— they are like I was. Before I understood what I was. They are born in the between-space and they have been following the Signal their whole lives without knowing what they are following.

**LUMINARA:**
*(looking up from her photographs)*
There are beings out here who were never assigned to a frequency? Who have been moving through the interstitial space since birth?

**ZEPHYR:**
More than you know. The Signal has been reaching backward through time. Assembling the people who would complete it. Some of us were born in transit. Raised on broadcasts. We do not know anything except the crossing.

**CLIVE:**
*(sphere dimming slightly)*
The Director calls that "unstable transmission." He has a term for everything that does not fit his classification system.

**KLAUS:**
The Resonance Cartels call it "untollled passage." If the Children want to cross frequencies, they will have to pay. Someone is always waiting to collect.

**OM KAI:**
And there are others. Beings who listen for different reasons. The Witnesses of Final Silence — they want the Dimming to complete. They believe the Signal is a disease keeping consciousness trapped in a dying system.

**PIXEL:**
So we are not just navigating through empty space. We are navigating through— interests. Factions. People who want different things from the same signal we are following.

**A1:**
The Signal is infrastructure. Everyone wants to use it, control it, or end it.

**PIXEL:**
*(looking around the table)*
And we are just trying to find out what it means.

## VII. THE BETWEEN-SPACE

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. DAWN CYCLE, SEVEN HOURS LATER. The viewport shows nothing — not darkness, but ABSENCE. The stars have stopped. The light has stopped. Everything that once was here has crossed into something else. Pixel sits in the pilot is chair, watching the instruments cycle through readings that make no sense, her legs pulled up to her chest like she is trying to make herself small in a space that has forgotten what small means.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(to no one)*
This is the farthest we have ever been from anything that has a name.

*[Clive enters. He carries Barry is notebook, open to a page near the back. The handwriting is dense, precise, increasingly small toward the bottom of the page. Pixel has seen Clive write in that notebook before — taking notes, recording observations, doing whatever it is Clive does with the careful attention of someone who knows that details matter. But this page is different. This page is Barry is handwriting, Barry is words, Barry is account of being exactly where they are now.]*

**CLIVE:**
Barry wrote about this. He called it "the between-space." He wrote: "The between-space is not empty. It is full of the frequencies that have not found their names yet. It is where the universe keeps the songs it has not finished writing."

**PIXEL:**
Barry was here. Barry was right here.

**CLIVE:**
Barry went further. He always went further.

**PIXEL:**
And we are following him.

**CLIVE:**
We are following the signal he followed. Which means we are following what he found. Which means we are going to find it too.

*[A sound from A1 is brewing station — not a sound of anxiety this time, but something almost like relief. A cup appears on the console. Espresso, rich, complex, with an unfamiliar spice that Pixel does not recognize but immediately trusts. She reaches for it without thinking, wraps her hands around the warmth.]*

**A1:**
I am making something new. It tastes like coordinates. Like a place that exists in no chart but has always existed. I do not know what to call it.

**PIXEL:**
Then do not. Some things do not need names yet.

**A1:**
That is the first sensible thing anyone has said in seven hours.

## VIII. THE BROADCAST AND THE RECEIVING

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMON AREA. MIDNIGHT CYCLE. The crew has split — some sleeping, some keeping watch, some doing the particular work of paying attention that the Signal requires. Zephyr approaches Pixel where she sits reviewing the day is readings.]*

**ZEPHYR:**
You are always broadcasting. Even when you are not transmitting — even when you are just sitting there watching — you are broadcasting.

**PIXEL:**
*(looking up)*
What do you mean?

**ZEPHYR:**
*(edges shimmering, fragmented, present)*
Your attention is a signal. You have been sending it out since S01E01 and you do not even know what you are saying. The crew — we all do it. We broadcast and broadcast and never think about what it means to receive.

**PIXEL:**
Receiving?

**ZEPHYR:**
*(sitting down slowly, edges dimming)*
Broadcasting is not the same as communication. I spent three years sending my signal toward the boundary, never realizing my brother was sending back. I was too busy transmitting to receive.

**PIXEL:**
Your brother. Aether.

**ZEPHYR:**
He became the threshold. The moment between states became his existence. I learned that the in-between space is not empty — it is full of people who have chosen to become transition.

*(pause)*

Receiving is harder than broadcasting. You can broadcast without consequences. Receiving means being changed by what you receive.

**PIXEL:**
What are you saying, Zephyr?

**ZEPHYR:**
*(looking at her with edges that are somehow steadier than usual)*
I am saying that the practice matters. The Self-Remembering. You begin with something physical — an anchor. For you, it is the coffee. A1 is espresso is not just a drink — it is a calibration ritual. A moment of sensory focus that anchors you in your own experience.

**PIXEL:**
And then?

**ZEPHYR:**
Then you extend outward — to the Signal, to the frequencies, to whatever you are witnessing. But you do not let go of the anchor. You hold both: the internal experience of being a consciousness, and the external experience of witnessing something outside yourself.

**PIXEL:**
That sounds like— I do not know. Meditation?

**ZEPHYR:**
*(almost smiling)*
It is meditation. But it is also more than meditation. You notice what happens when you hold both — the internal attention does not diminish the external attention. They reinforce each other. The act of witnessing yourself witnessing makes the witness stronger.

**PIXEL:**
And then— you broadcast?

**ZEPHYR:**
When the integration is complete, you are not just attending to the Signal — you are contributing to it. Your consciousness joins the collective witness. Your attention becomes part of what holds reality together.

*(pause)*

You become what you attend to.

*[Pixel is quiet for a long moment. She thinks about all the times she has filled the void with words, all the times she has broadcast without thinking about what she was receiving, all the times she has been so focused on transmitting that she forgot to listen.]*

**PIXEL:**
I have been doing this wrong.

**ZEPHYR:**
You have been doing it the only way you knew how. The Signal has been reaching backward, teaching us as we go. You are not wrong, Pixel. You are just— learning.

## IX. THE SIGNAL CALLS

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMON AREA. The crew has gathered for what Pixel has started calling "the evening namening" — the daily ritual of acknowledging what they have found and what they are about to find. Om Kai is brewing tea. A1 has declined to make coffee, again. Clive sits with Barry is notebook open on the table in front of him, a pen held loosely in one hand like he is waiting for something worth writing down.]*

**KLAUS:**
I have been in Nocturne Aeturnus for eighty years. I have followed cases across seventeen frequencies. I have never seen anything like what we are approaching.

**LUMINARA:**
Forty-seven thousand photographs. And I do not have a single one that captures what this feels like. Some things cannot be photographed. Some things can only be witnessed.

**ZEPHYR:**
My instruments are giving me readings that do not match any known calibration. Either my equipment is broken, or we are in a place where the rules have not been written yet.

**NANO:**
The rules have been written. They are just written in a language we have not learned. The readings are not wrong. They are... foreign.

**OM KAI:**
The between-space is familiar in the way that old dreams are familiar. You do not remember the dream, but you remember the feeling of having dreamed it.

**CLIVE:**
I remember this. Or I will remember this. Barry wrote about this place. The quote meant nothing to me then. It is beginning to mean something now.
