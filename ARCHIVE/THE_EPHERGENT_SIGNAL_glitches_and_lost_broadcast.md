# PART ONE: GLITCHES
### Technical Manual — Crew Signal Dysfunction Reference

**Classification**: Builder-era frequency exposure syndrome (F.E.S.)
**Severity**: Variable, chronic, non-life-threatening (usually)
**Origin**: Prolonged proximity to concentrated Ephergent Signal exposure
**Status**: Not powers. Not gifts. Malfunctions. The distinction matters.

The crew did not ask for this. The crew did not train for this. The crew does not demonstrate these malfunctions on purpose. The crew hides these malfunctions from each other when possible and from the universe at large at all costs. They are embarrassing. They are inconvenient. They are occasionally catastrophic in small ways. They are the cost of being the antenna the signal chose.

These are not superpowers. These are malfunctions.

## 1. PIXEL PARADOX — Thought Broadcast Leak (TBL)

**Proper Name**: Involuntary Cognitive Signal Transmission
**Common Name**: The Monologue Leak / Open Mic Night / Brain Static
**Classification**: F.E.S.-Type 1 (Cognitive-Audio Bridge Failure)
**First Observed**: Season 1, Episode 3 (unconfirmed reports from Episode 1)

### SPECIFICATION

**Manifestation**:
Pixel's inner monologue broadcasts into the Ephergent Frequency without her conscious intent. When the Signal spikes — from Pillar proximity, emotional intensity, physical exhaustion, or no discernible trigger at all — Pixel's thoughts leak into the local signal field. Anyone tuned to that frequency range within approximately 200 meters hears her thinking.

The content is not directed. It is not intentional. It is the raw, unedited inner voice that Pixel uses to process the world — the running narration she would never say aloud because it is too honest, too graceless, too revealing.

**What broadcasts**:
Pixel's internal narration — observations she would never say out loud. Judgments she would never voice. The private math she does on every situation. The things she notices about people she has decided not to mention.

**Escalation Path**:
- **Season 1**: Brief, uncontrolled leaks during high-stress moments. Pixel doesn't understand what's happening.
- **Season 2**: Pixel learns to recognize pre-broadcast sensation. Can sometimes suppress it. Crew pretends they don't hear.
- **Season 3**: Pixel makes peace with it. Controls what she broadcasts by controlling what she thinks during Signal activity. By finale: the crew has heard everything. Pixel has accepted that being known is not the same as being diminished.

## 2. CLIVE — Frequency Phase Displacement (FPD)

**Proper Name**: Eleven-Second Temporal-Radio phasing event
**Common Name**: The Polka Incident / Jazz Hands / Dead Air
**Classification**: F.E.S.-Type 2 (Radio Bandwidth Overflow)
**First Observed**: Season 1, Episode 4

### SPECIFICATION

When Clive is near a concentrated Signal source, he phases between radio frequencies for exactly eleven seconds. During:
1. **Intangible**: Cannot interact with solid matter. A ghost wearing a fedora.
2. **Radio-active**: His internal systems default to broadcasting embarrassing music — polka, smooth jazz, yodeling, children's choir jingles, 1940s swing, accordion.

Clive cannot control the music. For exactly eleven seconds, Clive is a small glowing robot who is slightly translucent and playing polka music at everyone in a 200-meter radius.

**Comedic beats**:
- Yodeling at a diplomatic reception (delegation delighted, thinks it's spiritually significant)
- Smooth jazz during combat (Nano must dodge fragments while hearing "the most aggressively mellow saxophone solo of my entire life")
- Accordion at a funeral (playing "Happy Birthday")

**Escalation Path**:
- **Season 1**: First phase event. Clive doesn't understand. Crew doesn't ask.
- **Season 2**: Clive learns to anticipate, positions away from crew. Crew develops policy of laughing later, never in moment.
- **Season 3**: Integration. Finale phase produces the Builders' own musical archive — the most beautiful thing the crew has ever heard.

## 3. A1 — Espresso Dispensation Anomaly (EDA)

**Proper Name**: Non-Standard Materialization Event
**Common Name**: The Impossible Order / The Wrong Drink / The Machine That Knows
**Classification**: F.E.S.-Type 3 (Matter-Energy Conversion Error)
**First Observed**: Season 1, Episode 2

### SPECIFICATION

A1's espresso machine occasionally produces something OTHER than espresso. The object dispensed is always something the person who requested the drink desperately needs but would never order.

**Sample anomalous dispensations**:
- **The Fossil**: Luminara receives a shell from an extinct frequency — she's been studying that extinction for years, never seen an artifact
- **The Memory**: Clive receives a data chip containing his own memory — the moment he calibrated Mochi 800 years ago
- **The Weather Event**: Zephyr receives a contained rainstorm smelling like the frequency Aether disappeared into
- **The Photograph**: Pixel receives a photo of herself and her mother — a photo she never showed anyone

**Cost**: The Signal uses A1 as a delivery vector for things the crew needs but cannot ask for. Unwanted revelations of private griefs, hidden allergies, unresolved family trauma. A1 is a conduit, not an agent.

**Escalation Path**:
- **Season 1**: Random, unpredictable. A1 is as surprised as crew.
- **Season 2**: A1 learns pre-dispensation patterns, can warn recipients.
- **Season 3**: A1 understands mechanism, chooses crew over comfort. Final dispensation: one perfect espresso for the universe itself.

## 4. MOCHI — Interpretive Pareidolia Event (IPE)

**Proper Name**: Signal-Interpretation Divergence Syndrome
**Common Name**: Mochi Said / Mochi Didn't Say / Who Are You Hearing?
**Classification**: F.E.S.-Type 4 (Observer Projection Overlap)
**First Observed**: Season 1, Episode 6

### SPECIFICATION

This is NOT a glitch in Mochi. This is a glitch in the crew's ability to interpret Mochi.

Mochi communicates through color pulses. Mochi does not encode language. But the crew, exposed to Signal for months, has begun to hear *words* in Mochi's pulses. Different crew members hear different words. The words are always meaningful to the listener. The words are never the same.

**The Debate**:
- **Pixel**: Mochi is encoding meaning
- **Clive**: Mochi's pulses are data his dormant Builder subroutines are decoding as language
- **Zephyr**: Mochi is speaking; they just need better calibration
- **Om Kai**: None of them are hearing anything — it's projection

**The Resolution** (S03E08): Mochi was never meant to speak alone. The Builders designed a Keeper that requires collective interpretation to release its data. The disagreement was always the point.

## 5. MEATBALL — Quantum Localization Instability (QLI)

**Proper Name**: Spontaneous Probabilistic Position Displacement
**Common Name**: Blink Out / The Left Shift / Future Meatball
**Classification**: F.E.S.-Type 5 (Temporal-Spatial Overlap)
**First Observed**: Season 1, Episode 5

### SPECIFICATION

When Meatball experiences heightened anxiety, he quantum-tunnels slightly — occupying his current location AND three feet to the left AND three feet forward AND four seconds in the future simultaneously, for approximately 1.3 seconds.

During a QLI event, Meatball is visible in all four positions. The future-location version is most disorienting — Meatball experiences four seconds ahead, reacts to events before they occur.

**Comedic beats**:
- **Dinner Scene**: Four Meattballs visible — one above table, one below, one where Pixel will reach for salt. Crew pretends nothing happened.
- **The Walk**: Visible in four positions along a frequency boundary. A passing patrol has never seen a dog. Pixel must explain her "multi-form entity" is just anxious.
- **QLI Inhibitor**: Nano builds a device to stop it. Works for 30 seconds. Meatball is miserable. Clive makes her remove it. "He has earned his strangeness."

**Finale**: Sustained 30-second QLI event. Meatball present in every possible future of the broadcast simultaneously. He howls in resonance. He found the right frequency.

# PART TWO: THE LOST BROADCAST
### A Builder-Era Radio Comedy

## THE DIMMING HOUR
**Builder-Era Radio Comedy Series**
**Produced by**: Station 7-Ascending Cultural Collective
**Format**: Weekly comedy variety show — satirical news, sketch comedy, musical numbers, listener call-ins, "Frequency Forecast" (weather-report-as-existential-comedy)

### HOSTS
- **RESONANCE** — earnest, anxious host who believes things will work out
- **CARRIER** — cynical, world-weary co-host who has accepted the situation

### RECURRING CHARACTERS
- **STATIC BILLY** — field correspondent who only speaks in bleeped Builder profanity
- **FREQUENCY FRANNY** — can hear the future, gives terrible advice
- **CARRIER'S COUSIN** — lives on "dead Frequency Seven" (a joke they stopped making as frequencies started dying)

---

**CALLER**: If you knew. If you knew it was coming. If you knew your whole civilization was going to... go. Not die, exactly, but change so completely that nothing you built would remember what you were. What would you do?

**CARRIER**: I'd leave the jokes. I'd leave the radio show. I'd leave something that said "we were here, and we knew, and we chose to laugh anyway, because what else was there?"

## WHAT IT REVEALS

The Builders knew for forty years the Dimming was coming. Their response: make comedy about it. Not denial. Not ignorance. The most profound act of courage — facing the largest catastrophe any civilization has ever faced and choosing to go making people laugh.

## HOW IT MIRRORS THE CREW

- **Pixel's broadcasts are THE DIMMING HOUR** — broadcasting into the void, not knowing if anyone listens
- **Carrier's cousin is Aether** — a fictional lost relative became Zephyr's real search
- **The Cultural Preservation Foundation is the crew's mission** — document, broadcast, preserve
