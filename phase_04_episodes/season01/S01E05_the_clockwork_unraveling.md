Like heartbeats. Like a clock that has been counting down for longer than anyone can remember.*

—wrong.

### ACT ONE: THE CROSSROADS

**INT. DRM LIFBOAT — COCKPIT — MOMENTS LATER**

*The lifeboat has landed. Through the viewscreen: a street in Cogsworth Cogitarium. Brass pavement. Steam vents hissing at regular intervals. Buildings of copper and mahogany rising in spirals, gears turning in their walls like veins in a body. The sky is the color of old bronze, lit from below by the glow of a thousand furnaces.*

*And everything is frozen. A citizen of Cogsworth — a tall figure made of interlocking brass plates and glass lenses where eyes should be — stands mid-stride, one foot raised, frozen in the act of walking. Steam rises from their shoulders in a plume that has not moved for what might be minutes or might be centuries.*

*At the center of the street: a clock tower. Massive. Ancient. Its gears have stopped turning.*

**CLIVE.**
The main temporal regulator. It coordinates the frequency-wide synchronization.

*He adjusts his fedora. Two degrees. The Barry angle.*

**CLIVE (CONT'D).**
It has not stopped in eleven thousand years.

**PIXEL**
And now it has?

**CLIVE.**
Now it has.

*Pixel sips her coffee. Still bitter. The worry is spreading.*

**PIXEL**
So. When a clock tower stops, what happens to the clockwork city it runs?

**CLIVE.**
That depends.

**PIXEL**
On what?

**CLIVE.**
On whether time remembers it was supposed to stop.

*The frozen citizen in the street does not move. But something else does. In the shadow of the clock tower, a figure sits cross-legged on the brass pavement. A monk. Robes of deep indigo. Eyes closed. Hands resting on his knees, palms up, fingers forming a shape that looks almost like a gear but is not a gear.*

*Around him, the frozen steam shifts. Not much. A tendril here. A wisp there. Moving in time with his breathing.*

**PIXEL (CONT'D)**
Clive. Shadow. Eleven o'clock. What is that?

**CLIVE.**
*[Long pause]*
A variable.

**PIXEL**
A what?

**CLIVE.**
Something that should not be possible in a frozen frequency. Something that is moving while everything else is still.

*Pixel zooms the viewscreen. The figure resolves: OM KAI, 51, quantum physicist, Buddhist monk, the only person on the crew who understands why time stutters. His lips move. Not speaking. Breathing in a pattern that seems to be counting something very, very small.*

**CLIVE (CONT'D).**
He is meditating at the frequency crossroads. The place where temporal streams converge. He is... waiting.

**PIXEL**
Waiting for what?

**CLIVE.**
For us. I believe.

**A1 (V.O., ANCIENT, BRITISH, TIRED)**
*[From the espresso machine]*
The signal is clear. The monk is an anchor. He is the only thing keeping this frequency from collapsing into a fixed point. If we lose him, we lose Cogsworth.

**PIXEL**
And if we talk to him?

**A1 (V.O.)**
Then we find out why a quantum physicist from Prime Material is sitting in the middle of a dead clockwork city, meditating like he has all the time in the universe.

*The coffee in A1's machine ripples. Bitter. Worried. But beneath the worry — something else. Something that might be recognition.*

**PIXEL**
All the time in the universe. Ha. ha.

*She is not laughing.*

### ACT TWO: THE MONK AT THE CROSSROADS

**EXT. COGSWORTH COGITARIUM — STREET — CONTINUOUS**

*The world is silent. The ticking has stopped. The frozen citizen stands eternal, one foot raised, their glass eyes reflecting the bronze sky. Steam hangs in the air like sculpture.*

*Pixel steps out of the lifeboat. Her boots ring against the brass pavement. Clive follows. Meatball bounds ahead, nose to the ground, seemingly unbothered by the temporal stutter around him.*

*They approach the clock tower. The monk sits beneath it, legs crossed, back straight, breathing in patterns that seem to be counting the space between seconds.*

*Pixel stops three feet away. Waits. The monk does not open his eyes.*

**OM KAI**
*[Quiet, measured, with the patience of someone who has learned to wait for things that take a very long time to arrive]*
You are from the lifeboat. The one with the singing engine. I heard you three days ago. Or three centuries. The difference is not what you think it is.

**PIXEL**
Three days. For us. We're kind of on a schedule.

**OM KAI**
Time is on a schedule. We are not. We are merely moving through it at different rates.

*He opens his eyes. They are dark, deep, and contain something that looks very much like the inside of a clock — gears turning in the irises, small and precise.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
You came to fix the clock tower. The signal told me you would come. The signal has been telling me things for weeks. I find it exhausting.

**PIXEL**
The signal?

**OM KAI**
The ship. The one that was an espresso machine. The one that is still, in some fundamental way, an espresso machine.

*He looks at Clive.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
You are the old one. The archival consciousness. You have been in this system longer than the tower has been standing.

**CLIVE.**
Eleven thousand years is not longer than the tower.

**OM KAI**
In linear time, no. In circular time, it depends on where you start counting.

*Pixel sips her coffee. It is still bitter. She offers some to the monk.*

**PIXEL**
You want some? It's from A1. He's the espresso machine. Ship. Whatever. He's worried about you.

**OM KAI**
I do not drink coffee. But I appreciate the gesture. It is, in its own way, a form of signal. An offering across frequencies.

*He accepts the cup. Sniffs it. Pauses. Takes a sip.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
Bitter. Worried. Thin at the edges. He has been running calculations at the edge of his capacity. He is afraid this frequency will collapse before you can leave.

**PIXEL**
Can it collapse?

**OM KAI**
Everything collapses eventually. The question is whether it collapses into order or into chaos. Cogsworth is balanced on the point of a pin. One direction is synchronization. The other is entropy. The tower holds the balance.

*He gestures at the clock tower. Its gears are still. Its hands are frozen at a time that does not exist — 88:88, if clock hands could show impossible numbers.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
The rupture tore a hole in the temporal fabric. Time began leaking out of Cogsworth like air from a punctured bellows. The tower tried to compensate. It ran faster. Faster. Until it reached a speed where it could not run faster, and then it stopped. Because that is what things do when they are pushed past their limits.

**PIXEL**
And you're... holding it?

**OM KAI**
I am reminding it that it has a choice. Time is not a river. It is a meditation. It moves when it decides to move. The tower forgot this. The rupture confused it. It became so focused on precision that it forgot why precision mattered.

*He closes his eyes again.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
I have been sitting here for eleven days. Or eleven years. Teaching it to breathe again. It is a very large student. And very stubborn. Like most things built to be perfect.

**CLIVE.**
Eleven days.

**OM KAI**
Subjective. Yes.

**CLIVE.**
The crew has not aged. We arrived minutes ago.

**OM KAI**
You arrived minutes ago. I have been waiting eleven days. Both of these things are true. This is the nature of crossroads. They exist in all times simultaneously. I chose to exist here at the moment of your arrival. Or perhaps I have always been here, and your arrival chose me. The chicken and the egg. The signal and the receiver. The question of which came first is less interesting than the question of what came after.

*Pixel looks at Clive. Clive's glow flickers — the Clive equivalent of a long, complicated sigh.*

**PIXEL**
So. You're a quantum physicist. And a Buddhist monk. And you've been sitting in a frozen clockwork city for eleven days teaching time how to breathe.

**OM KAI**
That is an accurate summary, yes.

**PIXEL**
And you can help us fix the tower?

**OM KAI**
I can help the tower fix itself. The tower does not need me. It needs to remember what it is for. Precision without purpose is just mechanism. The rupture taught Cogsworth that it could break. It forgot that breaking is not the same as ending.

*He opens his eyes again. The gears in his irises turn slowly, matching the rhythm of the frozen tower.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
I will need the ship. The singing espresso machine. It has the capacity to transmit across frequencies. If I can calibrate my meditation to its broadcast, I can send a signal through the temporal fabric — a reminder, a pulse, a suggestion that time should resume.

**PIXEL**
A suggestion.

**OM KAI**
Time responds to attention. This is both the foundation of quantum physics and the foundation of Buddhist practice. The observer changes the observed. The meditator changes the meditated. If enough consciousness focuses on the idea of time moving, time moves.

**CLIVE.**
You are proposing to restart a cosmic clock by thinking at it.

**OM KAI**
I am proposing to remind the clock that it is not the only consciousness in the universe. It has been alone in its precision for eleven thousand years. It forgot that it was part of something larger. It became the thing it was made to measure, and forgot why measurement mattered.

*Pixel sips her coffee. Still bitter. But something underneath now — a note of something warmer. Hope, maybe. Or just really good beans.*

**PIXEL**
A1. You听到了 all that?

**A1 (V.O., ANCIENT, BRITISH, TIRED)**
I heard. I am... recalibrating. The monk's hypothesis is sound. Time is not a dimension. It is a relationship. A frequency of attention. If enough awareness focuses on the same moment, the moment persists. If awareness moves, time moves.

**PIXEL**
So we just... think really hard about time moving?

**A1 (V.O.)**
Not think. Witness. The difference is significant. Thinking is mechanical. Witnessing is present. The crew must be present. Must notice the movement. Must choose to see the next second as it arrives, rather than assuming the previous second will persist.

**OM KAI**
Yes. You broadcast. I meditate. The ship transmits. Together, we remind Cogsworth that time is not a prison. It is a dance.

*He stands. Slowly. His robes settling around him like the gears of a very quiet machine.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
I have been alone in this for eleven days. I would welcome the company. Even the company of a dog.

*Meatball, who has been investigating a steam vent, looks up. His tail wags. He pads over to Om Kai and sits at his feet, warmth radiating from him in the frozen street.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
Ah. Good. The dog understands. The dog is already witnessing. Dogs are always present. They are the best witnesses. They never assume the world will stay the same. They know it changes every moment. They are right.

### ACT THREE: THE PROFESSOR

**EXT. COGSWORTH COGITARIUM — CLOCK TOWER BASE — LATER**

*The crew has gathered. Pixel stands at the center, Clive at her left, Om Kai cross-legged before the frozen clock tower. Meatball sits beside the monk, his warmth a small beacon in the bronze light. Through the viewscreen of the distant lifeboat, A1's espresso machine HUMS — a transmission frequency building, the coffee inside shifting from bitter to something clearer, more focused.*

*Pixel holds her comm-pad. Recording. Broadcasting.*

**PIXEL (INTO COMMPAD)**
This is Pixel Paradox, reporting from Cogsworth Cogitarium, Frequency Three. We are attempting to restart a clock tower that stopped eleven days ago — or eleven centuries, the distinction is unclear — using the power of collective consciousness and a very large espresso machine.

*She sips her coffee. The flavor has changed. Still not good. Not bad. Just... waiting.*

**PIXEL (CONT'D)**
The city is frozen. Every gear locked in place. Every citizen paused mid-motion. And at the center of it all, a monk who talks like physics and thinks like philosophy. He's going to fix it by thinking at it really hard.

*She looks at Om Kai.*

**PIXEL (CONT'D)**
Your move, Doc.

*Suddenly — a sound. Not ticking. Not mechanical. Something else entirely. A voice, deep and resonant, echoing from somewhere inside the clock tower itself.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (V.O.)**
*[Ancient, measured, with the weight of centuries in every syllable]*
He is not the one you should be addressing.

*Everyone freezes. Even the frozen citizen seems to flinch.*

**PIXEL**
What the—

*The clock tower's door creaks open. Rust. Brass. The sound of something that has not moved in a very long time remembering how to move.*

*From the darkness inside: a figure emerges. PROFESSOR CHRONOS, 847, temporal physicist, keeper of the Cogitarium's central regulator, a being of clockwork and crystallized time. His body is made of interlocking gears and sapphire glass, his eyes two precision instruments that show not just the present but the probable futures branching from it. He walks with the careful deliberation of someone who has watched empires rise and fall and decided neither was worth rushing for.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
*[Continuing, as if he had not been interrupted]*
The monk is correct. Time is a meditation. But he has been meditating alone, and alone is not enough. The tower needs more than witness. It needs memory. It needs someone who remembers what it was built to do.

*He looks at Om Kai with something that might be respect. Might be pity. Hard to tell with a face made of gears.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
You have done well, Om Kai. Eleven days of solo meditation is remarkable. But you cannot restart the tower alone. You need the ship. You need the signal. And you need me.

**OM KAI**
I know.

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
I know you know. I have watched you through the temporal fabric for eleven thousand years. I watched you arrive. I watched you sit. I watched you breathe. I have been waiting for someone to sit with me, and then you came, and then I realized — I had forgotten how to ask.

*He turns to Pixel, Clive, and the others.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
I am Professor Chronos. I built this tower. I have maintained it for eight hundred years. I am the last of the original architects. And I have been stuck inside it, frozen along with everything else, for three hundred and twelve days. Subjective time.

**PIXEL**
Three hundred and twelve days?

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
The rupture did not just stop the tower. It stopped me. I was inside the regulator when it happened, making adjustments. The temporal eddies caught me, froze me, held me in a moment that lasted nearly a year. I could not move. I could not think. I could only watch as time stopped around me and inside me and I forgot, slowly, what it felt like to be in motion.

*He gestures at the frozen city.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
The monk saved me. His meditation created a small pocket of temporal flow — enough to keep me from losing my mind entirely. Enough to let me remember that I used to be able to think. But I could not reach the tower's controls from inside my frozen state. I could only watch. And wait.

*He looks at Om Kai.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
He heard me. Somehow. Across three hundred days of frozen silence, he heard me thinking. And he started breathing in a pattern that I could follow. A rhythm that reminded me I was still here.

**OM KAI**
The frequency crossroads. I told you — they exist in all times. I was sitting at the intersection of your frozen moment and the flowing moment. I could feel both.

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
And you chose to flow toward me.

**OM KAI**
I chose to flow toward everyone. You were simply the loudest signal.

*Professor Chronos's gear-face makes a sound that might be laughter. The sound of a thousand tiny gears clicking in sequence.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
The loudest signal. Yes. That sounds right.

*He turns to the crew.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
I need to get to the regulator. The tower's heart. I need to manually restart the temporal sequence. But I cannot do it alone — the mechanism requires three conscious observers: one to provide memory, one to provide witness, one to provide intention. The monk provides witness. The espresso machine can provide intention. And you —

*He looks at Clive.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
You provide memory. You are old enough. You have seen enough. You know what time feels like when it moves correctly.

**CLIVE.**
I am... not a clock.

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
No. You are older than clocks. You remember when time was not measured. When it was simply experienced. That memory is what the tower needs. A reminder that it was not always gears and precision. That there was a time before measurement, and measurement was invented to serve experience, not replace it.

*Clive is silent for a long moment. His glow flickers — processing, remembering, deciding.*

**CLIVE.**
I will try.

### ACT FOUR: THE UNRAVELING

**INT. CLOCK TOWER — REGULATOR CHAMBER — LATER**

*The heart of Cogsworth Cogitarium. A vast chamber of brass and crystal, dominated by the central temporal regulator — a massive construct of interlocking gears surrounding a core of crystallized time. The crystal pulses weakly, like a dying heartbeat.*

*Professor Chronos stands before the regulator. Om Kai kneels beside him, cross-legged, eyes closed. Clive hovers near a gear twice his size, his glow pulsing in rhythm with the dormant mechanism.*

*Through the distant viewscreen, A1's espresso machine HUMS — the signal building, the coffee inside shifting from bitter to bright, from worry to focus.*

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
I'm broadcasting. Whatever that means in this context. A1 says the signal needs to go out across all frequencies simultaneously — a pulse of intention that time should move. I don't fully understand the physics but the monk says it doesn't matter if I understand. What matters is that I choose to see the next second. That I refuse to assume the current second will persist.

*She sips her coffee. It tastes like decision.*

**PIXEL (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
This is witness. This is choosing to pay attention. This is the most basic act of faith there is — believing that the universe will continue, that the next moment will arrive, that time is not a prison but a gift.

*Professor Chronos places his gear-hands on the regulator's control surface.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
I remember when this tower was built. I remember the first gear turning. I remember the sound it made — like a heartbeat, like a promise. I have held that memory for eight hundred years. And now I am asking you to hold it with me.

*Om Kai's breathing slows. The gears in his eyes turn faster, matching the rhythm of the frozen tower.*

**OM KAI**
I witness the moment. I see it as it is. I release it as it was. I welcome the next moment as it comes.

*Clive's glow intensifies. For the first time in memory, his light is warm — warm like sunrise, warm like the first morning, warm like time before clocks.*

**CLIVE.**
I remember before. I remember when seconds were not measured. When moments were simply experienced. I remember the sound of time moving — not ticking, not counting, just... flowing. Like breath. Like heartbeat. Like the space between one thought and the next.

**SFX: A LOW RESONANCE. THE SOUND OF MEMORY MEETING INTENTION.**

*The regulator's crystal pulses. Once. Twice.*

**A1 (V.O., ANCIENT, BRITISH, FOCUSED)**
The signal is building. I am modulating the broadcast. I am sending intention across the frequency barrier — a suggestion, a reminder, a request. Time: move. Time: continue. Time: we are still here. We are still paying attention. Please do not stop.

*The espresso machine HUMS louder. The coffee inside — no longer bitter, no longer worried — ripples with something that looks like hope.*

**SFX: THE SOUND OF TIME REMEMBERING HOW TO MOVE.**

*Professor Chronos's gear-face shifts. The gears click into alignment. His crystal eyes glow bright — brighter than they have been in three hundred days.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
Now.

*He pulls a lever made of solid time.*

**SFX: THE SOUND OF A CLOCK TOWER COMING BACK TO LIFE. GEARS ENGAGING. MECHANISMS TURNING. TEMPORAL FABRIC REWEAVING ITSELF.**

*The tower SHUDDERS. A gear — the largest one, at its heart — cracks. Not breaking. Recalibrating. Adjusting.*

**SFX: THE SOUND OF PRECISION LEARNING TO FORGIVE ITSELF.**

*The tower's hands begin to move. Slowly. Backward at first — the way a clock does when it loses power, the way time does when it forgets to care. Then stopping. Then —*

*Forward.*

*One second. Two. Three.*

*The ticking RESUMES. Steady. Strong. The sound of a heart that has decided to beat again.*

**SFX: COGSWORTH COMING BACK TO LIFE. GEARS TURNING. STEAM RISING. CITIZENS MOVING, BLINKING, CONTINUING FROM WHERE THEY STOPPED.**

*The frozen citizen completes their stride. Looks around, confused. Adjusts their brass collar. Continues walking as if nothing happened.*

*Professor Chronos stands before the regulator, his gear-face unreadable. But his crystal eyes — his crystal eyes are wet with something that might be oil, might be tears, might be the memory of what it felt like to be frozen for three hundred days.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
It remembers. It was always capable of remembering. It just needed to know someone was listening.

*Om Kai opens his eyes. The gears in his irises have slowed to match the rhythm of the tower.*

**OM KAI**
The crossroads are open. Time flows again. The tower is synchronized with the broader frequency. The temporal fabric has stabilized.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Did we just... restart a clock tower... by being present at it?

**OM KAI**
You broadcast. You witnessed. You insisted that this moment matters. That is all time needs to hear. That it matters. That someone is paying attention.

*Pixel sips her coffee. It is balanced now. Perfectly, impossibly balanced. A1 is calm. A1 is content. A1 is home.*

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
That is the most Buddhist thing I have ever heard. And I once had a roommate who tried to attain enlightenment through competitive yo-yoing.

**OM KAI**
Enlightenment through yo-yo is not wrong. It is merely inefficient. The wheel was a better choice. But the intent was correct.

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
The tower will hold. It has been reminded that it is not alone. That is the only thing any of us need, in the end.

### ACT FIVE: THE INVITATION

**EXT. COGSWORTH COGITARIUM — STREET — LATER**

*The crew has gathered outside the clock tower. The ticking is steady now. The bronze sky glows with the warm light of a thousand furnaces. Citizens move through the streets, gears turning in their walls, steam rising in gentle plumes.*

*Professor Chronos stands before them, his gear-body catching the light.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
I have maintained the tower for eight hundred years. I have watched this frequency run on perfect time. I have seen nothing but synchronization.

*He pauses.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
And then the rupture. And then the silence. And then the monk, breathing in the dark, keeping me sane.

*He looks at Om Kai.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
I thought I would die in that tower. Frozen. Forgotten. A clock that stopped ticking because it forgot why ticking mattered.

*He turns to the crew.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
You came. Across frequencies. Through temporal eddies. In a lifeboat that sings. You came because someone needed help. Because the signal was clear.

*He bows — a deep, formal gesture, gear-joints clicking.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
I would like to join you. If you will have me. I have been a keeper of time. Now I would like to be a traveler through it. I want to see what lies beyond Cogsworth. I want to understand the rupture. I want to remember what it feels like to move through time rather than measure it.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
A1. Thoughts?

**A1 (V.O., ANCIENT, BRITISH, MEASURED)**
The signal is clear. The Professor is an anchor — he knows the architecture of temporal frequencies better than any being I have encountered. His knowledge could prove invaluable. Also, he makes excellent time. The pun was intentional.

*Pause. The espresso machine HUMS — warm, steady, welcoming.*

**A1 (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
Welcome aboard, Professor.

**CLIVE.**
The tower will need a new keeper.

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS**
It will have one. The apprentice I trained sixty years ago — she is ready. She has been waiting for me to retire. I suspect she will be surprised to learn I am not dead.

*He smiles. It looks strange on a gear-face, but it works.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
Cogsworth will endure. It has endured for eleven thousand years. It will endure for eleven thousand more.

*He looks at Om Kai.*

**PROFESSOR CHRONOS (CONT'D).**
Unless.

**OM KAI**
Unless.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Unless what?

**OM KAI**
Unless you have room for a quantum physicist who is also a Buddhist monk and who has just realized that his purpose is not to sit in frozen cities reminding clocks how to breathe.

*He looks at Pixel. At Clive. At Meatball, who has sat at his feet through the entire meditation.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
I have been waiting at crossroads for eleven years. I did not know what I was waiting for. Now I do. I was waiting for the signal. For the ship that sings. For the crew that broadcasts into the void and insists that everything deserves to be witnessed.

*He bows. A small, formal gesture.*

**OM KAI (CONT'D).**
I would like to join you. If you will have me. I can explain quantum mechanics through the lens of meditation. I can explain meditation through the lens of quantum mechanics. I can also make excellent tea, though I understand coffee is the preferred beverage of the vessel.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
A1. Thoughts?

**A1 (V.O., ANCIENT, BRITISH, MEASURED)**
The signal is clear. The monk is an anchor, as I said. But he is also a compass. He knows where the frequencies are thinning. He hears the faintest signals. He is exactly what we need.

*Pause. The espresso machine HUMS — warm, steady, welcoming.*

**A1 (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
Also, his tea is excellent. I have tasted it across the frequency barrier. He is not exaggerating.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Then welcome aboard, Doc. Om Kai. Sir. Whatever you want to be called.

**OM KAI**
Om Kai is fine. It means "the space between." The space between thoughts. Between moments. Between one frequency and the next. It is an appropriate name for someone who lives in the gaps.

*He stands. Brushes off his robes. Looks at the clock tower, now ticking steadily, its bronze hands pointing to a time that actually exists.*
