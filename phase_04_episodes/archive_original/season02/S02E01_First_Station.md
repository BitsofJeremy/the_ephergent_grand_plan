# First Station

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

We thought the hardest part would be getting here. Turns out, the hard part is knowing what to do when you arrive. Station 3-Resonant is less a place and more a memory someone left out in the cold. The hull hums like it’s holding its breath. Zephyr’s hands are steady on the controls, but I can feel the tension in the air—like the whole crew is waiting for something to break, or maybe to begin. We find Barry’s notebook tucked behind a failed servomotor, his handwriting as precise as ever. Clive touches the page, and for a moment, the lights along his arm flicker in a language only he understands. Mochi presses against my boot, warm and silent. The Frequency is everywhere here, humming in the walls, in the air, in the spaces between us. I don’t know what we’re supposed to find, but I know this: nothing is ever just a coincidence out here.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The hull of the Ephergent vibrates with a low, expectant hum as the crew approaches Station 3-Resonant. Outside, stars scatter across velvet black, the station itself looming—ribs and glass suspended in the void, half-remembered and half-forgotten. Zephyr pilots with careful, deliberate movements, eyes flicking between readouts and the growing silhouette ahead. Clive stands at the viewport, his bronze alloy frame reflecting the station’s faint glow, sphere head pulsing with a soft, unreadable light. Pixel watches, notebook in hand, trying to capture the feeling of arrival when you’re not sure what you’re arriving to.

A1, stationed at the rear, brews coffee—today’s flavor is burnt cardamom, sharp and restless, matching the mood. Mochi weaves between boots, tail thumping once against a crate before settling by Clive’s leg, radiating warmth that grounds the moment.

### Station Arrival

The landing is smooth but tense. The hangar smells of machine oil and old coffee, the air thick with static. The crew steps out, boots echoing on metal grates. Panels along the walls ripple with static maps, and a terminal blinks with Barry’s annotations—dates, coordinates, fragments of thought. Clive finds the notebook, its pages filled with methodical entries and one phrase underlined: “keep the hum low; it remembers faces.”

When Clive touches the page, light patterns flicker along his forearm—robotic, gentle, private. For a heartbeat, memory floods in: the sound of tools, the geometry of a workbench, the sense of something small and alive in his hand. He can’t put words to it yet, but the feeling lingers, anchored by Mochi’s silent presence.

### Discovery and Resonance

The station’s archive is in decay, but the Frequency is strong—pulsing through terminals, humming in the hangar, threading through every interaction. Barry’s notebook offers breadcrumbs: coordinates, observations, a lattice of clues. The crew splits up, each following a thread—Zephyr tracing resonance data, Pixel cataloguing anomalies, A1 scanning for environmental hazards. Clive lingers by the archive, lost in thought, while Mochi presses close, tail thumping in time with the station’s hum.

### Closing Beat

As the crew regroups, the sense of something awakening lingers. The Frequency grows clearer, the mysteries deeper. Pixel files a dispatch: “First Station. Barry’s trail is real. Clive remembers something. The Frequency is everywhere. We are not alone.”
