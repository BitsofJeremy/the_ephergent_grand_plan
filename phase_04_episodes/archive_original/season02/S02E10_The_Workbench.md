# The Workbench

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

The workbench smelled of resin and oil. Tools hung in careful disorder. Clive slid his fingers across a bench scar, memory unspooling: a small creature’s heartbeat, the click of a winding key, a child’s laugh. Mochi sat in a bowl of cloth, bumping its head against Clive’s palm. The memory answered with a light-pattern pulse, matching the rhythm of a small gear. A1 brewed weak coffee—brown sugar notes, late nights and solder. Barry’s torn page was pinned to the bench: “foundations inward—follow maker signatures.” Coordinates, maker marks, tool serials—a breadcrumb trail disguised as inventory. We catalogued tools, tightening the map. Clive’s memory left him with the shape of a workshop and the knowledge of being both maker and keeper.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The crew enters the Workshop Node, the air thick with resin and oil. Tools hang in careful disorder. Clive runs his fingers across a bench scar, memory stirring.

Mochi sits in a shallow bowl of cloth, content and small. It bumps its head against Clive’s palm, triggering a light-pattern pulse. A1 pours weak coffee, brown sugar notes evoking late nights and solder.

### Discovery

Barry’s torn page, pinned to the bench, is a route fragment: “foundations inward—follow maker signatures.” The handwriting is tidy: coordinates, maker marks, tool serials. The crew catalogs the tools, cross-referencing with terminals. Each match tightens the map.

### Memory and Identity

Clive’s memory does not resolve everything, but leaves him with the shape of a workshop and the knowledge of being both maker and keeper.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “We follow maker signatures. Barry’s trail is inventory. Clive remembers the shape of making.”
