# Pixel & The Quiet Argument

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

Arguments in cramped quarters are quiet and sharp. I kept my fingers on the terminal, Clive answered in measured blinks and soft robotics. Our contention was almost polite—dropped phrases, light-patterns. Mochi pressed into the gap, tail ticking like a clock. A1 handed out deep almond-roast coffee, smoothing shoulders for an instant. The crew’s vote was procedural, a tally that made the decision official. I argued for a new route; Clive wanted to verify resonance. Barry’s note—'Follow signal when stable; keep buffer when uncertain'—carried weight. In the end, we followed Barry’s heading with a buffer. Trust, fragile as a tool, was rebuilt in shared procedure.
