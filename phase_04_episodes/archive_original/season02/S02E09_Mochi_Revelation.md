# Mochi Revelation

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

The archive smelled of dust and old glue. A sealed crate waited beneath a catalog shelf. When I released the latch, a folded chart slid out—Builder lines naming companion designations: C-1, C-2, C-3. Next to C-1, a faded schematic showed a small creature’s nervous architecture. Mochi watched from the console, eyes slow and patient, then climbed onto Clive’s knee, tail thumping twice—punctuation. A1’s coffee was nut-smoky, softening surprise. Barry’s note was terse: “Companion: maintenance-first. Treat as atlas stepper; small thermal pulses enable recall.” Not prophecy, but field entry. We realized Mochi was more than companion—a living datum in the system. Clive pressed his palm to the schematic, light-pattern matching Mochi’s tail rhythm. The revelation changed the stakes, but not Mochi. Unchanged, Mochi licked a thumb and settled back.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The crew enters a Builder archive, the air thick with dust and old glue. A sealed crate sits beneath a catalog shelf, waiting to be opened. Pixel releases the latch, revealing a folded chart with Builder designations: C-1, C-2, C-3. Next to C-1, a schematic of a small creature’s nervous system.

Mochi watches from the console, then climbs onto Clive’s knee, tail thumping twice. A1 pours nut-smoky coffee, the flavor softening the moment’s surprise.

### Discovery

Barry’s note, tucked with the schematic, reads: “Companion: maintenance-first. Treat as atlas stepper; small thermal pulses enable recall.” The note is clinical, not dramatic. The crew understands that Mochi may be a living datum in the wider system.

### Recognition

Clive presses his palm to the schematic, and the light-pattern along his sleeve matches Mochi’s tail rhythm. The recognition is mutual, not commanding. The revelation shifts the crew’s understanding, but Mochi remains unchanged.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “Mochi is more than companion. Barry’s notes reveal the atlas stepper. The Frequency remembers in living data.”
