# The Builders Were People

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

The broadcast opened like a slow letter. Builder fragments—records of building, small gestures—played across speakers. Not manifestos, but notes: how to tend a field, mend a membrane, keep a living thing warm. Mochi watched, tail thumping a small drumbeat. A1’s coffee tasted of boiled sugar—comforting, simple. The crew took turns translating, reshaping the Builders’ words. Barry annotated with diagrams. The tone was practical, tender, matter-of-fact—the Builders were people with small needs and big patience.
