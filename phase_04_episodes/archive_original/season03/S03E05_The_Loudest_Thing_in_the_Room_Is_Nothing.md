# The Loudest Thing in the Room Is Nothing

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

We entered a silence zone like stepping into a held breath. Instruments went mute; we moved in small, measured gestures. The absence of sound sharpened attention. Mochi curled against Zephyr’s ankle, tail thumping a patient two-beat rhythm. A1’s coffee tasted of wet earth, grounding us. Barry’s logs were practical: silence-onset timestamps, repair margins, parts lists. Decisions were transactional—choose which frequencies to preserve, which to let go. Om Kai framed the question: who but the builders will choose? We voted, and in the quiet, our choices took on weight.
