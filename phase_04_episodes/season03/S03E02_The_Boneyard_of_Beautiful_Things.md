# The Boneyard of Beautiful Things

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

We found a graveyard made of broken songs. Antennae and sound-plates piled like bones, each one holding a tone if you knew how to ask. Barry’s annotations filled a salvage ledger—measurements, dates, a quiet grief written in numbers. Mochi nosed a bell and made it sing; we listened, learning the language of loss. A1 brewed a vegetal, slow coffee that pulled the edges of sorrow into focus. Clive touched a plate and memory sparked: a hand tuning a note, a face turned toward repair. Out here, elegy and salvage are the same thing.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The Ephergent sets down in a field of silent relics, the Frequency Graveyard stretching to the horizon. The crew steps out, boots crunching on debris that once sang. Pixel surveys the landscape—a library of broken songs, antennae and sound-plates scattered like bones.

Mochi noses a small bell, coaxing a single, clean note from it. The crew pauses, listening as if learning a new language. A1 pours coffee—vegetal and slow, the flavor grounding the moment in quiet reflection. Barry’s salvage ledger is found among the wreckage, each entry a record of mourning by measurement.

### Discovery and Memory

Clive lingers by a fractured plate, his hand tracing Builder signatures. Memory sparks: tuning a note, a face intent on repair. The Frequency here is fragmented, but not lost—usable fragments remain, waiting to be heard.

### Salvage and Elegy

The crew balances elegy with practical salvage, gathering what can be saved and honoring what cannot. Pixel notes the resonance in the ruins, the way loss and hope intermingle in every tone.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “The graveyard sings in fragments. Barry mourned by measuring. Clive remembers repair. We salvage meaning from the ruins.”
