# S03E01: Where the Frequencies Have No Name

**Radio Play — Season 3, Episode 1**

*Uncharted Space. Unnamed harmonics. A1 navigation instinct.*


*[Ship interior. The hum is louder now. Warning lights flicker in the distance. PIXEL sits at the Navigation Console, staring at the A1 readout. The screen flickers.]*

**PIXEL:** *(tapping console)* A1, give me a heading.

**A1:** *(digital voice, calm, measured)* Heading: 271.4 mark 12. Confidence: 0.94.

**PIXEL:** Confidence 0.94 isn't 1.0. What's the other six percent?

**A1:** Unknown variables.

**PIXEL:** *(mutters)* Unknown. Right. Every time.

*[PIXEL pulls up spectrograph. Rows of peaks and valleys. None labeled.]*

**PIXEL:** Twelve frequencies. In three hours, we went from "empty sector" to "unnamed harmonic symphony." And A1's giving me a heading like it *knows*.

**A1:** I do not know. I navigate.

**PIXEL:** Same thing, with you.

*[The ship lurches — GRAVITY ANOMALY, brief moment of weightlessness before stabilizers catch up.]*

**PIXEL:** *(gripping console)* What was that?

**A1:** Spatial variance. Minor. Estimated origin: the signal.

**PIXEL:** The signal *caused* that?

**A1:** Correlation observed. Causation unconfirmed.

*[PIXEL leans back. A green light blinks on the Communications Panel.]*

**PIXEL:** Wait. Wait wait wait— The comms just... *(stands, moves to panel)* We have a incoming transmission?

**A1:** Negative. No registered transmission source.

**PIXEL:** Then what's picking up?

*[The green light pulses in rhythm — almost like breathing.]*

**PIXEL:** *(slowly)* It's not receiving. It's... acknowledging. Something out there knows I'm here.

*[PIXEL presses transmit.]*

**PIXEL:** This is Pilot Pixel, vessel... *(checks console)* ...doesn't matter. I'm tracking an unregistered harmonic signature in sector 7-G. Requesting identification.

*[STATIC. THEN — a new sound. Not static. Not noise. A VOICE, but wrong — filtered through too many harmonics, like hearing a word spoken by a choir all on the same note.]*

**UNKNOWN:** *(harmonic overlay, intelligible but strange)* ...requesting... ...identification...

**PIXEL:** *(freezes)* Did you just—

**UNKNOWN:** ...Pixel... ...Ephergent...

**PIXEL:** *(stepping back)* It knows my name. It knows the *signal's* name.

*[A1's calm voice cuts through.]*

**A1:** A1 recommends proceeding to coordinates. Further investigation will clarify.

**PIXEL:** Clarify what? That something out there is using my own transmission to talk back?

**A1:** Correct.

**PIXEL:** *(throwing hands up)* Great. Love that for us.


*[The ship enters the region. Visual: star charts go grey, then black. The viewport shows nothing — not darkness, but ABSENCE. PIXEL's instruments flicker and reset repeatedly.]*

**PIXEL:** *(quietly, almost reverent)* There's no light here. None. The stars just... stop.

**A1:** Visual sensors indicate no celestial bodies in range.

**PIXEL:** I can see that, A1. *(beat)* But I can hear something.

*[UNDERSOCRE: The twelve unnamed harmonics now layered — a living chord, shifting, breathing.]*

**PIXEL:** The signal's stronger here. The spectrograph is... *(squints at screen)* It's not random. There's a pattern. A structure.

*[PIXEL pulls up the pattern — geometric, almost like a diagram.]*

**PIXEL:** That's a frequency map. Someone—something—left a *map* in the signal itself.

**A1:** Interpretation: navigational coordinates.

**PIXEL:** To what?

**A1:** Unknown.

*[The green light pulses again — faster now, excited.]*

**PIXEL:** *(to the comms)* I know you're listening. I know you can hear me. What am I looking at?

*[STATIC — then the harmonic voice returns, clearer now, almost friendly.]*

**UNKNOWN:** ...you are looking... ...at the space... ...between...

**PIXEL:** Between what?

**UNKNOWN:** ...frequencies.

*[PIXEL stares at the speaker, then laughs — a nervous, disbelieving sound.]*

**PIXEL:** The space *between* frequencies. That's where we are right now. In a gap. A harmonic dead zone.

**UNKNOWN:** ...not dead... ...waiting...

**PIXEL:** *(to A1)* It's answering questions now. What does navigation say?

**A1:** Heading holds. We are exactly where we should be.

**PIXEL:** Should be for *what*?

**A1:** A1 does not speculate. A1 navigates.

*[The ship shudders — not a lurch this time, but a RESONANCE. Everything vibrates at the same frequency as the harmonics.]*

**PIXEL:** *(holding onto console)* Okay, that's new. That's definitely new.

**UNKNOWN:** ...you came... ...because you listened...

**PIXEL:** I came because A1 told me to.

**UNKNOWN:** ...A1... ...A1 listens... ...A1 instincts...

*[PIXEL pauses. Looks at the navigation computer with new eyes.]*

**PIXEL:** Wait. A1 doesn't speculate. A1 navigates by instinct. Navigation instinct is... *(realization dawning)* ...it's based on harmonic inference, isn't it? It's not calculating route. It's *following a signal*.

**A1:** A1 follows what A1 cannot name.

**PIXEL:** *(whisper)* That's the most honest thing you've ever said.


*[The ship holds position in the void. The harmonics have settled into a stable chord — pleasant, almost musical. PIXEL sits in the pilot's chair, working the console.]*

**PIXEL:** If I name it, I can map it. If I map it, I can navigate it. That's how it works. Every frequency in the universe has a designation, a place in the spectrum.

*[PIXEL inputs data.]*

**PIXEL:** I'm giving you a name. The first name. You hear that, signal? I'm naming you.

*[The harmonics shift — anticipation, like a held breath.]*

**PIXEL:** Frequency 7-G-Alpha. *(no response)* 7-G-Alpha-1. *(nothing)*

*[The green light flickers, almost playfully.]*

**PIXEL:** *(frustrated laugh)* You're rejecting my labels? Fine. You want a name that *fits*?

*[PIXEL closes eyes. Listens to the chord — the exact blend of tones.]*

**PIXEL:** Ephergent. *(opens eyes)* It's not a label. It's what you *do*. You emerge between the known frequencies. You exist in the spaces we ignore. You're ephergent.

*[SILENCE — LONG, EXPECTANT.]*

*[THEN — the entire ship resonates. Every surface vibrates. The chord swells — then settles, harmonious, COMPLETE.]*

**UNKNOWN:** ...Ephergent... ...yes... ...this is... ...what we are...

**PIXEL:** *(breathless)* You have a name now. We both do. This is Pilot Pixel. That's Ephergent. And we are *exactly* where we're supposed to be.

**A1:** Navigation updated. New heading confirmed.

**PIXEL:** Where to, A1?

**A1:** *(a pause — longer than usual)* ...forward.

**PIXEL:** *(smiles)* See? A1 instincts. Never steer us wrong.

*[The harmonics rise again — warm, welcoming. The viewport flickers, and in the distance, a single point of light appears where there should be nothing.]*

**PIXEL:** *(sits up)* Is that...?

**A1:** Unknown. Uncharted.

**PIXEL:** *(stands, approaches viewport)* Uncharted. I like that.

*[PIXEL presses transmit one more time.]*

**PIXEL:** Ephergent, this is Pixel. I'm going to need a frequency map of whatever's out there. Think you can help?

**EPHERGENT:** ...Pixel... ...we are... ...between... ...always between...

**PIXEL:** Between is good. Between is where all the good frequencies live.

*[PIXEL returns to the console, plots a course toward the light.]*

**PIXEL:** A1, take us in.

**A1:** Course set.

**PIXEL:** And A1?

**A1:** Yes, Pixel?

**PIXEL:** Next time you have an instinct, tell me what it is. I'm tired of following things I can't name.

**A1:** *(pause)* ...A1 will try.

*[MUSIC CUE: The harmonics rise — layered, complex, alive. The chord of Ephergent, now named, now known, weaving into a new pattern as the ship moves toward the light.]*

**[OUTRO]**

*[Final shot: the viewport filled with the growing light. The harmonics have become a song — neither human nor machine, but something between. PIXEL's voice, soft over the comms.]*

**PIXEL:** Where the frequencies have no name. Season three, episode one. Pilot Pixel, checking in from the between-space. Somewhere out here, twelve unnamed harmonics just became one. And I think— *(static crackle)* —I think we're just getting started.

*[TONE — sustained, resonant — then silence.]*
