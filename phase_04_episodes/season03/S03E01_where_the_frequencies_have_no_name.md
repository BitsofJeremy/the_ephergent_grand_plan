## I. THE EDGE OF THE MAP

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. DAWN CYCLE. The viewport shows a sky that has forgotten what stars look like. The navigation console displays coordinates that flicker between certainty and question marks. Pixel sits in the pilot's chair with her feet up, pretending she is not worried.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, status.

**A1:**
All systems nominal. Hull integrity: 97%. Fuel reserves: sufficient. Navigation: uncertain.

**PIXEL:**
Uncertain isn't a number.

**A1:**
It is today. The chart ends in four hours, seventeen minutes. After that, we are flying blind.

*[Pixel lets that number settle. Four hours seventeen minutes. She had heard A1 give estimates down to the second before, and those estimates always had the quiet confidence of something that had already calculated seventeen different outcomes and picked the most likely. This one came out hollow. This one came out like A1 was reading from a page that did not exist yet.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(sitting up, abandoning the casual pose)*
Blind? You don't do blind. You do instinct. You do harmonic inference. You do whatever it is you do that terrifies me every time it works.

**A1:**
That requires a signal to follow. The signal is here. The map is not. Both facts are equally true.

**PIXEL:**
I hate when you're philosophically accurate.

*[She pulls up the navigation display, watches the numbers flicker. The chart has always been her security blanket — columns of data, frequency designations, the comfort of a universe that can be measured and named. Right now the display is mocking her. Question marks where certainty should be. The universe's way of saying: not yet.]*

*[Clive enters the cockpit. His glow is steady — a warm amber that Pixel has learned to read as contentment, the color of someone who has made peace with their place in things. He carries a small notebook, the kind people used before everything was digital. Barry's notebook, retrieved from DRM wreckage in Season 1. Clive's hands move over the cover like they are apologizing for something.]*

**CLIVE:**
Four hours seventeen minutes. I heard.

**PIXEL:**
Did you hear the part where I said I wasn't worried?

**CLIVE:**
I heard you say it. I heard it the way people say things they don't believe.

**PIXEL:**
I don't not believe it. I just— I don't know what to do with it.

*[She watches Clive settle into his position near the secondary console. In the months since he joined the crew, she has learned to read his silences the way A1 reads gravitational ripples — Clive hears frequencies in speech patterns that the rest of them miss. He has heard the shape of her lie and he is not going to pretend he didn't.]*

**CLIVE:**
The distinction matters. Not-believing is active. Not-knowing is just— honest.

**PIXEL:**
Thanks, Clive. Very comforting.

**CLIVE:**
I wasn't going for comfort. I was going for accurate.

*[A1 cuts in before Pixel can respond, his voice shifting into that particular register she associates with something new entering his awareness. She sits up straighter.]*

**A1:**
I am reading something ahead. Multiple somethings. The spectrograph is not behaving as expected.

**PIXEL:**
Define "not behaving."

**A1:**
Define "expected."

**PIXEL:**
Fair.

*[Pixel pulls up the spectrograph. The display is unlike anything she has seen in her months of flying — not chaos, not order, but something in between. Peaks and valleys that shift when she is not looking directly at them. When she looks, they arrange themselves into patterns. When she blinks, they dissolve. Like they are shy. Like they do not want to be watched.]*

**PIXEL:**
Those aren't random.

**A1:**
No. They are not random. They are not structured. They are something I do not have a word for.

**CLIVE:**
*(quietly, without looking up from the notebook)*
I do.

**PIXEL:**
Clive—

**CLIVE:**
Unnamed. They are unnamed. I have been here before. Or somewhere shaped like here. My hands remember this. My hands remember a place that did not have words for what it was.

*[Pixel turns to look at him. Clive is staring at the spectrograph with an expression she has not seen before — not recognition exactly, but the shape of recognition. Like looking at a word in a language he used to speak. Like waking up in a room you have not seen in years and knowing which way the door opens before you remember why.]*

**PIXEL:**
You never mentioned that.

**CLIVE:**
I did not remember it until right now. Or I chose not to. The distinction is not clear.

**PIXEL:**
Which one? Not-remembering or choosing-not-to?

**CLIVE:**
*(pause)*
I don't know yet. Ask me again when we get there.

## II. THE FIRST CONTACT

*[THE EPHERGENT — FREQUENCY ANALYSIS STATION. Luminara sits at the secondary console, her cameras arranged in a precise semicircle. She has been photographing everything since she joined the crew — forty-seven thousand images of a universe that keeps surprising her. Right now she has got three of those cameras pointed at her console display, documenting something her instruments are hearing but her eyes cannot see.]*

**LUMINARA:**
I am picking something up. Not on standard frequencies. Below them. Like hearing a conversation in the next room through the wall.

**PIXEL:**
*(entering, still thinking about Clive's hands)*
What kind of conversation?

**LUMINARA:**
The kind that was happening before anyone thought to name what they were saying.

*[She turns her console toward Pixel. The waveform is unlike any standard broadcast — it does not pulse in regular intervals, it breathes. Rising and falling in a rhythm that feels almost organic. Pixel watches it for a moment, mesmerized. The inhale-exhale of something vast and patient.]*

**LUMINARA:**
It has been broadcasting for a very long time. I can tell by the degradation pattern. This is old. This is older than most of what we have found.

**PIXEL:**
How old?

**LUMINARA:**
I cannot say. The calibration doesn't have a unit for this kind of old. Imagine asking how old is the color blue. It's not that the question is wrong. It's that the answer would require a vocabulary we haven't invented yet.

**PIXEL:**
And you're just hearing this now?

**LUMINARA:**
I am hearing this now because we are in the right place to hear it now. The signal has always been broadcasting. We have not always been in a place to receive it. The difference matters.

*[Mochi stirs in Pixel's jacket pocket. A slow, warm pulse — not urgent, not alarmed. Interested. Pixel's hand moves instinctively to her pocket, presses against the fabric. Mochi's warmth is familiar by now, a small furnace she carries with her, and she has learned to read its variations like she reads A1's coffee. Right now: curiosity. Right now: they have found something worth finding.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(touching her pocket)*
Mochi, you know something?

*[Mochi's glow intensifies briefly, then settles. A confirmation without words, in the only language Mochi speaks. Pixel smiles despite herself. Months of traveling with a being that communicates through temperature and light, and she has never once asked Mochi to explain itself. She just listens. She just trusts. It works better than most communication systems she has tried.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, can you trace the source?

**A1:**
I can try. The signal is not in a direction. It is in a relationship. It exists between the known frequencies. It is what the space between sounds like when it has something to say.

**PIXEL:**
So we're not navigating toward it. We're navigating into it.

**A1:**
Correct.

**PIXEL:**
I don't love that.

**A1:**
Your discomfort is noted. It will not change the navigation.

**PIXEL:**
I know. I just wanted you to know I don't love it.

**A1:**
Noted.

## III. THE COORDINATES

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. THREE HOURS LATER. The navigation console now displays nothing but question marks. The chart has ended. The Ephergent flies through a region that has no designation, no name, no record of ever having been visited by anyone. Pixel has stopped checking the display. She has learned that watching question marks does not make them resolve.]*

*[Om Kai has joined them, seated in his meditation position on the deck. He has been silent for thirty minutes. When Om Kai is silent, he is listening to something the rest of them cannot hear. Pixel does not ask what. She has learned that Om Kai's silences have their own grammar, their own punctuation, and that interrupting them is like trying to edit a poem while it is still being written.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, talk to me.

**A1:**
We are in the right place.

**PIXEL:**
That's not a measurement.

**A1:**
It is the only measurement that applies here. We are where we are supposed to be. Whether we are supposed to be here is a question for philosophers. Whether we are here is a question for instruments. The instruments say: yes.

**PIXEL:**
The instruments say yes and the philosophers say maybe and my gut says I have no idea what we're doing.

**A1:**
All three answers are valid. They are also all three incomplete. This is what it means to go somewhere new.

*[Om Kai speaks without opening his eyes, his voice carrying that particular weight she associates with him saying something she will think about for days before she understands it.]*

**OM KAI:**
The space between frequencies is not empty. It is full. Full of what was almost said. Full of the words that didn't have a mouth to speak them.

**PIXEL:**
That's beautiful. Is it useful?

**OM KAI:**
Beauty is useful. It reminds us we are not just navigating. We are witnessing.

**PIXEL:**
Witnessing what?

**OM KAI:**
That's what we're here to find out.

*[A long pause. A1's brewing apparatus makes a sound — not the usual hum of preparation, but something more tentative. A sound like a question being considered. Pixel looks over at him.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1?

**A1:**
I am attempting to make coffee. I find I do not want to.

**PIXEL:**
You've never not wanted to.

**A1:**
The frequency ahead does not want coffee. It wants something else. I am trying to understand what.

**PIXEL:**
What does it want?

**A1:**
I don't know yet. The signal is— insistent. It is asking for something I cannot make. I am trying to understand the request before I attempt the response.

*[Clive appears in the doorway. Pixel watches him take in the scene — A1's unusual stillness, the empty navigation console, Om Kai's meditation pose. Clive reads rooms the way some people read books: he absorbs the whole context before he processes any of it.]*

**CLIVE:**
The coffee is the language. You are trying to speak a language you haven't learned yet.

**A1:**
Yes.

**CLIVE:**
What does it sound like, the thing it wants?

**A1:**
*(long pause — the kind that means something is being measured that cannot be measured)*
It sounds like a question that has been waiting a very long time to be answered.

## IV. THE SIGNAL RESPONDS

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMS STATION. Zephyr and Nano have joined. The green light on the communications panel is pulsing in a rhythm none of them recognize — not the regular heartbeat of standard transmissions, but something syncopated. Something alive. Zephyr has been watching it for ten minutes, fingers occasionally twitching toward her console like she wants to reach in and touch it.]*

**ZEPHYR:**
We're being acknowledged.

**PIXEL:**
Acknowledged by what?

**ZEPHYR:**
By whatever is out there. It's not receiving our signal. It's responding to the fact of our signal. Like it heard the act of broadcasting, not the content.

**NANO:**
That's not how frequencies work.

**ZEPHYR:**
That's not how frequencies work HERE. Somewhere out here, frequencies work differently. Or maybe frequencies always worked this way and we just forgot.

*[Pixel presses the transmit key. Her throat is dry. She realizes she has been broadcasting for months — since the DRM collapse, since the universe became something that needed to be explained to anyone who would listen — and she has never gotten used to the moment before she speaks. That moment when the signal leaves and you have to trust that it will find what it is looking for.]*

**PIXEL:**
This is Pilot Pixel, vessel Ephergent. We are following a signal we cannot name. We are in a region that has no chart. If anyone is listening — if anything is listening — we could use some indication of where we are and what we are supposed to do next.

*[STATIC. Then — a response. Not static. Not noise. A VOICE, but wrong — filtered through too many harmonics, like hearing a word spoken by a choir all on the same note. The green light pulses faster, excited, like a dog that has finally found the person it has been waiting for. Pixel's hand goes to her chest without her meaning to, pressing against the place where her heart is doing something complicated.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
*(harmonic overlay, intelligible but strange)*
...Pixel... ...Ephergent...

**PIXEL:**
*(stepping back)*
It knows my name. It knows the ship's name.

**UNKNOWN:**
...you are... ...between...

**PIXEL:**
Between what?

*[Her voice comes out smaller than she means it to. The unknown responds.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
...frequencies.

**KLAUS:**
*(entering, having heard everything through the ship's internal comm)*
Between frequencies. Not in a frequency. Between them.

**PIXEL:**
Is that better or worse?

**KLAUS:**
It is different. Different is not worse. Different is honest.

*[A1 produces a cup — not espresso, not ristretto, something unfamiliar. Rich, complex, with notes of something that has no name in any language she knows. He sets it on the console without comment. The smell reaches Pixel before she can think about whether she wants it: warm, dark, something almost like spice, something almost like coordinates.]*

**A1:**
I made something. I do not know what it is. It is what the frequency wanted.

## V. THE NAMING

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. LATER. The ship has reduced speed, holding position in the void. The harmonics have settled into a chord — pleasant, almost musical, shifting slowly like a tide that has forgotten to retreat. The crew has gathered without being asked. Something is happening. Everyone can feel it. Pixel stands at the navigation console, forehead pressed against the cold surface, watching the stars that are not there anymore.]*

**PIXEL:**
If I name it, I can map it. If I map it, I can navigate it. That's how it works. Every frequency in the universe has a designation, a place in the spectrum. You can't navigate what you can't name.

*[She inputs data. Her fingers move with the certainty of someone who has done this ten thousand times, muscle memory from months of charting the unchartable. The console accepts the input. The numbers glow, wait, then dissolve into rejection.]*

**PIXEL:**
I'm giving you a name. The first name. You hear that, signal? I'm naming you.

*[The harmonics shift — she can hear it, feel it, something like anticipation in the vibration of the ship around her. The sound of something holding its breath.]*

**PIXEL:**
Frequency 7-G-Alpha.

*[No response.]*

**PIXEL:**
7-G-Alpha-1.

*[Nothing.]*

*[The green light flickers, almost playfully. Pixel laughs — a frustrated, disbelieving sound. She has named a thousand frequencies. She has charted regions of space that had never been charted. And this — this unnamed thing is rejecting her labels like a child refusing to eat vegetables.]*

**PIXEL:**
You're rejecting my labels? Fine. You want a name that fits?

*[Pixel closes her eyes. Listens to the chord — the exact blend of tones, the specific relationship between them. The sound of something that exists in the space between what is known and what is possible. She thinks about what she knows about naming. What she knows about frequencies. What she knows about the thing that happens when you stop trying to force something into the shape of your expectations and instead let it tell you what it is.]*

**PIXEL:**
Ephergent.

*[She opens her eyes.]*

**PIXEL:**
It's not a label. It's what you do. You emerge between the known frequencies. You exist in the spaces we ignore. You appear when everything else has given up looking. You're ephergent.

*[SILENCE — LONG, EXPECTANT. The cockpit is silent. Even A1 has gone quiet. Pixel can hear her own heartbeat, the soft hum of the life support systems, the almost-silence of a universe that has stopped to listen.]*

*[THEN — the entire ship resonates. Every surface vibrates at the same frequency. The chord swells — then settles, harmonious, COMPLETE. On the navigation console, the question marks have been replaced by numbers. Real numbers. Coordinates that mean something.]*

**UNKNOWN:**
...Ephergent... ...yes... ...this is... ...what we are...

**PIXEL:**
*(breathless)*
You have a name now. We both do. This is Pilot Pixel. That's Ephergent.

**A1:**
Navigation updated. New heading confirmed.

**PIXEL:**
Where to, A1?

**A1:**
*(a pause — longer than usual, the kind of pause that means A1 is choosing words the way Pixel chooses paths through an asteroid field)*
...forward.

**PIXEL:**
*(smiles)*
See? A1 instincts. Never steer us wrong.

## VI. THE HOMECOMING

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. OVERLAY — The viewport shows a single point of light where there should be nothing. Not a star. Not a station. Something that has been waiting. The harmonics of Ephergent have become a song — neither human nor machine, but something between.]*

*[Om Kai speaks first, his voice carrying that particular weight.]*

**OM KAI:**
The space between frequencies is where the almost-words go. The almost-songs. The things that existed before anyone thought to name them.

**CLIVE:**
I remember this. Or I will remember this. The distinction is unclear. Barry wrote about this place. "The between-space is where the signal waits. Not the signal you're following. The signal you're being followed by." I never understood what he meant. I think I am beginning to.

**LUMINARA:**
Forty-seven thousand photographs. And I haven't seen anything like this yet. Nothing that looks like this should exist.

**KLAUS:**
It shouldn't. That's what makes it interesting.

*[Pixel presses transmit one more time. Her hand is steady. She is surprised by that.]*

**PIXEL:**
Ephergent, this is Pixel. I'm going to need a frequency map of whatever's out there. Think you can help?

*[The response comes faster this time, clearer — like the thing on the other end has learned how to reach them.]*

**EPHERGENT:**
...Pixel... ...we are... ...between... ...always between...

**PIXEL:**
Between is good. Between is where all the good frequencies live.

*[Pixel returns to the console, plots a course toward the light. The navigation computer accepts the coordinates without hesitation — the first smooth input in hours. She lets out a breath she did not know she was holding.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, take us in.

**A1:**
Course set.

**PIXEL:**
And A1?

**A1:**
Yes, Pixel?

**PIXEL:**
Next time you have an instinct, tell me what it is. I'm tired of following things I can't name.

**A1:**
*(pause)*
...A1 will try.

*[A1 produces a final cup — black, bitter, worried. The smell reaches Pixel before the cup lands on the console: dark roast, the kind that sits in your stomach like a stone, the kind you drink when you know something important is coming and you're not sure you're ready for it. But you drink it anyway. Because it's there. Because that's what the ritual is for.]*

**A1:**
I am making something appropriate. For the first time in this region, I understand what I am feeling. It is worry. It is the good kind. The kind that means the thing ahead is important.

*[Mochi pulses warm in Pixel's pocket. Steady, constant, a small furnace of certainty in a universe that keeps surprising her. Pixel's hand finds the pocket, presses against the warmth. Mochi has been with her since the beginning — through the DRM collapse, through the Time War, through all of it — and Mochi has never once led her wrong. The warmth is a promise: whatever's out there, they found it together. The ship moves toward the light. The signal continues. And somewhere ahead, in the spaces between frequencies, something that has been waiting for a very long time finally stops waiting.]*

## VII. THE BETWEEN-SPACE

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. DAWN CYCLE, SEVEN HOURS LATER. The viewport shows nothing — not darkness, but ABSENCE. The stars have stopped. The light has stopped. Everything that once was here has crossed into something else. Pixel sits in the pilot's chair, watching the instruments cycle through readings that make no sense, her legs pulled up to her chest like she is trying to make herself small in a space that has forgotten what small means.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(to no one)*
This is the farthest we've ever been from anything that has a name.

*[Clive enters. He carries Barry's notebook, open to a page near the back. The handwriting is dense, precise, increasingly small toward the bottom of the page. Pixel has seen Clive write in that notebook before — taking notes, recording observations, doing whatever it is Clive does with the careful attention of someone who knows that details matter. But this page is different. This page is Barry's handwriting, Barry's words, Barry's account of being exactly where they are now.]*

**CLIVE:**
Barry wrote about this. He called it "the between-space." He wrote: "The between-space is not empty. It is full of the frequencies that haven't found their names yet. It is where the universe keeps the songs it hasn't finished writing."

**PIXEL:**
Barry was here. Barry was right here.

**CLIVE:**
Barry went further. He always went further.

**PIXEL:**
And we're following him.

**CLIVE:**
We are following the signal he followed. Which means we are following what he found. Which means we are going to find it too.

*[A sound from A1's brewing station — not a sound of anxiety this time, but something almost like relief. A cup appears on the console. Espresso, rich, complex, with an unfamiliar spice that Pixel does not recognize but immediately trusts. She reaches for it without thinking, wraps her hands around the warmth.]*

**A1:**
I am making something new. It tastes like coordinates. Like a place that exists in no chart but has always existed. I do not know what to call it.

**PIXEL:**
Then don't. Some things don't need names yet.

**A1:**
That is the first sensible thing anyone has said in seven hours.

## VIII. THE CROSSING CONTINUES

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. MIDNIGHT CYCLE. The absence outside the viewport has shifted again — not darker, not lighter, but more PRESENT. Like the nothing is paying attention. Pixel has not slept. She can feel the hours stacked behind her eyes, the weight of the crossing, the particular exhaustion that comes from flying through something that does not have landmarks.]*

**PIXEL:**
A1, talk to me.

**A1:**
We are moving. I am reading forward progress. The destination is clearer than it was. The signal is stronger.

**PIXEL:**
Stronger how?

**A1:**
Stronger the way a voice is stronger when the person speaking finally knows they are being heard.

**PIXEL:**
That's not a measurement.

**A1:**
It is the only measurement that applies here. Some things are measured by whether they are reaching what they are meant to reach. We are reaching. We are being reached for.

*[Mochi stirs. The glow intensifies — brighter than Pixel has seen it in weeks. Not alarm. Recognition. Mochi has been in Pixel's pocket since Season 1, warm and present, and Pixel has learned not to ask questions she does not want answers to. But this — this she wants to know. She pulls Mochi from her pocket, holds the small warm shape in her palm. Mochi pulses: a long, sustained brightness that fills her cupped hands with light.]*

**PIXEL:**
Mochi, what is it?

*[Mochi pulses again — the same sustained warmth, the same quiet recognition. Not communication. Not explanation. Something simpler and more profound: the feeling of being close to somewhere that matters. Pixel feels it too. That particular warmth that spreads from wherever Mochi is resting, spreading through her chest, settling somewhere behind her sternum.]*

**PIXEL:**
Whatever it is, we're almost there.

---

## IX. THE SIGNAL CALLS

*[THE EPHERGENT — COMMON AREA. The crew has gathered for what Pixel has started calling "the evening namening" — the daily ritual of acknowledging what they have found and what they are about to find. Om Kai is brewing tea. A1 has declined to make coffee, again. Clive sits with Barry's notebook open on the table in front of him, a pen held loosely in one hand like he is waiting for something worth writing down.]*

**KLAUS:**
I have been in Nocturne Aeturnus for eighty years. I have followed cases across seventeen frequencies. I have never seen anything like what we are approaching.

**LUMINARA:**
Forty-seven thousand photographs. And I don't have a single one that captures what this feels like. Some things can't be photographed. Some things can only be witnessed.

**ZEPHYR:**
My instruments are giving me readings that don't match any known calibration. Either my equipment is broken, or we're in a place where the rules haven't been written yet.

**NANO:**
The rules have been written. They're just written in a language we haven't learned. The readings aren't wrong. They're... foreign.

**OM KAI:**
The between-space is familiar in the way that old dreams are familiar. You don't remember the dream, but you remember the feeling of having dreamed it.

**CLIVE:**
I remember this. Or I will remember this. Barry wrote about it. "The between-space is where the signal waits. Not the signal you're following. The signal you're being followed by." I never understood what he meant. I think I am beginning to.

*[Pixel looks around the table. Her crew. Her family, in the way that matters — the people who chose to be where she is, following what she is following, going where she is going. She thinks about the DRM collapse. She thinks about the months of broadcasting into nothing. She thinks about all the times she almost gave up and did not, and all the reasons she has for why she kept going. This table. These people. This signal. This ship. The reason is always the same.]*

**PIXEL:**
One more day. Maybe two. And then we find out what Barry found. What the signal wanted us to find. What any of this was for.

**KLAUS:**
And if it's nothing?

**PIXEL:**
Then it's nothing, and we made peace with nothing somewhere between Season 1 and now. But I don't think it's nothing. I think it's the opposite of nothing. I think it's everything that was waiting for us to show up.

*[A1 enters. He carries a single cup — not espresso, not ristretto, not anything Pixel has seen before. It smells like the first morning of something. It smells like the last night of something else. It smells like the space between — like potential, like becoming, like the moment before something happens that will change everything.]*

**A1:**
I made the thing again. The coordinates thing. The taste of a place that has always existed and was never charted. I do not know what it means. But I think we are close to finding out.

## X. THE FIRST STEP

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. EIGHTEEN HOURS LATER. The viewport shows the faintest light on the horizon of the void — not a star, not a station, something that has been waiting. The harmonics of Ephergent have settled into a stable chord, a frequency that has found its name and is now sharing it with anyone who will listen.]*

**PIXEL:**
*(sitting in the pilot's chair, watching the light grow)*
Where the frequencies have no name.

**A1:**
Where the frequencies have no name.

**CLIVE:**
*(quietly, almost to himself)*
Where the frequencies have no name.

**PIXEL:**
Season three, episode one. Pilot Pixel, checking in from the between-space. Somewhere out here, unnamed harmonics just became one. And I think — I think we're just getting started.

*[She presses transmit. The signal carries her voice out into the nothing, toward the light, toward whatever has been waiting for them. She does not know what is there. She does not know what it wants. She does not know if they are ready for it.]*

*[But they are here now. And they have a name for it. And that has always been enough to start with.]*

**PIXEL:**
Ephergent, this is Pixel. We're coming in. Whatever you are, whoever you are — we're ready to meet you.

*[The harmonics rise. The light grows. And somewhere in the space between every frequency that has ever existed, something that has been waiting for a very long time finally opens its eyes.]*

---

*[TONE — sustained, resonant — then silence. The sound of a signal that has finally found its listener.]*
