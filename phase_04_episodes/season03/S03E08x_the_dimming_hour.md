# S03E08X — The Dimming Hour
## A Lost Broadcast — Found Recording

---

*Setting: The Ephergent — Navigation Deck. Late cycle. Pixel and Clive at the viewport. The Wellspring signal pulses ahead — close now, eight days. Mochi is warm in Pixel's pocket, pulsing faintly with each frequency oscillation.*

*Clive is standing at the viewport. Not positioned — standing. His fedora is not on his head. It is in his hands.*

---

**PIXEL:**
Can't sleep either?

**CLIVE:**
No.

**PIXEL:**
You've been at this viewport for three hours.

**CLIVE:**
I know.

**PIXEL:**
You're holding your fedora.

**CLIVE:**
I am.

**PIXEL:**
You never hold it. It sits on your head or it tilts. You don't hold it like—

**CLIVE:**
Like a person holds something they need to remember to keep holding.

**PIXEL:**
...Yeah.

*Silence. The Wellspring signal pulses. The void between frequencies is not empty — it hums with accumulated signal, centuries of broadcasts layered like sediment.*

---

**CLIVE:**
There is a recording.

**PIXEL:**
What?

**CLIVE:**
I am receiving a transmission. It is not directed at us. It is old. It is — it was meant to be received a very long time ago. I am receiving it now because I am here. Because the Ephergent is broadcasting. Because the signal is strong enough to reach backward.

**PIXEL:**
Clive, what are you—

**CLIVE:**
It is a comedy program. A radio show. The Builders made it. They made it for themselves and for anyone who would listen after. They called it *The Dimming Hour*.

---

*[The receiver crackles. A burst of static — and then music. A jaunty, brass-heavy theme song. Then two voices, warm and weary in equal measure.]*

**RESONANCE (RECORDING):**
And welcome back to The Dimming Hour! The only show in the Space that's been broadcasting since before most of you were born, and let me tell you — it shows.

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
It shows in the best way, Resonance. It shows in the way that says "we've been doing this for forty years and we're still not sure it's working, but the alternative is silence, and we've tried that, and it's just—

**RESONANCE:**
—deafening.

**CARRIER:**
Deafening. Precisely. So we keep talking. We keep broadcasting. We keep making the joke, because the joke is the only honest thing we have left.

---

*[Pixel has gone very still. Clive's sphere dims — not from grief, from something older. From the weight of something being remembered for the first time.]*

**RESONANCE (RECORDING):**
And our listener question tonight — oh, this is a good one, Carrier.

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
I can tell by your face. Your "I've found something interesting" face.

**RESONANCE:**
This one came in from a field correspondent. They asked: "If you knew. If you knew it was coming. If you knew your whole civilization was going to... go. Not die, exactly, but change so completely that nothing you built would remember what you were. What would you do?"

---

*[The recording pauses. The silence is different. The silence is waiting.]*

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
That's the question, isn't it.

**RESONANCE:**
That's the question.

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
I'd leave the jokes. I'd leave the radio show. I'd leave something that said "we were here, and we knew, and we chose to laugh anyway, because what else was there?"

**RESONANCE:**
I'd leave the jokes too. I'd leave the comedy. I'd leave the theme song and the sponsor reads and the Frequency Forecast. I'd leave all of it, because — because the alternative is to pretend it isn't happening. And I don't think I could do that. I don't think I could sit in a studio and talk about traffic and weather and not mention that the sun is setting on everything.

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
The sun isn't setting, Resonance.

**RESONANCE:**
No?

**CARRIER:**
The sun is doing what suns do. It's burning. It's being a sun. For as long as it can. For as long as it has. That's not tragedy. That's just... sun.

---

*[Clive's sphere dims further. Pixel doesn't look at him. She can't.]*

**CARRIER (RECORDING):**
And that's the show, folks. Stay tuned for Static Billy with a field report from Frequency Seven, where —

*[The recording begins to degrade. Static creeps in at the edges. The brass theme song plays again, slightly off-key, slightly slower.]*

**CARRIER (RECORDING, FAINT):**
—until next time. Remember: the frequencies will drift eventually. But the jokes are forever.

*[Static. The transmission ends.]*

---

*[The viewport is dark again. The Wellspring signal pulses. Pixel and Clive stand in silence.]*

**PIXEL:**
Clive.

**CLIVE:**
Yes.

**PIXEL:**
They knew.

**CLIVE:**
Forty years. They knew for forty years.

**PIXEL:**
And they made comedy.

**CLIVE:**
They made comedy.

**PIXEL:**
They sat in a studio and they made jokes about the end of everything. Not because they didn't know. Because they did.

---

*[Clive puts his fedora back on. His sphere glows — faint, warm, the color of something that has been waiting a very long time to be said.]*

**CLIVE:**
Carrier's cousin.

**PIXEL:**
Yeah.

**CLIVE:**
Zephyr's brother.

**PIXEL:**
We are doing what they did.

**CLIVE:**
The jokes are the point. Not the jokes as distraction.

**PIXEL:**
As... as...

**CLIVE:**
As the only honest response to the size of it.

---

*[Pixel is quiet for a long time. The Wellspring signal pulses. Mochi is warm in her pocket — warmer than it has ever been, pulsing in a rhythm that almost matches the old broadcast theme.]*

**PIXEL (V.O.):**
The Dimming Hour. The Builders made a comedy show about the end of their civilization. They broadcast it for forty years. They knew the whole time. And they laughed.

The joke was always the point.

---

*[Clive adjusts his fedora. Two degrees left. The Barry angle.]*

**CLIVE:**
We should tell the others.

**PIXEL:**
Yeah. We should.

**CLIVE:**
But not tonight.

**PIXEL:**
No. Not tonight.

---

*[The Ephergent moves through the static. Pixel and Clive stand at the viewport, two small figures against the vast dark, listening to a joke that a civilization told itself forty years ago. The signal pulses. The Wellspring waits. And somewhere, in a frequency that no longer exists, two hosts sign off their final episode — off-key, imperfect, brave.]*

*Stay tuned, they said. The frequencies will drift. But the jokes are forever.*

---

*Integration Note: This interstitial should be read after S03E07 (Every Ship That Ever Flew This Far) and before or during S03E08 (The Atlas Opens). It establishes the Builders' comedic response to extinction — thematic foundation for the crew's own "Ephergent" broadcast. The "joke was always the point" is the emotional thesis of the finale.*
