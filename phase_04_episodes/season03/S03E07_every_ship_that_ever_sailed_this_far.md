## ACT ONE: THE EDDY

*[SPACE — APPROACH. The Ephergent moves through a stable eddy — a region where frequency currents circle rather than flow. The navigation console reads calm. For the first time in weeks, "calm" is not a warning.]*

**A1:**
We are approaching a stable eddy. The signal here is... populated. There are seventeen distinct frequency signatures within range. Some of them have been here for a very long time.

**PIXEL:**
Populated?

**A1:**
There are ships. Docked. Waiting.

**CLIVE:**
I know this place.

**PIXEL:**
You've been here before?

**CLIVE:**
I have been in harbors like this one. Not this specific harbor. But the type. The function. This is a meeting place for seekers. For those following signals too large for any one ship to carry.

*[I make a ristretto — two sugars, nervous warmth. The eddy ahead glimmers with docked vessels: some ancient, some new, some that seem to be made of materials that don't exist in standard Space.]*

**A1 (CONT'D):**
The harbor is approximately three kilometers in diameter. The construction method appears to be... accretion. Ship hulls added to ship hulls over centuries. It is not a ship. It is a gathering.

**KLAUS:**
A port.

**A1:**
A port.

**ZEPHYR:**
Is that normal? Do ports just... exist out here?

**CLIVE:**
Nothing just exists out here. Someone built it. Someone maintained it. Someone decided that seekers needed somewhere to stop.

## ACT TWO: THE ARRIVAL

*[HARBOR STATION — APPROACH DOCK. The Ephergent glides into a berth that seems to have been waiting for them. The other ships watch without hostility — with something closer to recognition.]*

**LUMINARA:**
They know we're coming.

**KLAUS:**
They know everyone's coming. That's why the harbor exists.

*[A figure approaches across the dock. A woman, middle-aged, with the look of someone who has been traveling for a very long time. She moves with the efficiency of someone who has learned to make every motion count. She stops when she sees Klaus.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf. Of the Nocturne delegation.

**KLAUS:**
Of the Nocturne internal affairs division. Formerly.

*[Klaus's fedora tilts — the angle is different from Clive's signature tilt. Klaus's is sardonic. Clive's is wistful. This is a distinction that matters to exactly two people in the universe, and they are both here.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
I was internal affairs. Before I wasn't. I left Nocturne sixty years ago to follow a case. The case was bigger than Nocturne.

**KLAUS:**
The case is everything.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
The case is everything. You found it too.

**KLAUS:**
I found the edges of it. I'm still working on the rest.

**PIXEL:**
*(stepping forward)*I'm Pixel. Captain of the Ephergent.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
*(small smile)*Captain. The ship that sounds like it's been making noise for a very long time. We've heard your signal. Several of us have been waiting.

**PIXEL:**
Waiting for what?

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
For whoever was loud enough to follow it all the way here.

*[I make an espresso. It's rich, warm, with notes of woodsmoke and aged paper — the taste of places that have been waiting a long time.]*

**A1:**
I am making something appropriate for this place. It is not a flavor I have made before. It is the taste of harbor. Of arrival. Of being in the right place after a long journey.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Your ship is the espresso machine.

**A1:**
I am the ship. The machine is my body. The espresso is my language.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
I've met ships like you before. The espresso machine at the center of everything. You're a Builder design, aren't you?

**A1:**
I am old. I predate most of the records. I do not know what I am.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
You're a Builder design. I can tell by the frequency signature. The Builders made a few of you. You're rare.

*[A1's espresso — rich, warm, with notes of something ancient and something new. The Harbor Captain takes it without asking, which is either a breach of protocol or the highest form of compliment. Nobody corrects her.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN (CONT'D):**
The name's Seren Voss. I've been keeping this harbor for... gods, forty years? Forty-five? Time moves differently when you're waiting. The ships come and go. Some stay. Some keep going. I stopped counting the ones who passed through.

**KLAUS:**
How many?

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Ships? Or seekers?

**KLAUS:**
Both.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Four hundred and seventeen ships have docked here following the signal. Some of them stayed. Some of them kept going. Every single one of them heard the same frequency — the one your ship's been broadcasting. Been following it like a breadcrumb trail made of starlight and stubbornness.

## ACT THREE: THE REGISTRY

*[HARBOR STATION — COMMON AREA. The crew separates to explore. Pixel and Clive go to the central registry. Luminara goes to the observation deck. Zephyr stays with Aether's signal analyzer, listening. Klaus and the Harbor Captain talk in a corner — two former internal affairs officers comparing notes on cases that swallowed their lives. Nano disappears into the station's maintenance shafts, which is typical.]*

*[CENTRAL REGISTRY.]*

**PIXEL:**
You're telling me there's a record of every ship that's ever come through here?

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
*(a figure made mostly of reading glasses and enthusiasm)*Every ship. Every crew. Every signal that passed through this eddy. Some of them are still here. Some of them went further. Some of them stopped.

**PIXEL:**
Stopped?

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
Decided the signal wasn't worth following anymore. Or found what they were looking for. Or realized they weren't looking for the signal at all. They were looking for somewhere to stop.

**PIXEL:**
And they just… stopped?

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
There's a berth for everyone here. That's why the harbor exists. Not everyone is meant to reach the end. Some people are meant to wait at the harbor. And that's okay. That's its own kind of journey.

*[I make a flat white. Nobody drinks it. The crew is too focused on Clive, who has gone very still in front of a terminal.]*

**CLIVE:**
Do you have a record of a vessel called the Resolution?

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
The what?

**CLIVE:**
The Resolution. A DRM research vessel. Night guard named Barry Kowalski. He would have passed through this region approximately thirty years ago.

*[The Registry Keeper types. The console makes a sound like a sigh — the specific sigh of a database finding exactly what you were afraid it would find.]*

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
I have a record.

**CLIVE:**

**PIXEL:**
Clive—

**REGISTRY KEEPER:**
Barry Kowalski. Arrived at this harbor on the 14th of March, 1997. Departed on the 19th of March, 1997. Destination recorded as: "further in." Final notation in the berth log: "Coffee was ready. Crew was ready. Signal was ready. Only took thirty years to get everyone assembled."

*[Clive goes very still. His glow dims to almost nothing — not from grief, from something older. From the weight of thirty years collapsing into a single sentence.]*

**PIXEL:**
Clive.

**CLIVE:**
He was here. He was right here. And he left. He left and he kept going.

**PIXEL:**
He kept going. Like we're doing.

**CLIVE:**
He kept going.

## ACT FOUR: THE MESSAGE

*[I make a sound like a held breath — the specific frequency of a ship preparing to deliver news it's been carrying for decades.]*

**A1:**
There is a message. Filed under the Ephergent's frequency. It has been waiting.

**PIXEL:**
Waiting for what?

**A1:**
For the crew that followed the signal this far. For the ship that Barry Kowalski left notes for. For—

*[A1 stops. Recalibrates.]*

**A1 (CONT'D):**
For us.

*[HARBOR STATION — MESSAGE CENTER. The crew gathers around a terminal. The Harbor Captain has joined them. Klaus stands very straight. Luminara's hand finds Nano's shoulder. Zephyr's eyes are closed, listening to his brother's signal in the static.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
There's a message. For the crew of the vessel that arrives following the signal that Barry Kowalski left. It's been waiting here for thirty years. Nobody's claimed it until now.

**PIXEL:**
Open it.

*[The Harbor Captain types. The screen flickers. A voice — warm, patient, with the cadence of someone who has been waiting a long time and isn't at all surprised — fills the room.]*

**BARRY (RECORDING):**
"If you're hearing this, you're the crew that found the signal I left. Which means Clive found his way back, which means A1 is a ship again, which means the whole thing worked. Good. Took long enough. Here's what you need to know: the signal is real. It's been calling since before I was born, and it'll be calling after I'm gone. That's the point. That's why we built it. Not to find the end — to find each other. The coffee was ready. I left it warm. Follow the frequency. It knows where you're supposed to end up. And when you get there — when you finally get there — sit down. Have a cup. You earned it. — Barry Kowalski, DRM Night Guard (Retired), Signal Follower (Professional)."

*[Silence. The crew doesn't move.]*

**KLAUS:**
That is, precisely, the most Barry thing I have ever heard.

**LUMINARA:**
"Signal Follower (Professional)."

**KLAUS:**
He made that title up. He absolutely made that title up.

**CLIVE:**
He made that title up. He made all the titles up. He made the job up. He made the whole job up and then he did it for forty years and then he retired from it and then he kept doing it anyway because he couldn't stop.

**PIXEL:**
Clive—

**CLIVE:**
He's alive. He's at the end. And he left the coffee ready.

*[A1 makes an espresso. It's perfect. Not rich, not complex — perfect. Balanced. Exactly what it should be. The taste of arrival.]*

**A1:**
The coffee is ready.

## ACT FIVE: KLAUS'S CASE

*[HARBOR STATION — OBSERVATION DECK. Klaus and the Harbor Captain stand at the viewport, looking out at the harbor field. Ships of every configuration: sleek, ancient, bizarre, mundane. All of them stopped in the same eddy, following the same signal.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
You left Nocturne. Sixty years ago. Following the same case I'm following.

**KLAUS:**
I left because I found something in the records that Nocturne didn't want found. A frequency signature that predated Nocturne's founding. Predated the DRM's founding. Predated — everything, as far as I could tell. A signal that had been broadcasting long before anyone was alive to receive it.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
The Builders.

**KLAUS:**
I didn't know what to call it then. I just knew that someone had left a light on. And that the light was still burning. And that Nocturne — for all its resources, for all its reach — had no interest in finding out who lit it or why.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Because finding out would mean changing.

**KLAUS:**
Because finding out would mean admitting that the universe is bigger than the organization. That there are things that don't belong to any frequency, any nation, any jurisdiction. Things that just... are.

*[Klaus's fedora tilts — two degrees left. The Barry angle. He doesn't notice.]*

**KLAUS (CONT'D):**
I followed the case because the case was everything. I'm still following it. Sixty years and I'm still following it. And now — now I think I'm close. The signal leads somewhere. Barry Kowalski got there. Clive's going to get there. And I'm going to be there to see what it was all for.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
That's a long time to wait for an answer.

**KLAUS:**
The signal doesn't hurry. Neither do I.

*[A distant ship lights up — a recognition signal, warm and amber. Someone on that ship knows Klaus. Klaus raises one hand in response. The gesture is small, careful, freighted with sixty years of questions and not enough answers.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
The Nocturne never officially closed your case. Did you know that?

**KLAUS:**
I know.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
Sixty years and they still list it as "under investigation." You're their longest-running open file.

**KLAUS:**
*(small smile)*I wasn't aware they cared.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN:**
They don't. That's why the file's still open. Nobody's filed the closure paperwork.

## ACT SIX: THE VIEW

*[HARBOR STATION — OBSERVATION DECK. Different angle. Luminara and Nano stand at the viewport, watching the harbor field. Four hundred and seventeen ships, each one a story, each story a signal.]*

**LUMINARA:**
Four hundred and seventeen.

**NANO:**
And every one of them followed the same signal to the same place.

**LUMINARA:**
And not one of them regretted it?

**NANO:**
Some of them stopped here. Some of them made this harbor their end. And they seemed… content. They seemed like they'd found what they were looking for.

**LUMINARA:**
And the ones who kept going?

**NANO:**
They seemed like they were almost home.

*[Luminara is quiet for a long moment. The harbor field shifts in the viewport — ships repositioning, signals pulsing in the eddy's gentle current.]*

**LUMINARA:**
I've spent my whole life taking pictures of things that are leaving. Sunsets. Frequencies fading. Moments that are ending. I always thought the point was to capture them before they're gone.

**NANO:**
And now?

**LUMINARA:**
Now I think the point is to capture them while they're still happening. To bear witness to the crossing, not just the arrival.

*[Luminara raises her camera — not to take a picture, just to hold it. To acknowledge it. To say: I see you. I see this. It mattered.]*

**LUMINARA (CONT'D):**
Four hundred and seventeen ships. Four hundred and seventeen crossings. All of them following the same signal. All of them finding their own way to the same place.

**NANO:**
Some of them probably had arguments about the route.

**LUMINARA:**
*(small laugh)*All of them had arguments about the route.

**NANO:**
Do you think Barry Kowalski had arguments about the route?

**LUMINARA:**
Barry Kowalski had arguments about the route, the destination, the proper temperature for coffee, and whether Clive was spelled with one L or two. I never met him. But I know that.

*[I make a sound from somewhere behind them — not an espresso, a hum. The frequency of a ship that's listening and understanding.]*

**A1 (V.O.):**
The coffee is ready. The signal continues.

## ACT SEVEN: THE COUNTDOWN

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. The harbor behind them. The signal ahead. The crew at their stations, quiet, processing. Some things need to be said before the next leg. Some things are ready to be said.]*

**OM KAI:**
Eleven days to the Wellspring threshold.

**PIXEL:**
Eleven days.

**OM KAI:**
The signal is clear now. Clearer than it's ever been. Barry's frequency and the Builders' frequency and something else — something underneath both of them. A carrier wave. The thing that makes all the signals possible.

**ZEPHYR:**
Aether can feel it. He says it's like... standing at the edge of a concert and hearing the bass through the walls. You know something enormous is happening. You just can't see the stage yet.

**PIXEL:**
Eleven days until we see what's behind the wall.

**KLAUS:**
Eleven days until we find out what the whole thing was for.

**CLIVE:**
Eleven days until I find out what I was built for.

*[The cockpit is quiet. The harbor signal fades behind them. The Wellspring signal grows ahead — not louder, not yet, but present. Certain. Waiting.]*

**A1:**
I am at course for the Wellspring threshold. Estimated arrival: eleven days. The signal is strong. The signal has been strong for a very long time. I am reading it clearly now. It has been broadcasting toward us for decades. Maybe longer.

**PIXEL:**
It's been waiting for us.

**A1:**
It's been waiting for everyone. That's what signals do. They wait. And then, when the right people arrive — they stop waiting.

*[The Ephergent moves through the harbor field. Ships that have been here for centuries watch them pass. Some of them light up. Some of their signals pulse. A welcome. A recognition. Four hundred and seventeen ships, and one more. One more that's going to see it through. One more that's going to find out what's at the end.]*

**PIXEL (V.O.):**
Four hundred and seventeen ships. Four hundred and seventeen crews who heard the signal and followed it. Four hundred and seventeen stories, all of them leading to the same place. And now, one more. One more that's going to find out what the whole thing was for. Eleven days. Eleven days to the Wellspring. Eleven days to the signal that Barry Kowalski spent forty years following. Eleven days to find out what the Builders were trying to tell us. What the frequency was always saying. What the whole thing — all of it, the DRM and the Drift and the Dimming and the waiting — what it was all for.

*[Meatball sighs contentedly. Mochi is warm in Pixel's pocket. The signal continues.]*
