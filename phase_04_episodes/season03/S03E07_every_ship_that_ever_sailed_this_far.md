# S03E07 — Every Ship That Ever Sailed This Far

**Season 03: The Source**
**Episode 07 — Every Ship That Ever Sailed This Far**

---

## Cold Open

*[SPACE — WELLSPRING EDDY. The Ephergent emerges from a static cloud into a field of ships. Dozens of them. Maybe hundreds. Vessels of every size, every frequency signature, every era. Ships that look like they were built by civilizations that don't exist anymore. Ships that look like they were built yesterday. Ships that look like they're not quite ships at all — structures of pure signal holding a shape that resembles a vessel.]*

*[SHIP — COCKPIT. The crew at the viewport. Nobody speaks for a long moment.]*

**KLAUS**
Well.

**LUMINARA**
How many?

**A1**
I am reading four hundred and seventeen distinct frequency signatures. Of those: sixty-three are active vessels. The rest are… echoes. Ships that have been here so long they've become part of the field. Ships that are still broadcasting, even after their crews have gone.

**CLIVE**
How long have they been here?

**A1**
Some of them arrived centuries ago. Some arrived millennia. One signature predates the Builders. It is older than the DRM. Older than the frequency classification system. It is older than anything I have a name for.

**OM KAI**
They followed the signal.

**A1**
They followed the signal.

**OM KAI**
Like us.

**A1**
Like all of us. We are not unique in this. We are not special in this. We are one of four hundred and seventeen ships that followed the signal to the edge of everything. We are one of sixty-three that are still moving.

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Four hundred and seventeen ships. Four hundred and seventeen crews who heard the same thing we heard. Who went the same places we went. Who asked the same questions.

*[BEEP — incoming transmission.]*

**VOICE (TRANSMISSION)**
Unidentified vessel at bearing zero-four-seven. You are not the first to arrive here, and you will not be the last. State your name and your business, or you will be assumed hostile and dealt with accordingly.

**KLAUS**
I will handle this.

**PIXEL**
Klaus—

**KLAUS**
I am, by designation, the head of security. This is precisely my function. Also: I know this voice.

---

## Act One — The Harbor

*[HARBOR STATION — APPROACH. A massive structure at the center of the eddy — not a ship but a station, built from the hulls of dozens of ships lashed together over centuries. A port. A gathering point. A place where seekers find each other.]*

*[CREW ON THE DECK. The Ephergent docks at a berth that seems to have been waiting for them specifically. The harbor is alive with activity — crews from dozens of ships, moving between vessels, trading, talking, sharing frequencies.]*

**KLAUS**
I know this station.

**PIXEL**
You've been here before?

**KLAUS**
I have been in harbors like this one. Not this specific harbor. But the type. The function. This is a meeting place for seekers. For those following signals too large for any one ship to carry.

*[A figure approaches. A woman, middle-aged, with the look of someone who has been traveling for a very long time. She stops when she sees Klaus.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf. Of the Nocturne delegation.

**KLAUS**
Of the Nocturne internal affairs division. Formerly.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
I was internal affairs. Before I wasn't. I left Nocturne sixty years ago to follow a case. The case was bigger than Nocturne.

**KLAUS**
The case is everything.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
The case is everything. You found it too.

**KLAUS**
I found the edges of it. I'm still working on the rest.

*[Klaus turns to the crew.]*

**KLAUS**
Everyone, this is Captain Seren Voss. She was Nocturne internal affairs before I was. She left to investigate the same anomalies I was investigating. She's been out here for six decades following the same thread.

**PIXEL**
Six decades.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
The signal doesn't hurry. Neither do I.

*[A1's machine makes an espresso. It's rich, warm, with notes of woodsmoke and aged paper — the taste of places that have been waiting a long time.]*

**A1**
I am making something appropriate for this place. It is not a flavor I have made before. It is the taste of harbor. Of arrival. Of being in the right place after a long journey.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
Your ship is the espresso machine.

**A1**
I am the ship. The machine is my body. The espresso is my language.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
I've met ships like you before. The espresso machine at the center of everything. You're a Builder design, aren't you?

**A1**
I am old. I predate most of the records. I do not know what I am.

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
You're a Builder design. I can tell by the frequency signature. The Builders made a few of you. You're rare.

*[A1's espresso — rich, warm, with notes of something ancient and something new.]*

---

## Act Two — The Seekers

*[HARBOR STATION — COMMON AREA. The crew separates to explore. Pixel and Clive go to the central registry. Luminara goes to the observation deck. Zephyr stays with Aether's signal analyzer, searching. Klaus and the Harbor Captain talk in a corner. Nano disappears into the station's maintenance shafts.]*

*[CENTRAL REGISTRY.]*

**PIXEL**
You're telling me there's a record of every ship that's ever come through here?

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
Every ship. Every crew. Every signal that passed through this eddy. Some of them are still here. Some of them went further. Some of them stopped.

**PIXEL**
Stopped?

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
Decided the signal wasn't worth following anymore. Or found what they were looking for. Or realized they weren't looking for the signal at all. They were looking for somewhere to stop.

**PIXEL**
And they just… stopped?

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
There's a berth for everyone here. That's why the harbor exists. Not everyone is meant to reach the end. Some people are meant to wait at the harbor. And that's okay. That's its own kind of journey.

**CLIVE**
Do you have a record of a vessel called the Resolution?

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
The what?

**CLIVE**
The Resolution. A DRM research vessel. Night guard named Barry Kowalski. He would have passed through this region approximately thirty years ago.

*[The Registry Keeper types. The console makes a sound like a sigh.]*

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
I have a record.

**CLIVE**

**PIXEL**
Clive—

**REGISTRY KEEPER**
Barry Kowalski. Arrived at this harbor on the 14th of March, 1997. Departed on the 19th of March, 1997. Destination recorded as: "further in." Final notation in the berth log: "Coffee was ready. Crew was ready. Signal was ready. Only took thirty years to get everyone assembled."

*[Clive goes very still. His glow dims to almost nothing.]*

**PIXEL**
Clive.

**CLIVE**
He was here. He was right here. And he left. He left and he kept going.

**PIXEL**
He kept going. Like we're doing.

**CLIVE**
He kept going.

*[A1's machine makes a sound like a held breath.]*

**A1**
He said coffee was ready.

**CLIVE**
That's what he said. That's exactly what he said. That's what he always said when he was ready to go. When everything was prepared and the signal was clear and all that was left was to follow. "Coffee's ready, Clive. Time to fly."

**PIXEL**
Clive—

**CLIVE**
He was here. And he left. And he got there before us. Which means he knows we're coming.

## Act Three — The Message

*[HARBOR STATION — MESSAGE CENTER. The crew gathers around a terminal. The Harbor Captain has joined them.]*

**HARBOR CAPTAIN**
There's a message. For the crew of the vessel that arrives following the signal that Barry Kowalski left. It's been waiting here for thirty years. Nobody's claimed it until now.

**PIXEL**
Open it.

*[The Harbor Captain types. The screen flickers. A voice — warm, patient, with the cadence of someone who has been waiting a long time and isn't at all surprised.]*

**BARRY (RECORDING)**
"If you're hearing this, you're the crew that found the signal I left. Which means Clive found his way back, which means A1 is a ship again, which means the whole thing worked. Good. Took long enough. Here's what you need to know: the signal is real. It's been calling since before I was born, and it'll be calling after I'm gone. That's the point. That's why we built it. Not to find the end — to find each other. The coffee was ready. I left it warm. Follow the frequency. It knows where you're supposed to end up. And when you get there — when you finally get there — sit down. Have a cup. You earned it. — Barry Kowalski, DRM Night Guard (Retired), Signal Follower (Professional)."

*[Silence. The crew doesn't move.]*

**KLAUS**
That is, precisely, the most Barry thing I have ever heard.

**LUMINARA**
"Signal Follower (Professional)."

**KLAUS**
He made that title up. He absolutely made that title up.

**CLIVE**
He made that title up. He made all the titles up. He made the job up. He made the whole job up and then he did it for forty years and then he retired from it and then he kept doing it anyway because he couldn't stop.

**PIXEL**
Clive—

**CLIVE**
He's alive. He's at the end. And he left the coffee ready.

*[A1 makes an espresso. It's perfect. Not rich, not complex — perfect. Balanced. Exactly what it should be. The taste of arrival.]*

**A1**
The coffee is ready.

---

## Tag

*[THE EPHERGENT — COCKPIT. The harbor behind them. The signal ahead. The crew at their stations, quiet, processing.]*

**OM KAI**
Four hundred and seventeen ships. And every one of them followed the same signal to the same place.

**PIXEL**
And not one of them regretted it?

**OM KAI**
Some of them stopped here. Some of them made this harbor their end. And they seemed… content. They seemed like they'd found what they were looking for.

**PIXEL**
And the ones who kept going?

**OM KAI**
They seemed like they were almost home.

*[A1's espresso — perfect, balanced, the taste of something that's been waiting a long time to be exactly what it is.]*

**A1**
I am at course for the Wellspring threshold. Estimated arrival: eleven days. The signal is strong. The signal has been strong for a very long time. I am reading it clearly now. It has been broadcasting toward us for decades. Maybe longer.

**PIXEL**
It's been waiting for us.

**A1**
It's been waiting for everyone. That's what signals do. They wait. And then, when the right people arrive — they stop waiting.

*[The Ephergent moves through the harbor field. Ships that have been here for centuries watch them pass. Some of them light up. Some of their signals pulse. A welcome. A recognition.]*

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Four hundred and seventeen ships. Four hundred and seventeen crews who heard the signal and followed it. And now, one more. One more that's going to see it through. One more that's going to find out what's at the end. Eleven days. Eleven days to the Wellspring. Eleven days to the signal that Barry Kowalski spent forty years following. Eleven days to find out what the whole thing was for.

*[Meatball sighs contentedly. Mochi is warm in Pixel's pocket. The signal continues.]*
