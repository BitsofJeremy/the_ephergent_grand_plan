# S03E02 — The Boneyard of Beautiful Things

*[FREQUENCY GRAVEYARD — SURFACE. The crew steps out onto a field of debris. The ground crunches underfoot — not rock, not metal, but something between: the remains of things that once sang. Antennae jutting from the dust like grave markers. Sound-plates stacked in neat rows by someone who measured grief in decibels.]*

*[MOCHI pads ahead, nose to the ground. MOCHI sniffs a small bell half-buried in the debris, nudges it with a paw. A single, pure note rings out across the graveyard — clean, sustained, impossibly alive.]*

**LUMINARA**
*(whispers)*
Oh.

**A1**
The bell still functions. Its frequency was preserved by the surrounding silence. It has been waiting to be heard.

**OM KAI**
Everything here is waiting. That's what death means, out here. Not ending — waiting. Waiting for someone to come back and listen.

**KLAUS**
How do we honor the dead?

**OM KAI**
We listen to what they left behind.

*[MOCHI nudges the bell again. Another note — different pitch, harmonizing with the first. MOCHI tilts head, listening.]*

**PIXEL**
Barry's field notes. He logged each artifact he found. Frequency, coordinates, condition. His handwriting gets smaller toward the end of each entry. Like he's trying to fit grief into margins.

*[PIXEL holds up the salvage ledger, pages dense with precise measurements and, occasionally, fragments of something almost like poetry.]*

**PIXEL (CONT'D)**
"Antlers of pure signal, 3.2 megahertz, deceased. Beautiful. Not salvageable. Left for the next traveler."

*[FREQUENCY GRAVEYARD — DERELICT VESSEL. A small ship, maybe a research craft, wedged between two dead frequency fields. Its hull is intact but its interior is a museum of silence — every instrument still, every surface coated in the fine dust of ended frequencies.]*

*[CLIVE moves through the vessel slowly. His glow pulses erratically — the pattern of a stapler in distress.]*

**CLIVE**
*(to himself)*
This is his layout. This is Barry's layout. He built this cabin like he built every cabin — everything within reach, nothing unnecessary. He lived in this ship for how long? How many years, Barry? How many years did you spend in here, alone, following something you couldn't explain?

*[CLIVE's hand reaches out — not to open a panel or check an instrument, but to press against the wall. Against a small pattern stamped into the metal.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
*(breath catches)*
Staple patterns.

**PIXEL**
*(entering)*
Clive?

**CLIVE**
His patterns. My patterns. The ones we developed together — the language we made, the one that only we could read. He put them HERE. He put them where I would find them.

*[PIXEL moves closer. On the wall, a sequence of small stamped marks — angular, precise, the visual rhythm of a stapler's work.]*

**PIXEL**
What does it say?

*[CLIVE stares at the pattern for a long moment. His glow dims to almost nothing, then surges back.]*

**CLIVE**
"Clive — followed the signal too far. Turned back too late. Found what I was looking for. Kept going. The coffee was ready. I'm leaving it warm for you. — B."

**PIXEL**
He knew you'd come.

**CLIVE**
He always knew. He knew me better than I knew myself. He knew I'd follow the signal eventually. He knew I'd find this place. He left me a message in our private language, pressed into dead metal, waiting for me to walk in and read it.

*[CLIVE's staple mechanism fires — a rapid, arrhythmic succession of marks into the air. Not a message. A reaction. Grief expressed in the only language a stapler has.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
He was my partner. We worked the night guard together. We learned each other's frequencies like a language. And then one day he just… left. Said he had to follow something. Said he'd explain when he got back. That was thirty years ago.

**PIXEL**
He's still out there. The notes say he kept going.

**CLIVE**
He always kept going. That was Barry. Never stop. Never stay. Just follow the signal until there's nothing left to follow.

*[SHIP — COMMON AREA. The crew gathered around the salvage ledger. A1 makes coffee — slow, vegetal, the flavor of something preserved in time. KLAUS and LUMINARA examine the debris samples outside. ZEPHYR studies frequency readouts at the nav station.]*

**PIXEL (V.O.)**
Barry's field notes. A record of mourning by measurement. Forty-seven frequencies catalogued in his handwriting, each entry a small elegy.

**OM KAI**
He was documenting loss with the precision of a scientist and the heart of a poet.

**A1**
I am reading his notations. "Frequency 0012: Ocean world. Last known life: 4,000 years ago. Cause of death: natural entropy. Condition: beautiful. Salvage value: none. Historical value: immeasurable." He treats each dead world like a patient he couldn't save.

**NANO**
*(entering, data tablet in hand)*
I pulled what I could from the derelict's data banks. Corrupted, fragmented — but recoverable. Barry was thorough. His logs reference a pattern he found here, in this graveyard, that pointed him further in.

**PIXEL**
Further in where?

**NANO**
He called it "The Wellspring." Said it was the place where signals begin — where the frequency originates. He believed the Ephergent signal was leading all of them there. All the travelers. All the seekers.

**CLIVE**
He left me a note about that too.

*[CLIVE gestures to a small stamp near the vessel's helm — another staple pattern.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
"Wellspring. The place where signals begin. The coffee is ready there. I'll leave it warm."

**KLAUS**
He made that coffee metaphor up along the way, didn't he?

**PIXEL**
I think that's exactly what he did.

**A1**
He created a language of coffee and signals and staple patterns. He used it to leave a trail. For you. For anyone who came looking.

*[A1's machine produces an espresso — rich, warm, with notes of woodsmoke and something almost like hope.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
I am making something appropriate. It is the flavor of waiting. Of things preserved. Of someone who knew you'd come.

**LUMINARA**
*(entering, dust on her sleeves)*
I took photographs. Of everything. The antennae, the sound-plates, the derelict. I couldn't stop taking them. Every time I framed a shot, I thought — this is someone's last moment. This is a world that died and no one was there to see it. And then I thought — but I'm here now. I can see it. Someone should.

**OM KAI**
You bear witness. That is its own kind of salvage.

*[FREQUENCY GRAVEYARD — SUNSET. There is no sun here, but the light shifts anyway — the last echoes of dead stars casting a pale gold across the debris field. The crew stands among the remains, each processing the weight of what they've found.]*

**PIXEL**
We need to finish the survey. The graveyard has information about how frequencies die — whether they fade or are cut short. That's data we need before we go further in.

**CLIVE**
I want to follow his trail. Now. While it's still fresh in me.

**PIXEL**
It's thirty years old, Clive. It's not fresh. It's archaeological.

**CLIVE**
It feels fresh. It feels like he left it yesterday. It feels like he's still ten minutes ahead of me, and if I just go faster, I can catch up.

*[PIXEL is quiet for a moment. The weight of the request hangs between them.]*

**PIXEL**
If we rush this, we miss what the graveyard is teaching us. We miss the chance to understand what we're walking into. Barry didn't rush. He documented. He measured. He listened. That's why he got further than anyone.

**CLIVE**
I know.

**PIXEL**
Help us finish the survey. One more day. And then we follow the trail.

*[CLIVE's glow pulses — slow, deliberate, like a heartbeat.]*

**CLIVE**
One more day.

*[He turns back to the vessel. To the wall of staple patterns. To the message in a language only he can read.]*

*[SHIP — COCKPIT. Night watch. The graveyard recedes on the rear display — a field of silent monuments growing smaller. CLIVE sits alone at the viewport, watching.]*

*[A1's machine produces a small cup — not espresso, something softer. It sets it beside Clive without a word.]*

**CLIVE**
Thank you.

**A1**
I do not know how to help a stapler grieve. But I can make things that are warm.

*[A1 withdraws. CLIVE picks up the cup. His glow steadies — not bright, but stable. Present.]*

*[On the console beside him, a small device. A pattern-maker. Clive's staple mechanism begins to fire — not frantically now, but slowly, deliberately. Each mark a word. Each word a name.]*

*[BARRY. BARRY. BARRY.]*

*[The signal continues. The graveyard falls away. And somewhere ahead, at the place where signals begin, a coffee pot waits.]*
