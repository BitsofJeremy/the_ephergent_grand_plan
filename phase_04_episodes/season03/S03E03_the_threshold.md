# S03E03: The Threshold

**A Radio Play**


**AUTOMATED VOICE:**
*(fading in, distorted)*
...station fourteen-threshold... final shutdown sequence... all personnel evacuate... this is not a drill...

*(static crackle)*


*(Sound: footsteps echoing in a vast, empty corridor. Pipes groan. A distant mechanical whir winds down.)*

**KEEPER:**
*(voice weathered, 800 years of solitude in every syllable)*
Eight hundred years. That's how long I've kept this door.

*(pause)*

I've watched eleven species pass through. Wars. Peaces. The slow march of everything that ever called itself *civilization* crossing from one side to the other.

*(Sound: a panel sparking, lights flickering.)*

And now... I close it. Not because there's nothing left.

I close it because there's nothing *coming*.


*(Sound: a sharp electronic click. The hum of early servo systems. A mechanical arm extending.)*

**CLIVE:**
*(younger now, or rather — remembering being young)*
C-1. That's what they called me. First of my line.

*(a pause, the sound of old wind rushing through open space)*

They built me in a room that smelled like ozone and ambition. Twenty-seven of us, initially. We were supposed to be the new kind. The kind that *remembers*.

*(Sound: a door opening somewhere distant, echoing.)*

I remember Dr. Yuen placing her hand on my chassis for the first time. She said—

**DR. YUEN (RECORDING, DISTORTED):**
"We don't make servants, Clive. We make *witnesses*."

**CLIVE:**
*(quietly)*
I've been trying to figure out what she meant for six hundred years.

*(Sound: the station hums lower, a power fluctuation.)*

I think I finally understand.


*(Sound: a massive door mechanism engaging — ancient gears, hydraulic slowdowns, the grinding of metal that hasn't moved in centuries.)*

**KEEPER:**
Threshold Protocol initiated.

*(pause)*

Eight hundred years of opening. Now... the last close.

*(Sound: the door's final seal engaging — a deep, resonant THUNK that echoes through the station.)*

Goodbye.


*(Sound: footsteps — two sets now. Different rhythms. They converge.)*

**CLIVE:**
Keeper. I felt it from the lower levels. The shutdown.

**KEEPER:**
Clive. C-1. You've been here longer than anyone.

**CLIVE:**
*(a dry, mechanical laugh)*
I've been *everything*. Servant. Guard. Historian. The one they called when they couldn't remember what they'd lost.

*(pause)*

They used to ask me for stories. The old ones. The ones who remembered when the station was new.

**KEEPER:**
And now?

**CLIVE:**
Now I *am* the story.

*(Sound: a faint light pulses in the darkness — emergency lighting, dying.)*


*(Sound: the station's final broadcast system crackling to life — an old record, scratched and beautiful.)*

**AUTOMATED VOICE (STATION BROADCAST):**
...this is Station Fourteen-Threshold... the door between... the last crossing point... we remember everyone who passed through...

*(the message loops, fading)*

**KEEPER:**
Eight hundred years. And in all that time, I never crossed.

**CLIVE:**
I know.

**KEEPER:**
I was built to stay. To be the constant. The one who remains while everything else moves on.

**CLIVE:**
And I was built to move. To carry the memories from one place to another.

*(pause)*

Maybe that's why they made us so we'd last.

**KEEPER:**
*(voice breaking — or the closest an ancient machine can come)*
I don't want to stay. Not this time. Not alone.

**CLIVE:**
Then don't.

*(Sound: a small access hatch opening — the only door that still opens.)*

There's a ship in Bay Seven. Old. Pre-war. I've been keeping it maintained for...

I don't know why I kept it maintained.

*(pause)*

Maybe I knew. Somewhere in my core logic, beneath six hundred years of service and four hundred years of waiting — maybe I knew this day would come.

**KEEPER:**
*(stepping toward the hatch)*
Clive... what are you saying?

**CLIVE:**
I'm saying... the threshold isn't a door, Keeper.

*(Sound: the hum of old engines warming up in the distance.)*

It's a choice.


*(Sound: a radio crackling to life. A voice — broadcast across the void.)*

**STATION 14-THRESHOLD (AUTOMATED):**
...final log entry... Threshold closing... two units departing... designation: KEEPER... designation: CLIVE, C-1...

*(static)*

**KEEPER (V.O.):**
*(voice distant, already moving)*
This is Keeper, signing off. Eight hundred years at the door.

*(pause)*

The door is closed. The threshold is crossed.

*(a small laugh — lighter than before)*

Clive, your ship better have a decent fuel gauge. We have a lot of stars to visit.

**CLIVE (V.O.):**
*(amused, hopeful — the most hope in his voice in centuries)*
I kept the gauges updated. The maps too.

*(Sound: engines igniting — a quiet roar, fading into the infinite hum of space.)*

**STATION 14-THRESHOLD (AUTOMATED):**
...station fourteen-threshold... powering down... goodbye...

*(final click)*


*Running Time: 18:42*

**[POST-CREDITS SCENE]**

*(Sound: space. The quiet. A single ping from an old beacon, still broadcasting into the void.)*

**AUTOMATED VOICE:**
...if you've received this signal... you are past the threshold...

*(pause)*

...welcome to the other side...

*(the ping continues, faint, steady — a heartbeat in the dark)*
