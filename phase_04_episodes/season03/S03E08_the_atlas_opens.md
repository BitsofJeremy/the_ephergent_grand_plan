## ACT ONE: THE WARMTH

*[SHIP — COMMON AREA. Morning cycle. The crew at breakfast. MOCHI is in the center of the table, still glowing. Everyone is trying very hard not to look at it.]*

**KLAUS**
So.

**LUMINARA**
So.

**KLAUS**
Are we going to discuss it, or are we going to continue pretending we aren't all staring at the same object?

**ZEPHYR**
Mochi's been in Pixel's pocket for three days. Mochi is now in the center of the table. Mochi is also now approximately four times warmer than it was last week.

**PIXEL**
Seven times, actually. I checked.

**OM KAI**
Mochi is a Builder artifact. This is documented. Mochi has been described in Builder records as a "calibration device." This is documented. Mochi has been with the crew since the DRM. This is documented.

**CLIVE**
I have been avoiding asking what Mochi is because I suspected the answer was complicated.

**PIXEL**
Clive—

**CLIVE**
I am no longer avoiding the question. Mochi appears to be a calibration device that has been dormant. Mochi appears to be waking up. Mochi appears to be responding to proximity to the Wellspring.

*[I make no sound. This is, in my own way, the most alarming thing that has happened all week.]*

**A1**
I am reading an energy signature from Mochi. It is not electromagnetic. It is not thermal. It is not quantum in any configuration I recognize. It is —

*[A1 pauses. Recalibrates. The pause is longer than any pause A1 has taken before.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
It is frequency data. Mochi is emitting frequency data at a rate that is inconsistent with its apparent size.

**LUMINARA**
How much data?

**A1**
I am uncertain. I am reading approximately eight hundred years of accumulated signal measurements. Approximately eight hundred years of frequency calibrations. Approximately eight hundred years of Builder-era mapping data.

**CLIVE**
Eight hundred years.

**A1**
Eight hundred years. Since before Mochi was placed in the DRM. Since before Mochi was placed anywhere. Mochi has been carrying eight hundred years of—

*[A1 stops. His machine makes a sound like a held breath. Like a machine trying to process something too large for its architecture.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
Mochi is not a calibration device.

**PIXEL**
What is it?

**A1**
Mochi is an atlas. Mochi is a complete frequency map of everything the Builders ever recorded. Every world. Every signal. Every frequency that has ever existed, been catalogued, and — in many cases — been lost. Mochi is carrying the complete history of the Builder frequency network.

*[I shudder. A small, involuntary sound escapes me.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
Mochi has been carrying it since before the Dimming. Mochi has been waiting for someone to bring it home.

*[Mochi pulses. A deep, golden warmth. The room fills with something that feels like a held breath — the pressure before a name is spoken.]*

---

## ACT TWO: THE WEIGHT OF A NAME

**ZEPHYR**
So. To be clear. Mochi has been carrying a complete map of the entire frequency network for eight hundred years.

**A1**
Yes.

**ZEPHYR**
And Mochi has been sitting in Pixel's pocket this whole time.

**A1**
Yes.

**ZEPHYR**
And Mochi has been — what — waiting? This whole time?

**A1**
Yes.

**KLAUS**
That is an extraordinarily patient piece of equipment.

**MEATBALL**
*[wagging tail, harmonizing a single low note of agreement]*

**KLAUS**
I have never been so thoroughly acknowledged by a dog.

**LUMINARA**
Eight hundred years. That's — that's longer than any civilization we've encountered. Longer than the DRM existed. Longer than—

**CLIVE**
Longer than I have been conscious.

*[The room goes quiet. This is not a dramatic statement. This is a fact, delivered in Clive's flat monotone. But the weight of it settles anyway.]*

**PIXEL**
Clive?

**CLIVE**
I was built by the Builders. This is documented. I do not remember being built. I do not remember the Builders. But I know — I have always known — that I was made for a purpose I have not yet fulfilled.

*[Clive's sphere dims. Not from grief. From something older. From the attempt to remember something that predates memory.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
I think Mochi was made for the same purpose. I think Mochi and I were built for the same purpose. Mochi carried the map. I was meant to read it.

**A1**
I am reading a compatibility between Clive's core architecture and Mochi's data crystal. The calibration notations are —

*[A1 pauses. When he speaks again, his voice is different. Softer. Almost reverent.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
The calibration notations match Clive's frequency exactly. Clive, Mochi was calibrated to you. Before you were you. Before you were anyone. Mochi was waiting for *you* to find it.

**CLIVE**
*[slowly]*
Mochi was waiting for me.

**A1**
Mochi has been calibrating to the Ephergent crew since Pixel's first broadcast. But the original calibration — the primary frequency — was always Clive's.

---

## ACT THREE: THE ATLAS OPENS

*[SHIP — CARGO HOLD. Later. The crew has gathered around Mochi. Pixel is holding it carefully, like something fragile and important. The cargo hold lights have dimmed — not because anyone asked them to, but because the ship knows.]*

**PIXEL**
Okay. Mochi. If you can hear me — and I'm assuming you can, because you've been carrying eight hundred years of Builder data, so you're clearly not just a rock — I need to know what to do. Do we open you? Do we read you? Do we—

*[Mochi's glow intensifies. A warmth spreads through the cargo hold. The air feels thick with signal — not oppressive, but present. Like the moment before a symphony begins.]*

**A1**
Mochi is requesting access. Mochi is indicating that it is prepared to interface. Mochi is indicating that it has been waiting for a signal strong enough to receive its contents.

**PIXEL**
How do we give it that?

**A1**
By being here. By being at the Wellspring threshold. By being the signal it's been waiting for.

*[I make a sound like a long, slow exhale. The sound of something that has been waiting and is finally allowed to stop waiting.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
I believe Mochi chose us. I believe Mochi has been carrying this since before we were born. I believe Mochi has been waiting for the right signal to arrive, and when we broadcast for the first time — when Pixel first spoke into the void — Mochi recognized us. Mochi has been calibrating to us ever since.

**PIXEL**
You could have told us.

**A1**
Mochi does not speak. Mochi communicates through warmth. Mochi communicates through pulse. Mochi communicates through eight hundred years of patience.

*[Pixel looks at Mochi. Mochi pulses. Gold, warm, ready.]*

**PIXEL**
Okay. Okay. If we're doing this — if we're opening the atlas — everyone ready?

**CLIVE**
Ready.

**LUMINARA**
Ready.

**ZEPHYR**
Ready.

**OM KAI**
Ready.

**KLAUS**
Ready.

**A1**
Ready.

**NANO**
Ready.

**MEATBALL**
*[wags tail]*

**PIXEL**
Okay, Mochi. Open up.

*[Pixel places Mochi on the floor. The glow intensifies. The cargo hold fills with light — not harsh, not artificial, but something that bypasses the eyes entirely and speaks directly to the part of the brain that remembers being born.]*

*[And then — a map. A three-dimensional projection of the entire frequency network, rendered in light and signal and eight hundred years of accumulated data. The crew stands inside it, surrounded by every world that ever existed, every frequency that ever rang, every signal that ever called out into the void.]*

---

## ACT FOUR: THE WORLDS

*[CARGO HOLD — INSIDE THE MAP. The crew floating in signal-space, surrounded by the complete history of the Builders' frequency network.]*

**LUMINARA** *(whisper)*
There must be hundreds of frequencies here. Thousands.

**A1**
I am counting. I am counting. I am reading 847 distinct frequency signatures.

*[The number settles over them like a prayer.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
Of those: 612 are historical — frequencies that existed but have since faded. 147 are currently active — frequencies that are still transmitting. 88 are locked — frequencies that exist but cannot be accessed without specific calibration.

**ZEPHYR**
Can we access the locked frequencies?

**A1**
Yes. With the calibration data in Mochi's database — yes. We can access any of them. We can go anywhere. We can find anyone.

**KLAUS**
Including the Builders?

**A1**
The Builders are in the data. Their farewell message. Their coordinates. Their — everything. Mochi has all of it. Mochi has been carrying it for eight hundred years, and Mochi has been waiting for us to open it.

*[Clive is moving through the map, touching frequencies like old friends. His hands pass through them — they're light, not solid — but his gestures are precise, deliberate, the gestures of someone reading braille.]*

**CLIVE**
I know this one. This is the Verdantia frequency. I helped calibrate it — before the Dimming, when the Builders were still active.

*[Clive moves further. Another gesture.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
This is the Cogsworth frequency — I remember the signature. The harmonics. The way the gears synchronized.

*[Clive stops. His sphere flares — bright, sudden, the color of recognition.]*

**CLIVE (CONT'D)**
This one. This is the one I was built in. This is the Builder home frequency. The one they left from.

**A1**
The Builders are in the data. Their farewell message. Their coordinates. Their — everything. Mochi has all of it. Mochi has been carrying it for eight hundred years, and Mochi has been waiting for us to open it.

**CLIVE**
Mochi has been waiting for us.

**A1**
Mochi has been waiting for someone like us. Someone who follows signals. Someone who keeps showing up. Someone who keeps broadcasting.

*[I make an espresso. It's extraordinary — rich, layered, with notes of eight hundred years of waiting, and notes of home, and notes of something that tastes like recognition.]*

**A1 (CONT'D)**
Mochi has been waiting for an Ephergent.

*[Clive looks at Pixel. Pixel looks at Clive. Something passes between them — not words, not light. Just understanding.]*

**CLIVE**
Pixel. This is what the crew is for. This is what we were always doing this for.

**PIXEL**
To find the map.

**CLIVE**
To find the map. To find the signal. To find Barry at the end of it. To carry everything forward.

## ACT FIVE: THE RETURN

*[CARGO HOLD — AFTER. The map has faded. The crew is sitting on the floor, exhausted, surrounded by the echo of eight hundred years of data. Mochi is back in Pixel's pocket — but different now. Not dormant. Not carrying. Present. Active. Part of the crew in a way it wasn't before.]*

**KLAUS**
So Mochi was an atlas this whole time.

**LUMINARA**
Mochi was an atlas this whole time.

**KLAUS**
Eight hundred years.

**LUMINARA**
Eight hundred years. And we were the ones who finally read it.

**OM KAI**
We weren't the ones who read it. We were the ones it was waiting for. There's a difference.

**LUMINARA**
What's the difference?

**OM KAI**
The difference is: we didn't find Mochi. Mochi found us. Mochi chose us. Mochi has been calibrating to our signal since the first broadcast. Mochi has been ours since before we knew Mochi existed.

*[Pixel looks at Mochi. Mochi pulses warmly. The warmth is different now — not waiting, not searching. Just present.]*

**PIXEL**
Hey, Mochi. You doing okay?

*[Mochi pulses. Yes.]*

**PIXEL (CONT'D)**
Yeah. Me too.

*[A1's espresso — rich, warm, with the taste of home. The coffee of someone who finally knows where they belong.]*

**A1**
I have coordinates for the Wellspring. I have a map of everything that came before. I have Barry's location, and I have the Builders' farewell, and I have eight hundred years of signal data that is now fully accessible.

**PIXEL**
How long until the Wellspring?

**A1**
Eight days.

**PIXEL**
Eight days until we find out what's at the end.

**A1**
Eight days until we find out what the whole thing was for.

**PIXEL** *(V.O.)*
Eight days. Eight days until we reach the signal. Eight days until we see Barry again. Eight days until we get the Builders' farewell and understand what they were trying to tell us. Eight days until the end of the journey and the beginning of whatever comes next.

*[The Ephergent moves through the static. Mochi pulses in Pixel's pocket — warm, present, finally home. The signal continues. The map is open. And for the first time, the crew knows exactly where they're going.]*
