# Every Color Was a Name

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

Chromatica exhaled color, then began to forget. Light panels dimmed, shutters closed like eyelids. Luminara moved through the rooms, copying patterns into her ledger, humming to coax filaments awake. Mochi pressed into her arm, a warm comfort. A1’s coffee was toffee-bitter, a patient flavor. We catalogued hues, names attached to wavelengths, glyphs for ownership. Barry’s notes surfaced: “color is record. if color dims, listen for the memory under it.” In a workshop, Clive found a device that hummed memory frequencies—voices, rhythms, intent encoded in spectrum and sound. The Builders tried to archive people in color. We archived what we could, memory layered in light.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The city of Chromatica breathes color, but the light is fading. Luminara leads the crew through dimming corridors, her hands gentle, her ledger open. Mochi nestles in the crook of her arm, a silent comfort. A1 pours toffee-bitter coffee, the taste of patience and resolve.

### Discovery

The team catalogs the remaining hues, attaching names to wavelengths, recording glyphs that signal ownership. Barry’s marginalia appears in the records: dates, arrows, and a single line—“color is record. if color dims, listen for the memory under it.”

### Memory and Frequency

In a workshop beneath the plaza, Clive discovers a device that hums with memory frequencies. When pressed to his temple, he hears a chorus of voices, builders counting frames, someone tapping a rhythm. The device leaves a residue of intent—the Builders’ attempt to encode identity into spectrum and sound.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “We archived what we could. Chromatica’s memory lingers in color and hum. Barry’s notes guide us. The Frequency remembers.”
