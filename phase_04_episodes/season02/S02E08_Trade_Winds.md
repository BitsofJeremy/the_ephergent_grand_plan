# Trade Winds

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

Passage through the belt depended on favors. Toll-keepers lingered in the shadows, measuring routes in toll-calls and ledgers. We bargained with parts, repaired sensors, and traded cups of coffee as polite currency. Mochi sat on a crate, calm enough to make a toll-keeper count less carefully. A1’s coffee was heavy black—molten caramel—a toll-keeper accepted it with a surprised grin. The exchange bought us safe passage. In a back-room, a Squeeze-Bot operator had Barry’s note taped up: “prefers barter over story; leaves less mess.” We left a repaired sensor and a thermos cap with Barry’s sticker, gaining a mapped corridor. Clive communicated with a light-pattern blink, the toll-keeper answering in kind. We moved out with a new chart and coordinates taped to the hull.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The crew approaches the Belt Passage, where toll-keepers and trappers complicate travel. Favors and barter are the currency of passage. The crew prepares to negotiate, parts and coffee at the ready.
