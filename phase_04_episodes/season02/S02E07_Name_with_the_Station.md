# Name with the Station

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

The station’s files read like minutes from a long conversation. ‘Ephergent’ appeared everywhere: labels, ledgers, terminals. Not a slogan, but a descriptor—an action. Mochi nosed a packet of dried food, leaving a warm smear. A1’s coffee was burnt orange peel, brisk and sharp. Clive sat in the archive bay, letting memory unspool: a workshop, hands, a small living thing being wound. Barry’s breadcrumb was a calibration table—how a station’s hum related to civic function. We learned to read ‘Ephergent’ as a verb, an instruction to align systems to life.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The crew enters Station 7-Ascending, archive files stretching like a long conversation. The word ‘Ephergent’ recurs—on lockers, in ledgers, scrawled on terminals. It is not branding, but instruction.

Mochi noses a packet of dried food, leaving a warm smear. A1 pours burnt orange-peel coffee, brisk and clarifying. Clive sits in the archive bay, letting files and memory unspool: a workshop, hands, a living thing being wound.

### Discovery

Barry’s calibration table links the station’s hum to civic function. The crew studies the table, learning to read the name as a set of instructions, not an answer. Patterns emerge across stations.

### Memory and Pattern

Clive’s memories come in long, thin threads, filling more of his chest than his language. The crew reframes their understanding: ‘Ephergent’ is a verb, a way to align systems to life.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “The name is a tool, not an answer. Barry’s table reframes the pattern. We align by instruction, not by label.”
