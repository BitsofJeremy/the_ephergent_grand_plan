# Wreckage and Will

## Pixel-Voice Synopsis

The wreckage lay at the edge of a broken ring—a hull torn like a turned page. Panels flapped in vacuum, the hum of a dying generator lingering. We catalogued salvage plates, tracing dates and patterns. Mochi sat in the shadow of a crate, tail thumping like a metronome. A1 sipped peat-ash coffee, scanning a plate marked by Barry: timestamps and a charted resonance decline. Barry’s logs were clinical—parts lists, repair windows, buffers. We realized we were tracing decay, not just coordinates. Clive’s sleeve blinked a repair cadence, matching Barry’s notes. The direction was clear: maintain rhythm, not just location.

## Narrative Structure

### Opening

The crew enters the wreckage ring, picking through twisted struts and salvage plates. Each plate is catalogued, dates and patterns noted.

Mochi’s metronomic presence grounds the crew. A1 pours peat-ash coffee, the flavor grounding. Barry’s logs are precise, mapping resonance decline and station failures.

### Discovery

The crew identifies a pattern: station drift and frequency instability. Repairs are possible but require patience and method.

### Repair and Resonance

Clive’s light-pattern matches the repair cadence in Barry’s log. The lesson: rhythm is as vital as location in repair.

### Closing Beat

Pixel files a dispatch: “We trace decay, not just coordinates. Barry’s logs teach patience. The Frequency is a rhythm to maintain.”
