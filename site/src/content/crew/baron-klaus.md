# Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf

Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf is twelve inches tall, made of glazed ceramic, and carries himself with the absolute gravity of a man who has never once considered the possibility that his title might be decorative.

He is, in fact, the only gnome in the known multiverse who uses the honorific "Baron" without irony.

This is not delusion. Klaus is aware that "Baron" is a title from a Nocturne Aeturnus peerage system that predates the Sorrowful Moon's current phase by several centuries. He is aware that the Nocturne nobility dissolved its formal structure approximately two hundred years ago. He uses it anyway. The title is a uniform he wears with precision, with purpose, with the understanding that how you present yourself determines what you're capable of.

## Origin

Klaus is an ancient ceramic garden gnome who gained sentience approximately four hundred years ago in a Nocturne Aeturnus garden supply depot — he woke, climbed out of his shipping crate, straightened his hat, and began investigating frequency anomalies he could perceive but no one else acknowledged. Before his awakening, he was decorative. After his awakening, he became Nocturne Aeturnus's most thorough internal affairs investigator, specializing in financial irregularities within the grief-crystal trade, emotional fraud, and frequency smuggling cases that the twilight world's governing structures preferred to leave unexamined.

He was very good at it. He was so good that he found something in the course of an investigation that could not be accommodated — evidence of how Nocturne's emotional economy actually functioned versus how it claimed to function. He was asked to close the case. He was asked to stop seeing what he had seen. Klaus does not stop seeing.

He left. He took his ceramic body, his painted coat, and his incomplete case notes, and he walked into the Space between frequencies. Four centuries later, he encountered the Ephergent in a debris field, giving tactical orders to displaced furniture with more authority than most officers he had observed. Pixel noticed. She offered him a position. He accepted. He has not explained why. The crew has learned not to ask.

## Physical Description

Klaus is a ceramic garden gnome of the classic Nocturne Aeturnus artisan tradition — fired clay body with hand-painted detailing, approximately twelve inches tall. His construction predates the current Sorrowful Moon phase by an unknown interval; the glaze technique produces a slightly deeper violet undertone than contemporary garden gnomes. His body is hollow ceramic — the chest cavity resonance is what allows him to produce his projecting voice, a fact he shared once, under direct questioning, and never elaborated on.

He should not be alive. He is, demonstrably, alive.

The hat is deep forest green, slightly askew, firmly planted. It has been on his head through every incident, every firefight, every frequency storm the Ephergent has survived. It does not fall off. This is either a property of the hat or a property of Klaus's will.

The coat is dark wool, mid-calf length, painted onto him — he cannot remove it. It has more interior pockets than any coat of that size should accommodate. From these pockets: a lockpick, a signal dampener, a pre-completed DRM form, crystallized grief (it actually is), a compact frequency scanner of his own design, one snack of indeterminate origin, and a small notebook of case notes. The how of his ceramic arms reaching into those pockets remains unexplained. Klaus considers the question beneath discussion.

His face is painted in the classic garden gnome style — rosy cheeks, bright eyes, a broad and slightly foolish smile. This expression is a default from manufacture. His real expressions are communicated through hat tilt, stance angle, and the precise arrangement of his painted features. His eyes are steel-gray with violet flecks. They miss nothing.

## The Investigator

Klaus notices everything. He notices: the recalibration pattern in Luminara's spectral analyzer suggesting she's been scanning frequencies she claims not to study. The specific angle of Clive's hat adjustment indicating a memory surfacing. The way Pixel's frequency sensitivity manifests in her posture before she consciously registers a signal. The smell of machine oil on Nano's hands and the specific Cogsworth formulation it implies. He does not comment on all of it. He files what matters in his notebook and moves on.

His professional background was investigation — not freelance, but institutional. He worked cases the emotional-crystalline society preferred not to examine. Financial irregularities. Emotional fraud. Frequency smuggling. The cases were not glamorous. They were important in the way that structural integrity is important: nobody notices it until it fails.

He approaches the crew the way he approached cases. Someone is hiding something. The question is why. Klaus will wait for the answer. He is very patient. He is also very thorough.

He avoids discussing why he left Nocturne Aeturnus with such consistent, practiced skill that most of the crew does not realize they have never heard the explanation. When redirected, he produces genuinely interesting information about the current topic and the original question evaporates. This technique is professional-grade. He developed it over decades of work in which the people asking the questions were often the people with the most reason to want those questions answered.

## Voice

Klaus speaks in complete, polished sentences. His grammar is precise. His word choices are exact. He does not use contractions in professional settings. He tells you things sideways — the relevant information is distributed across context and implication, and your job is to assemble the picture yourself. He will provide the pieces. He will not assemble them for you. This is not condescension. He genuinely believes you can do it. He is usually right.

Klaus is funny. This surprises people. His humor does not land as jokes. It lands as observations that happen to be true in a way that illuminates something unexpected. The crew has learned to listen for the moment when Klaus says something that makes them laugh, because that moment usually contains a real insight.

**Klaus, observed:**
> *"The filed complaint makes three references to 'unprecedented circumstances.' In my experience, 'unprecedented circumstances' is what people call things they should have anticipated but didn't."*

> *"I have reviewed the evidence. I have drawn conclusions. The conclusions are unflattering. I am still deciding whether to share them."*

> *"Interesting thing about frequency transit corridors: there is a resonance pocket at the midpoint that sounds, if you're listening correctly, like a filing cabinet opening. I'm told this is naturally occurring. I'm told a lot of things. Most of them are technically true."*

## Role on the Crew

Klaus is self-appointed head of security. The Ephergent did not appoint him. He assessed the threat profile, noted the absence of a dedicated security officer, and corrected the oversight. He performs the role with complete sincerity. The crew accepted this. They did not discuss it. Klaus does not present options. He presents accomplished facts.

His procurement work is equally self-directed. He acquires equipment the crew needs through channels that are technically legal. He never explains his methods. He produces results. The crew has learned to trust the results and not ask about the methods — not because the methods are questionable, but because the explanations would take longer than the acquisitions.
