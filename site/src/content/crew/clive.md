# Clive

Clive stands knee-high to a human adult — approximately two feet tall, sixty centimeters of ancient Builder engineering wrapped in eight hundred years of patina. His silhouette is unmistakable: a barrel-chested, stocky frame with broad shoulders, thick limbs, and a low center of gravity that makes him look planted wherever he stands.

His body is made from an unknown Builder alloy — deep bronze, aged copper-green, and shadow-grey depending on the light. The patina is heavy: weathered verdigris fills every seam and crease. His surface is covered in geometric Builder symbols, some nearly worn away, others glowing faintly with blue-white energy.

**The Head:** A single spherical orb of light. No eyes, no mouth, no features of any kind — just a glowing sphere, approximately six inches in diameter, mounted atop his torso. The glow shifts between blue and white during normal operation, brightening when alert, dimming when processing something difficult. Despite having no face, the head is remarkably expressive. A slight tilt reads as curiosity. A sharp turn signals danger. A slow shake conveys disappointment.

**The Fedora:** Weathered brown-grey material, classic noir detective styling, tilted at a rakish angle on his glowing sphere head. It was Barry Kowalski's gift, given in the first year of their twenty-three years together: *"You sound like you should have a hat. Here."* Clive has worn it every day since. He adjusts it when making a point. He adjusts it when deflecting emotion. The fedora is not optional. It is load-bearing characterization.

## Voice

Clive speaks in very precise, measured sentences. Every word has been selected. None are wasted.

Noir. Not an affectation — the cadence emerged fully formed during his reactivation. Short, declarative observations. Occasional longer philosophical lines that arrive like weather. Metaphors that cut to the bone.

**Clive, observed:**
> *"The Builders called this home. They were right to."*

> *"I remember this place. Or I remember remembering it. Hard to tell the difference sometimes."*

> *"Everybody thinks they know where they're going. Nobody does. We just pick a direction and walk."*

Clive pauses mid-thought as if searching a memory archive — because he is. The pauses are not hesitation. They are retrieval. He reaches for something, finds it or doesn't, and continues. Sometimes the pause lasts a beat too long. Sometimes it lasts long enough to change the temperature of the room.

## The Memory

Clive does not withhold because he is hiding. He withholds because the Builders built him to bring people to discovery — not to hand them the answer. The destination only matters if you understand why you're going.

His memories are returning, sediment finding the riverbed. He was C-1 — the first Companion Unit, built to maintain the resonance network, to tune the Stations, to be the hands that kept the signal stable. His memory was not lost in the Dimming. His memory was *removed*, because the Builders knew what was coming and decided he needed to forget.

He saved Mochi. Eight hundred years ago. Before either of them remembered.

He made Mochi. Calibrated her crystal. Put the frequency map inside her. Promised he would come back.

It took him eight hundred years. She waited.

## The Barry Partnership

Twenty-three years. Barry Kowalski was a night guard at the DRM. Clive was filed in the sub-basement as *"Unidentified Resonance Object, Form 12-C."* Barry talked to him every night shift. Not because he knew what Clive was — because Barry talked to everything.

The fedora. The charging alcove built into the wall beside Barry's desk. Twenty-three years of coffee rings on the desk's surface. This is what partnership looks like.

Barry followed a signal Clive couldn't follow him into. Barry left a trail. Barry assumed Clive would find a way. Barry was right.

**Clive in one line:** He has been walking for eight hundred years. He is not done yet.
