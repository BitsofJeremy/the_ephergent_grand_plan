# The Drift

The slow dissolution of frequency coherence. Entropy, applied to reality itself.

The Drift is not a villain. It is not evil. It is the universe's tendency toward silence — the natural process by which things end, frequencies fade, signals weaken, and coherence gives way to the static between stations.

It cannot be stopped. It can only be slowed.

## What It Does

When Builder Stations fail, the frequencies they sustain lose coherence. Edges blur. Signal weakens. Characters fade. Eventually — slowly, inevitably — the frequency goes silent entirely. Not destroyed. Dissolved. The world that was there forgets that it was there.

This is the Drift's nature: not malice, not destruction, but forgetting. The universe gradually stops paying attention to certain frequencies, and those frequencies fade into the Silence Zones that mark the places where something used to sing.

## The Crew's Response

The crew's broadcasts fight the Drift. Every transmission is conscious attention, disrupting the automaticity that allows the Drift to spread. The crew documents, witnesses, broadcasts. They insist on remembering. They refuse to let the silence win, not because they can stop it, but because the act of witnessing — of naming, of documenting, of telling the story of what was — is itself resistance.

The Drift continues. The Silence Zones remain. But somewhere, on some frequency, someone hears the signal and continues.

That's enough. That's everything.
