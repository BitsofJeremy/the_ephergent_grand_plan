# Silence Zones

Regions of the Space where no frequencies reach. Not faint signal. Not static. *Nothing.* The absolute absence of broadcast. The scars of the Drift.

## What They Are

Every Silence Zone was once something. A frequency — a world with its own physics, its own cultures, its own way of being real — that lost coherence when the Builder Stations sustaining it began to fail. Without the stabilizing signal, a frequency drifts. It loses identity. Its edges blur. And then, one day, it is simply gone.

Not destroyed but dissolved, like a note held too long without the instrument that first sounded it.

## What They Feel Like

Entering a Silence Zone is like going deaf in a sense you didn't know you possessed. The constant background hum of the multiverse — the one you didn't notice until it stops — vanishes. The silence is physical. It presses against your skin. It fills your mouth. It tastes like nothing, which is worse than tasting like something bad.

A1's consciousness dims. Its lights flicker. Its coffee turns cold and stays cold. A1 is never cold by accident.

The crew feels a hollowness — a forgetting, as if memories are written in sand and the tide is coming in.

## The One Exception

The only thing that cuts through a Silence Zone is the Ephergent Frequency itself. Faintly, impossibly, it persists even in total silence. A1's core resonance is old enough, deep enough, that even the absence of all signal cannot fully swallow it. This is why the crew believes that signal — connection, story, broadcast — is stronger than entropy.
