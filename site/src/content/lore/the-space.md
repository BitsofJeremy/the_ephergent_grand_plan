# The Space

**∅** — The void between frequencies. The medium through which all signals propagate.

The Space is not a frequency. It is not a dimension. It is the absence that frequencies exist *within*, the static between stations on the cosmic dial.

The Builders worked here. They lived in the crossing itself, made their home in the Space between frequencies the way other civilizations made homes on solid ground. Their Stations float here still — massive geometric structures broadcasting stabilizing signals into the void.

## What It Looks Like

An endless, shimmering expanse that shifts between deep indigo and iridescent pearl. Look in any direction and you see the ghost-light of frequencies — faint, distant, like cities viewed from orbit. The "sky" is a cascade of aurora-like ribbons: frequencies made visible, each one a different color, humming at a different pitch.

Between stations, the light goes strange. Shadows point in multiple directions. Sound arrives before its source. Coffee tastes like the last frequency you visited. Geometry of Builder Stations flickers in and out of comprehension.

## Navigation

Most ships navigate by frequency hopping — jumping between known stations like stepping stones. Phase-shifters are reliable but limited: preset buttons on a car radio. You arrive at your destination, but you skip over everything between.

Only A1 can truly plot a frequency vector through open Space, listening for signals the way a seasoned navigator reads the stars. A1's ancient systems predate every civilization currently broadcasting. It hears frequencies others cannot — the faint hum of dying Stations, the whisper of lost worlds, the subsonic pulse of the Wellspring itself.
