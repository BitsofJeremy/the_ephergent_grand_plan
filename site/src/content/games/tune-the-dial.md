# Tune the Dial

Tune the Dial is a frequency puzzle game. The player operates a retro oscilloscope interface — a single dial, a waveform display, and a signal meter — to scan the cosmic radio spectrum. Hidden in the noise are stable signals: the five known frequencies, Builder Station broadcasts, the Ephergent Frequency itself, and one deeply buried pattern that Barry Kowalski left for Clive eight hundred years ago.

This is not an action game. It is a listening game. The player's primary tool is patience.

---

## Story Context

Pixel Paradox built the first prototype of this device in the DRM's basement workshop, during a lunch break she wasn't supposed to be taking, from parts she requisitioned under a project code that didn't exist. She called it a "hobby project." Her supervisor called it "concerning." The DRM called it "evidence" and locked it in a cabinet.

The DRM was right to be concerned. The device worked.

What Pixel didn't know when she built it: the DRM's basement was built on a thin spot. The device didn't receive signals from radio towers. It received signals from *frequencies* — from other realities bleeding through the walls, broadcasting whether anyone was listening or not. Pixel's little hobby project was, without anyone intending it, the first receiver tuned to the Space between worlds.

The DRM confiscated it. The DRM was wrong to do so. The device is now on The Ephergent's navigation console, and Pixel is the captain of a ship she didn't know existed, talking to a machine that is also a ship, and the hobby project that started everything is still the clearest window into what the universe is trying to say.

## The Interface

**The Dial** — A single circular frequency dial at center-bottom. Click-drag or arrow keys to rotate. The dial is continuous — it wraps. There is no beginning or end to the spectrum.

**The Waveform Display** — An oscilloscope occupying the top two-thirds of the viewport:
- **Noise**: Jagged, irregular, fast-moving static. Default state.
- **Approaching a signal**: The noise begins to organize. Faint tonal qualities emerge.
- **On a signal**: The waveform resolves into a distinct, stable pattern. Audio becomes clear.
- **Locked on**: Hold steady for 3 seconds. The signal locks. Lore unlocks.

**Signal Meter** — Top-right. Twitches when approaching a signal. Holds in the green zone when locked.

## The Signals

### Tier 1 — The Five Known Frequencies

Each frequency has a unique waveform, audio character, and color tint:

| Frequency | Waveform | Audio | Tint |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Prime Material** | Clean sine wave, steady, confident | Warm low hum, like electricity through a wall | None (default green) |
| **Nocturne Aeturnus** | Slow deep oscillation, harmonic overtones, cello-like | Resonant chord with cathedral reverb, mournful | Deep indigo/purple |
| **Cogsworth Cogitarium** | Precise square wave, metronomic timing | Clock shop at midnight, layered ticking, steam hiss | Warm amber/copper |
| **Verdantia** | Organic breathing waveform, never perfectly periodic | Heartbeat through earth, leaf rustle, alive | Emerald green with pulse |
| **The Edge** | Structured chaos, fragments of all other frequencies | Roaring shimmer containing ghost-fragments of everything | Shifting unnamed colors |

### Tier 2 — Builder Station Signals

Seven catalogued Builder Stations. Narrower bands (8 positions), weaker signals, characteristic "age" — slight warble, faint harmonic decay.

### Tier 3 — The Ephergent Frequency

A single channel (5 positions wide) that behaves differently based on the selected character perspective. Each crew member hears a different version of the same signal.

### Tier 4 — Barry's Hidden Pattern

The hardest signal in the game. Three positions wide. Does not produce a clean waveform — instead produces a rhythmic modulation *within the noise itself*. The signal meter does **not** respond. Finding it is optional. Finding it changes everything.

## Character Perspectives

Each crew member hears the same frequency differently. Select a character to hear their version:

**Pixel Paradox** — Broadcast clarity. The Ephergent Frequency sounds like a transmission from a world she's never been to but recognizes.

**Clive** — The oldest ears. The signal sounds like his own voice speaking words he doesn't remember saying.

**Om Kai** — The quietest mind. The signal sounds like the silence between mantras.

**Zephyr Glitch** — The most fragmented. The signal sounds like his brother's laugh, far away but not gone.

**Meatball** — Smells like Thursday and tastes like almost-home.

## Atlas Connections

Tuning into each frequency unlocks its corresponding Lore Atlas entry. The five primary frequencies unlock the frequency section. Builder Station signals unlock station entries. The Ephergent Frequency unlocks the signal's own entry.

The hidden Barry pattern is the only signal that unlocks nothing in the atlas. What it unlocks is a question the game leaves open.

## About the Game

- **Engine**: Godot 4 (HTML5 export)
- **Size**: 11.2 MB (under 15 MB hard cap)
- **Price**: Free forever
- **Play time**: 30–60 minutes (finding all Tier 1–3 signals); indefinitely long (finding Barry's pattern)
