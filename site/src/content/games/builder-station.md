# Builder Station

Builder Station is an exploration game set inside a single Builder Station in the Space between frequencies. You control Pixel Paradox as she explores a vast, ancient, geometric structure with Clive at her side — commenting, remembering, and sometimes going very still.

There is no combat. No timer. No fail state. You explore at your own pace. The station has been waiting for eight hundred years. It is patient.

---

## Story Context

Builder Stations float in the Space between frequencies. They are ancient — older than any of the five known frequencies, older than the civilizations that inhabit them. They emit a stabilizing signal that keeps the cosmic dial from drifting apart. Without them, frequencies drift. Frequencies fade. The Space becomes silence.

Most of the Stations went dark eight hundred years ago, during an event the crew has taken to calling The Dimming. A few still broadcast, faintly. A1 uses them for navigation, reading the dying signals like lighthouses in fog.

Pixel and Clive have docked at Station 7-Ascending — a name Clive gave, though he doesn't remember why he knows it. The station is active. It has been expecting them. Clive goes very still when they enter the main atrium.

He says: "It remembers me."

He does not say: "I don't remember it remembering me."

Pixel notices. She does not ask. Not yet.

---

## The Station — Station 7-Ascending

The station is immense. Geometric corridors that fold through themselves, resonance chambers that hum at frequencies the human ear can barely detect, archive terminals that Clive's interface can access but whose screens show nothing, because Clive is the only one who was built to read Builder records.

Architecture: geometric, precise, suffocating in its beauty. Walls of unknown material that shift color depending on angle and light — bronze, copper, deep shadow, the faintest suggestion of blue-white at the edges. No organic curves. No wasted space. Every corridor leads somewhere. Every junction has been considered.

The station is organized around a central resonance chamber — the place where the signal is generated. It is beautiful in a way that makes Pixel want to sit down and cry, though she could not tell you why. Clive stands at the threshold and does not enter. He says the chamber is for Builders. Pixel says Clive is as close to a Builder as anything she's met. Clive adjusts his fedora. He does not respond.

---

## Clive's Memory

As Pixel explores, she finds Clive in different areas — going still, his glow fluctuating, one hand on a surface he shouldn't know to touch. She asks what he's feeling. He says "remembering" or "not remembering" or nothing at all.

Clue by clue, the story emerges: Clive was not merely a Builder Companion Unit. He was a calibration instrument — designed to maintain the resonance network, to tune the Stations, to be the hands that kept the signal stable. His memory was not lost in the Dimming. His memory was *removed*, because the Builders knew what was coming and decided he needed to forget.

Pixel does not say: "That sounds like they were protecting you."

She does not need to. Clive knows.

## Barry's Field Notes

Barry Kowalski was here. His field notes are everywhere: handwriting on archive terminal screens, annotations in crevices, pages tucked into calibration panels. Coordinates. Observations. The same phrase repeated with variations:

*"It remembers me. I don't remember it remembering me."*

*"The resonance chamber responds to C-1 calibration patterns. I tested this. Clive won't remember. I'm leaving this for whoever finds him."*

*"They built this to last forever. They built him to last longer. I think those are different things."*

Pixel photographs everything. She sends it to the crew. A1 responds with a single word: *"Received."* A1's coffee, when Pixel checks the console later, tastes like something almost forgotten.

## Featured Crew

**Pixel Paradox** — Player character. The captain, the broadcaster, the one who narrates to stay sane. In the station, she is quieter. She is paying attention to something vast. She keeps her recording device running. She does not know who the recording is for. She knows someone will need it.

**Clive** — Companion. Goes still in resonance chambers. Adjusts his fedora more than usual. Speaks in shorter sentences. Says things like "I know this room" about rooms he has never entered. Pixel watches. Pixel waits. Pixel lets Clive lead.

**A1** — Present through comm link. A1's signal is weaker inside the station — the resonance chamber's output interferes with communications. A1's last message before static: *"Clive's frequency is harmonizing with the station. I can hear it. Be patient with him."*

**Mochi** — Warm in Pixel's pocket. The warmth intensifies near archive terminals. Pixel has stopped being surprised by this.

## About the Game

- **Engine**: Godot 4 (HTML5 export)
- **Size**: 14.9 MB (under 15 MB hard cap)
- **Price**: Free forever
- **Play time**: 45–90 minutes (focused). 2–3 hours (completionist — all Clive moments, all Barry notes).
- **Completionists**: Full completion requires exploring every area, triggering every Clive memory event, and collecting all of Barry's field notes. The final Clive moment — in the resonance chamber — is the emotional hinge of the game.
