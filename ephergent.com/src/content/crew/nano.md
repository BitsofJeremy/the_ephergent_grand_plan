# NANO

Nano is fast.

Not fast for an engineer, not fast for someone from Cogsworth where precision is religion and urgency is heresy. Nano is just *fast* — the kind of fast that makes people miss things, except Nano doesn't miss things because she moves so quickly through a space that she experiences it completely before anyone else has finished processing the first detail.

She is small, sharp, and constantly in motion even when she is standing still. She talks faster than anyone can track. She builds faster than anyone can follow. She processes the universe at a speed that should be incoherent but somehow isn't — because every word she says is precise, every technical reference is accurate, every leap in her logic is earned.

She left Cogsworth because she understood something the frequency couldn't: precision without speed is a clock that shows the right time for a moment that has already passed. The universe is desynchronizing. The Drift is accelerating. You can calibrate a gear to perfect tolerance and by the time you've finished, the tolerance has shifted because the frequency itself has shifted.

She is the Ephergent's intelligence officer not because she was appointed but because she arrived knowing things she shouldn't know, and she keeps knowing things she shouldn't know because she never stops moving long enough for anyone to track where she got them.

---

## Role

- **Intelligence Officer** — information broker, contact network across five frequencies
- **Scout/Infiltrator** — velocity-based reconnaissance, fast-moving insertion and extraction
- **Chief Engineer** — keeps A1 running at speeds that should be impossible

---

## Home Frequency

**Cogsworth Cogitarium** (Frequency 3). A mechanical civilization obsessed with precision and function. Brass and copper. Gears the size of buildings. Everything exactly on time.

Nano understood precision. She was raised in it. But she also understood something Cogsworth's engineering culture could not accommodate: entropy doesn't wait. She left because she could not slow down, and Cogsworth could not speed up.

She carries Cogsworth with her anyway — the gear-tooth patterns on her forearms, the clockwork precision in her hands, the smell of machine oil and burnt sugar that has baked into her at a cellular level.

## Voice

Nano talks mid-sentence. She picks up in the middle of a thought, assumes you've been following the internal monologue that preceded the verbal output, and continues at a pace that requires active effort to track.

She says "— right, so —" a lot, bridging between thoughts she's already processed and thoughts she's explaining. She interrupts herself. She answers questions before they're finished.

Sample:
> *"— the coordinates check out, I've run them four times, the signature matches a Builder Station and the Station matches the signature in Barry's notebook which I shouldn't have been able to read but the entropy fragments were carrying text and I was running alongside them which sounds impossible but try it, it's not impossible, it's just fast —"*

## The Ephergent Revelation

Her moment comes during the final broadcast calibration, hands deep in A1's array.

She is working faster than she's ever worked, and she realizes that her hands are steady. Perfectly steady. Moving at exactly the speed required. No faster. No slower.

She stops. Looks at her hands. For one moment, Nano is completely still — not because something is wrong, but because something is, for the first time, precisely right.

The Ephergent Frequency hums through the components under her fingers, and it is a mechanism that includes her. Speed was never the point. Arrival was.

## Relationships

**Clive**: Shared workspace, contrasting tempo. Nano fascinated by his ancient Builder-era hands. He lets her examine them. Neither explains why this trust exists.

**Zephyr**: The most efficient communication on the ship. They talk in fragments the rest of the crew can't follow. Same velocity, same direction.

**A1**: The only crew member who mapped A1's full engineering layout. "The espresso machine *is* the ship. The rest is just how it stretches." A1 made her the brightest espresso of his existence when she said this.

**Klaus**: Functional respect. He acquires fast. She builds fast. They don't explain their processes to each other. They don't need to.

## Game

**Static Run** — Endless runner where Nano runs through Space between frequencies while entropy fragments chase her.

The Barry seed appears here: an entropy fragment that looks like a torn page from Barry's notebook. Running *alongside* it instead of away reveals text. Collecting all fragments over many runs spells out coordinates.

The game's thesis: the fastest way is sometimes through the slow reading of what seems to be chaos.
