## Violations Detailed

### Rule 1: Frequencies, not dimensions

**LOCKED RULE**: Call them "frequencies." Never "dimensions," "planes," or "realms."

**VIOLATIONS:**

- **S01E06 "The Song at the Edge of Everything"** — Uses "multiverse" in dialogue:
  > "I can't believe I just said 'multiverse' like it's a normal word."
  - *Issue*: "Multiverse" is dimension-adjacent terminology. Canon calls for "frequencies" or "the Space."
  - *Recommended Fix*: Replace with "frequencies" or "the spectrum between worlds."

- **S01E05 "Root and Branch"** — Uses "dimension" in Luminara's internal monologue:
  > "A PLANT DIMENSION. A PLANT DIMENSION."
  - *Issue*: Directly violates Rule 1.
  - *Recommended Fix*: "A plant frequency." / "A plant world."

---

### Rule 2: The Space, not the Sea

**LOCKED RULE**: Space vocabulary only: fly, dock, navigate, cockpit. No nautical metaphors: sail, anchor, port, starboard, helm.

**VIOLATIONS:**

- **S01E06 "The Song at the Edge of Everything"** — Uses nautical terms:
  > "Take us to the song."
  - *Context*: This is Zephyr's dialogue, not narration. However, "helm" appears in S01E12 "Wreckage and Will":
  > "FLANK LEFT! FLANK LEFT, YOU INCOMPETENT OTTOMAN!"
  - *Issue*: Baron Klaus dialogue is comedic and in-character, but still uses "port" implicitly in tactical commands. The tactical gnome character may use nautical language as intentional anachronism — recommend keeping for character voice but adding correction in editorial notes.
  - *Recommended Fix*: Add editorial note: "Baron Klaus uses naval tactical terminology as character voice; this is intentional anachronism but should be minimized in future drafts."

---

### Rule 4: A1's Coffee Flavor

**LOCKED RULE**: When A1 is present in a scene, note the coffee flavor. Bitter when worried. Perfectly balanced when content. Thin and watery when exhausted.

**INCONSISTENCIES:**

- **S01E02 "Ruins, Ripples"** — Coffee described as "sharp and bright, almost citric" when detecting signal. ✅ Acceptable — citrus can indicate active problem-solving.
  
- **S01E03 "The DRM Archive"** — Coffee goes "cold" — a first. This is noted as significant. ✅ Good use of absence as characterization.

- **S01E04 "When the Moon Stopped Weeping"** — Coffee noted as "sharp and bright, almost citric" for problem-solving mode. Consistent. ✅

- **S01E05 "Root and Branch"** — Coffee "tasted sharp and bright" again — same flavor profile reused. ⚠️ Consider varying the descriptors to avoid repetition.

- **S01E06 "The Song at the Edge of Everything"** — Coffee "tastes like star charts" — Om Kai explains this is encoding navigational data. ✅ Excellent integration of the rule into plot.

- **S01E08 "Learning to Be Large"** — Coffee changes flavor depending on which system A1 focuses on (salt air for navigation, old paper and cinnamon for archive data). ✅ Creative extension of the rule.

- **S01E11 "Toll Roads and Trade Winds"** — Coffee described as "rich and honeyed with a bright citrus lift" when A1 is content working. ⚠️ Different flavor profile for contentness — should taste "perfectly balanced" per Rule 4 when content. Recommend clarification that "balanced" can manifest as honeyed/citrus rather than neutral.

- **S01E12 "Wreckage and Will"** — Coffee noted as "humming in the commons" with "notes of toasted grain and a distant citrus." ⚠️ No explicit flavor callout for A1's emotional state.

- **S01E13 "Meatball's Frequency"** — Coffee goes from "thick/syrupy" (grief) to "thin and watery" (depletion) to "floral" (Builder memory) to "balanced" (resolution). ✅ Excellent progression following the rule.
