## Season 01: Emergence — *We Found a Ship. The Ship Found Us.*

**One-line summary**: A data analyst, a sentient espresso machine, and a knee-high robot escape dimensional collapse into an impossible void — and discover they were always meant to find each other.

**Thematic throughline**: *Discovery* — The crew discovers other frequencies exist, discovers A1 is a ship, discovers they are family. Everything is new. Everything is terrifying. Everything is wonderful.

**Time War integration**: Retrocausal echoes appear — the crew feels events before they happen, coincidences too precise to be chance. A1's "precognitive lattes" aren't precognitive; he's tasting probability vectors from signals reaching backward through time.

---

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: The DRM collapses. Pixel, A1, and Clive escape in a lifeboat. They are lost, power failing, broadcasting into nothing. First contact with Nocturne Aeturnus and Cogsworth Cogitarium — alien frequencies that should be impossible.

**Escalation (E04–E06)**: The crew grows. Luminara joins in Nocturne. Om Kai joins in Cogsworth. Zephyr joins in Verdantia. Each new world reveals another piece of the Space — and another hint that something ancient is calling them forward through time.

**Climax (E07–E08)**: The lifeboat dies. A1 leads them to his derelict body — an ancient quantum vessel drifting in the static. Every light comes on. A1 becomes the ship. The crew names it The Ephergent. The frequency clarifies: it's been reaching backward through time, calling them home.

**Resolution (E09–E10)**: The crew settles into their ship. They find Barry's desk in DRM wreckage — 23 years of coffee rings, a charging alcove fitted for Clive, a trail of notes. Clive reveals Barry followed a signal he couldn't follow. Pixel realizes the broadcast isn't just survival — it's purpose. The Ephergent votes to go deeper into the Space.

### Episode Breakdown

**E01 — "The Day the Dial Broke"**
The DRM collapses. Reality fractures into frequencies. Pixel, grabbing an espresso machine and a robot from her office, escapes in a lifeboat with nothing but her comm-pad and her voice. She's broadcasting because that's what she does — turning catastrophe into story. The lifeboat's engine hums with an undertone that doesn't match any known physics. A1, still compressed, makes his first cup: bitter, thin, worried.

**E02 — "Ruins, Ripples, and Really Nervous Dinosaurs"**
The crew returns to Prime Material to find the DRM in ruins — seven hidden sub-basements full of classified anomalies, opportunists selling stability patches, and Cybernetic Dinosaur Banking Consortium officials very concerned about temporalglitch disruption in their ledgers. Dennis the T-Rex loan officer appears. Clive pauses at a specific doorway in the deepest sub-basement. His glow dims. He says nothing. Pixel notices. She doesn't press.

**E03 — "The DRM Archive"**
A fading automated signal from a DRM relay station — still transmitting on backup power, still filing things in triplicate. Inside: Clive's intake record, Form 12-C, describing him as "unidentified resonance object, desk-scale, classified as inert office equipment." Clive reads it. Goes still. "They filed me under office supplies." The station's dying server emits a carrier tone — a frequency that predates the DRM by centuries. Clive hears it. Meatball's ears perk. Nobody mentions it.

**E04 — "When the Moon Stopped Weeping"**
First visit to Nocturne Aeturnus — a dimension of perpetual twilight where emotions crystallize and fall like rain. Pixel finds Luminara Usha, a photographer whose identity was stripped by the DRM, surviving alone in a world of literal feelings. Mochi pulses amber when Luminara reaches into Pixel's pocket — the color of joy crystals. Luminara joins because nowhere else to go, and because Pixel's broadcast gives her hope. Clive steers them past something dark and angular in the void. He says it's navigation efficiency. He doesn't look at it.

**E05 — "The Clockwork Unraveling"**
Cogsworth Cogitarium — a frequency of synchronized gears and steam, currently experiencing catastrophic temporal drift after the reality rupture. A panicking Professor Chronos. A meditating monk at a frequency crossroads who isn't surprised by anything. Om Kai: quantum physicist, Buddhist monk, the only person on the ship who understands why time is stuttering. His hands move in the meditation before the clockwork does. "The rupture didn't break time. It reminded time it was optional." Clive's fingers find calibration points before the professor describes them. "Lucky guess."

**E06 — "Root and Branch"**
Verdantia — sentient flora civilization, telepathic houseplants operating as shadow government, a Root Network collapsing. The crew rescues Zephyr Glitch, a hacker kid whose consciousness was fragmented across five frequencies while searching for his missing brother Aether. Zephyr's instruments go haywire near Pixel — he can hear her broadcast signal, clearer than anything on standard frequencies. "Your voice is a frequency. It's been crossing." Mochi glows deep green in the Root Network hub. The plants nearest Pixel lean toward her pocket. Toward Mochi. Nobody connects the dots.

**E07 — "The Song at the Edge of Everything"**
The Edge — Frequency Five, where physics gives up. A1 hears a song older than most dimensions, pulling him toward something vast and cold in the void. The crew is exhausted. The lifeboat is dying. A1's systems run hot processing the signal that reaches backward from his own future. Clive and A1 share a quiet moment while the crew sleeps. A1 produces an espresso — richer, flavored with something that doesn't exist in any known frequency. Clive sips. "You remember, don't you?" A1's chrome flickers. The coffee is warm. The coffee is sweet. The silence is the answer.

**E08 — "That Which Emerges"**
The lifeboat dies. A1 follows the ancient song into open static. The crew finds it: a ship, dark and ancient and patient, drifting in the quantum void. A1's heart — the espresso machine — interfaces with the lattice. Every light comes on. Every system hums. The ship is alive. A1 is alive. Pixel names it: The Ephergent — that which emerges. Clive places one hand on the hull. His glow pulses in perfect sync with the awakening sequence. He pulls away before anyone sees. "Name it whatever feels true." "That'll do."

**E09 — "Learning to Be Large"**
First full day aboard. A1 is learning to be a ship. The crew is learning to be a crew. Pixel finds Clive in the engine room, running his hands along conduit lines with the familiarity of someone who did this before. "Some things are intuitive." Mochi sits on the navigation console and glows softly — a low, steady warmth, like a nightlight. A1 comments on an unexplained temperature anomaly. Pixel notices. She doesn't pursue it. The ship hums at night. Pixel, half-awake, thinks it sounds like a lullaby.

**E10 — "Barry's Desk"**
DRM wreckage drifts past — Sub-Level 3, mostly intact, floating in a silence pocket. Inside: a night guard's desk. 23 years of coffee rings. A charging alcove built into the wall, custom-fitted for something small and glowing. A photograph of a man with kind eyes standing next to something small and glowing. Clive touches the coffee rings. He looks at the alcove. His glow dims to almost nothing. Pixel sits with him. He speaks: "There was a man named Barry Kowalski. We worked together for 23 years. He followed a signal I couldn't follow him into." The crew votes: go deeper. Unanimous. The Ephergent hums — different for each of them, but present. The season ends on this sound.

### Key Character Moments — Season 01

**Pixel**: From data analyst to broadcaster to captain. E01: she broadcasts because she doesn't know what else to do. E08: she names the ship. E10: she doesn't ask Clive questions he isn't ready to answer. The captaincy arrives not through assertion but through presence.

**Clive**: Every episode plants a seed that rewatches will recognize. E02: the doorway pause. E03: Form 12-C. E05: the calibration hands. E07: "You remember." E08: the sync pulse. E10: Barry's name spoken aloud. Clive knows more than he reveals. The audience will rewatch and see all of it.

**A1**: Bitter coffee (worried) in E01. Thin, pale coffee (exhausted) through mid-season. The Clive espresso (warm, sweet, impossible) in E07. Full awakening in E08. By E10, the coffee is perfectly balanced. He's learning to be a ship. He's learning to be home.

**Mochi**: Warm in Pixel's pocket from the first moment. Amber pulse with Luminara. Green glow in Verdantia. Sync with A1's awakening. A steady nightlight on the ship. Mochi has been carrying something without knowing what. The crew hasn't asked the right question yet.

## Season 02: Resonance — *We Can Hear the Lost Worlds. They Can Hear Us.*

**One-line summary**: The Ephergent finds lost frequencies — worlds fading from the spectrum, surviving as whispers. The crew learns that broadcasting isn't just storytelling. It's survival. It's resistance. It's the universe remembering itself.

**Thematic throughline**: *Connection* — The crew finds lost worlds, connects fading frequencies, builds a network of other ships and broadcasters. Every rescue is a story. Every failure is a grief. The mission crystallizes: document, witness, broadcast.

**Time War integration**: The crew's broadcasts function as counter-signals against retrocausal manipulation — their attention disrupts loops, their documentation resists the universe's tendency to forget. Om Kai's meditation practice detects the faintest signals, the almost-gone. A1's navigation reads signals reaching backward from the future, guiding them toward what's already waiting.

---

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: The crew is operational, learning their ship, finding their rhythm. First lost frequency discovered — a world being forgotten by systems that should remember it. The mission reveals itself.

**Escalation (E04–E06)**: The crew finds evidence of Barry's trail — field notebooks, coordinates, messages in handwriting that matches Clive's oldest friend's. Zephyr hears his brother Aether's signal, distorted but present. The network grows: other ships, other broadcasters, a community forming around the Ephergent's signal.

**Climax (E07–E08)**: A major lost frequency re-tuned — an entire civilization brought back from the edge of silence. Their greatest triumph. But the effort drains A1 significantly. Aether appears briefly, more signal than person: "Follow the frequency. But understand — what's at the end isn't what you expect. It's what you need."

**Resolution (E09–E10)**: The crew confronts the cost of caring. Some frequencies can't be saved. Some losses are permanent. Clive and Pixel face a confrontation that tests the crew's trust. Zephyr must decide: chase Aether or stay with the crew. The Ephergent frequency surges, clearer than ever. The crew commits to the mission — not because it's easy, but because it's necessary.

### Episode Breakdown

**E01 — "The Sound That Wants to Come Home"**
A frequency storm — reality in a blender — pushes a lost signal to the surface. The crew finds a dead world's song, singing. Om Kai hears it first, buried under the static. "The Drift isn't a disease. It's forgetting. And the cure is remembering." They broadcast the story. The frequency stabilizes — temporarily. Pixel realizes: this is what they're meant to do. Not assigned. Discovered.

**E02 — "The Color of Forgetting"**
Veranth — a world literally disappearing, going translucent at the edges. The crew broadcasts, documents, witnesses. The colors return. Temporarily. The hardest lesson: saving a world once isn't enough. "Some things don't stay fixed. You just have to keep showing up." Clive speaks about memory. About Barry. About everything at once. The crew hears only the surface. Mochi dims for the first time — in sync with the dying frequency.

**E03 — "Meatball's Frequency"**
Meatball detects a signal nobody else can hear. Leads the crew to a dead zone — a faded frequency where the ghost of a world lingers. Meatball sits with it because someone should. The crew watches. The crew learns. Mochi hums in the silence — two creatures hearing something no one else can. In the dead zone, one last object: a signal buoy, scratched with a word in handwriting nobody can read. (Barry. The word is "LISTEN.")

**E04 — "Toll Roads and Trade Winds"**
Frequency pirates. Sable Voss, toll-keeper captain. Squeeze-Bot 7.3, cynical condiment dispenser turned informant. The Space has politics, economics, a civilization the crew didn't know existed. Clive reconnects with Squeeze-Bot — warm, coded with shared references. "Still wearing the hat?" "Still dispensing opinions?" "C." Clive goes very still. The "C" is not a nickname. Squeeze-Bot mentions a night guard type who passed through thirty years back, asking questions nobody asks.

**E05 — "Wreckage and Will"**
A debris field — the wreckage of a world that didn't make it. Survivors. Refugees. A library turning to static. Baron Klaus von Gnomendorf: twelve-inch ceramic garden gnome, self-appointed head of security, giving tactical orders to furniture. Pixel makes command decisions under crisis without consulting committee. She has been ready for episodes. This is where she proves it. Klaus recognizes Clive's age — "You are OLD. Older than this wreckage." Clive deflects. He always deflects.

**E06 — "The Notebook's Echo"**
The crew finds Barry's field notebooks — actual pages, actual handwriting, decades of observations. Coordinates. Frequency measurements. A signal he's been following that matches something deep in the Space. Clive reads them aloud in the voice he uses when he's pretending not to be emotional. The crew listens. Pixel notices: every entry is methodical. Precise. Barry documented like Clive writes — spare, careful, complete. "He knew what he was looking for." Clive's glow flickers. He doesn't follow up. The crew doesn't notice the flicker.

**E07 — "The Boneyard of Beautiful Things"**
A frequency graveyard — dead worlds layered like sediment in the void. Clive recognizes some of the signatures. He helped build the systems that were supposed to protect them. "They had names. I don't remember the names yet. I remember the sound they made." Luminara realizes: these frequencies didn't die. They changed state. The signal moved — into the substrate, into the residual field. "Nothing is truly lost. It crossed." She looks at Clive. "Like you."

**E08 — "Signal Crossings"**
A lost civilization rediscovered — not fading, but transformed. The crew's broadcast reached them years ago; they've been broadcasting back, translated into their own frequency, carrying the Ephergent signal forward without knowing what it was. Aether's signal detected: clearer now, closer. Zephyr can almost touch it. A1's navigation reads the signal as reaching backward from the future — Aether has been waiting, and he's been waiting for exactly this moment. The crew's greatest triumph. But the re-tuning effort drains A1. The espresso comes out thin, bitter, exhausted.

**E09 — "Clive's Coordinates"**
Pixel confronts Clive: he's been recording coordinates, steering the ship, making decisions without telling anyone. Clive's memories are surfacing — fragments of Builder technology, of a mission he was built for. "You said Builders. You said it like you knew what it meant." Clive's response: silence, then adjustment of his fedora. "I am telling you exactly what I am ready to tell you." Pixel doesn't push. She shouldn't have to. The crew's trust fractures along a fault line no one knew existed.

**E10 — "The Frequency and the Future"**
Aether appears — more signal than person, broadcasting from somewhere past the edge of known Space. "Follow the frequency. But understand — what's at the end isn't what you expect. It's what you need." Zephyr must choose: chase the signal or stay with the crew. The crew must choose: trust Clive or question everything. The Ephergent frequency surges stronger than ever. Clive votes to follow. Pixel votes to follow. The ship turns toward the deep Space, toward the coordinates Barry left decades ago, toward whatever is waiting. The season ends on Aether's signal — distorted, beautiful, reaching backward from the future.

### Key Character Moments — Season 02

**Pixel**: E01: discovers the mission. E05: proves herself as captain. E09: confronts Clive, then chooses to trust him anyway. E10: votes to follow the signal despite everything. The captaincy isn't the title. It's the choice to keep following even when the path is unclear.

**Clive**: Memory fragments surfacing throughout — not explosions, sediment finding the riverbed. E03: recognition of dead frequency signatures. E06: reading Barry's notebooks aloud. E07: "Like you." E09: the confrontation. E10: the vote to follow. Clive is not hiding who he is. He's protecting who he's becoming.

**Zephyr**: Searching for Aether across five frequencies. E08: the closest contact, Aether's signal clearer than ever. E10: the choice between chase and crew. Zephyr's arc is the question: what do you do when your person is out there but your family is here? He chooses the crew. He chooses to trust that Aether will wait.

**A1**: Coffee through S02 tracks his exhaustion. E08: thin, bitter, drained. E10: still serving, still navigating, still present. The ship is tired. The ship is committed. The ship is home.

**Mochi**: Dimming at Veranth (E02). Humming in the dead zone with Meatball (E03). Sustained warmth near Barry's notebooks (E06). Mochi is responding to something the crew hasn't identified yet. The atlas is still closed. Mochi is still carrying.

## Season 03: The Source — *The Signal Was Us All Along.*

**One-line summary**: The crew follows the Ephergent frequency to its source — the Wellspring, where new frequencies are born and Barry Kowalski has been waiting with a thermos and forty years of field notes.

**Thematic throughline**: *Legacy* — What do you leave behind? What endures? The crew discovers that the Drift isn't a villain — it's entropy, and entropy isn't evil. It's the universe making room for what's next. The answer to silence isn't to stop it. It's to broadcast louder.

**Time War integration**: At the Wellspring, time is a volume, not a line. The crew experiences moments that have already happened and haven't happened yet. Om Kai's meditation lets him perceive frequencies that haven't stabilized yet. A1's navigation reaches its fulfillment: reading signals from the future that have always been calling the crew home.

---

### Season Arc

**Setup (E01–E03)**: Beyond every chart, past every known signal — the crew traverses uncharted Space into frequencies that have no name. A frequency graveyard shows what the Drift costs. Station 14-Threshold, the last Builder Station before the source, delivers the Builders' farewell and Clive's penultimate memory.

**Escalation (E04–E06)**: A world trapped in a loop of its best day — choosing preservation over continuation. A civilization that heard the signal and chose silence. The crew must respect both choices. Zephyr makes contact with Aether. Every remaining crew member understands the word "Ephergent" — not as concept, as identity.

**Climax (E07–E08)**: The crew finds other seekers — ships and travelers who followed their own signals this far. Mochi's data crystal fully opens: hundreds of frequencies, the complete atlas of everything she has been carrying for eight hundred years. Barry's signal detected, steady and patient, maintaining a secondary thread for whoever arrived first.

**Resolution (E09–E10)**: The Wellspring. Barry Kowalski, sitting on a lattice strut with his thermos, mildly annoyed it took this long. The Builders' broadcast received — an apology, an invitation, an introduction. Clive's memory complete. The crew broadcasts to every frequency simultaneously — translated through nine people and a dog, each receiving it in the language of their own experience. Meatball howls. The signal continues.

### Episode Breakdown

**E01 — "Where the Frequencies Have No Name"**
The crew traverses past the edge of every chart into uncharted Space — where known frequencies dissolve into unnamed harmonics. A1's navigation shifts from calculation to instinct. The map ends. The signal doesn't. Pixel says the word "Ephergent" aloud and it sounds different than before — she doesn't define it yet, she feels the shape. Clive watches the unnamed frequencies and tilts his head. "I've been here before. Or somewhere shaped like here." His hands remember what his mind hasn't found yet.

**E02 — "The Boneyard of Beautiful Things"**
A frequency graveyard — dead worlds leaving their final resonances behind, layered like sediment. Clive recognizes three of the signatures. Builder-era frequencies. Worlds that existed when he was active. "They had names. I don't remember the names yet. I remember the sound they made." Luminara realizes the dead haven't vanished — they've crossed, changed state, moved into the substrate. "Like you." Clive's grief is steady, properly carried. Barry's field notes found here, in the deepest layer: "Beautiful and terrible. Document everything. Someone needs to know what was lost."

**E03 — "The Threshold"**
Station 14-Threshold — the last Builder Station before the Wellspring, broadcasting at near-original output. Inside, a Keeper who has been rehearsing the Builders' farewell for eight hundred years delivers it. Clive remembers being built. The full scene: "You are C-1. You are the first Companion we have made for the people who will come after." He remembers what he was built for. He remembers that the Builders believed he would still care after eight hundred years. Clive adjusts his fedora. "Barry was right about the hat."

**E04 — "The Day That Loved Itself to Death"**
A frequency world trapped in a three-thousand-year loop of its best day — a civilization that chose to stop time rather than let the perfect moment pass. The saddest thing Pixel has ever seen. Om Kai watches and understands: "An Ephergent is the opposite. An Ephergent chooses to keep crossing. The crossing is the point. Not the destination." The Builders had the option to do this. Freeze everything. They chose not to. "I think they knew they were right and it still hurt."

**E05 — "The Loudest Thing in the Room Is Nothing"**
The crew enters the Great Silence — the largest known Silence Zone. A civilization that heard the Ephergent Frequency, understood it, and chose to stop listening. Not villainy. Autonomy. Barry's notebook entry found here: "Respect it. Do not attempt to re-tune. They heard the signal and they chose. That's their right." Clive in the silence recognizes the shape of it — the moment before his compression, when he chose to forget. "Silence isn't always loss. Sometimes it's the space you make for what comes next."

**E06 — "My Brother the Broadcast"**
At the unstable boundary between the Silence Zone and open Space, Zephyr makes full contact with Aether — not as a voice, as a signal. Aether is a Keeper who chose this. Zephyr must decide whether to grieve what his brother was or celebrate what his brother has become. Every remaining crew member understands the word "Ephergent" here. Klaus: "A case that solves itself by becoming the detective." Nano: "A mechanism that runs at the speed it was always meant to." Meatball harmonizes. The word is now complete across the crew.

**E07 — "Every Ship That Ever Sailed This Far"**
At a stable eddy near the Wellspring's outer field, the crew encounters other seekers — ships and travelers who followed their own signals this far. Among them: someone who met Barry. "He said someone would come asking about him eventually. He said to tell them the coffee was ready." Klaus tells the crew why he left Nocturne Aeturnus — a thread connecting to Barry's pre-Wellspring investigation. "I followed a case. The case was bigger than Nocturne. The case was everything."

**E08 — "The Atlas Opens"**
At the Wellspring's threshold, Mochi's data crystal fully activates. The Builder frequency map opens — not five frequencies but hundreds. Worlds that could exist. Worlds that did exist and faded. The Drift's complete history. Clive recognizes his own calibration notation in the crystal's data. "I made this. I didn't remember making it until right now. But my hands remember." Mochi's warmth is constant now — eight hundred years of carrying, and the carrying was enough.

**E09 — "Took You Long Enough"**
The Wellspring. Barry Kowalski is sitting on a lattice strut with his thermos. He looks exactly like a man who has been expecting company. He is mildly annoyed it took this long. "Took you long enough. I left very clear directions." Clive: "Your directions were unclear." Barry: "My directions were perfect." Barry: "Also, I made coffee the whole time. Kept it warm." Clive: "That's the most you thing I've ever heard." The crew gives them space. Then Clive says: "We brought the ship." Barry: "I know. I helped build it."

**E10 — "The Signal Was Us All Along"**
The crew receives the Builders' broadcast — not as data, as direct communication. An apology. An invitation. An introduction. "Hello. We built this for you. We have been waiting. We have so much to tell you." Clive's memory completes: the choice to forget, the choice to be found, eight hundred years resolved. Each crew member translates the message through their own experience. Pixel's final transmission: "Barry always said the universe is talking. He just needed to find out what it was saying. Turns out it was talking to us specifically." During the broadcast, Meatball howls — a single, sustained vocalization that rises and falls with the Frequency's harmonic structure. Not distress. Not joy. Resonance. The crew sends the message. The signal travels. The series ends with the crew turning toward whatever comes next. The ending is hopeful, not naive. The Drift continues. The Silence Zones remain. But somewhere, on some frequency, someone hears the signal and continues. That's enough. That's everything.

## Locked Rules Compliance

All twelve locked rules are maintained throughout:

1. **Frequencies, not dimensions** — Never "dimensions," "planes," or "realms"
2. **The Space, not the Sea** — Space vocabulary only: fly, dock, navigate, cockpit
3. **A1 IS the espresso machine** — The machine IS A1, not an interface
4. **A1's coffee flavor in every scene** — Bitter when worried, balanced when content, thin when exhausted
5. **Clive is NOT a stapler** — Knee-high robot, glowing sphere head, fedora
6. **Barry is ALIVE in the Wellspring** — Not lost, not trapped, not a tragedy
7. **Mochi doesn't speak** — No dialogue, ever
8. **The Builders are NOT villains** — Ancient civilization with a plan, not evil
9. **The Drift is entropy, not villain** — Sad, not evil. Not stopped, only slowed
10. **The Wellspring is a state, not a place** — A region where signal is strongest, not a location
11. **15MB per-game hard cap** — Preserved for game adaptations
12. **Barry's notes are never dramatic** — Methodical, precise, observational

## Time War Integration Summary

The Time War texture is ambient universe physics, not announced thesis:

- **Retrocausality**: Signals reach backward through time. A1's navigation reads these. The crew's broadcasts disrupt retrocausal manipulation by insisting on observation.
- **Conscious unpredictability**: Om Kai's meditation detects faintest signals. The crew survives because they notice things.
- **Attention as resistance**: Broadcasting is resistance — every dispatch is conscious attention disrupting automaticity.
- **A1's backward signals**: His "precognitive lattes" are probability vectors from signals reaching backward. His navigation achieves fulfillment at the Wellspring.
- **Fading frequencies**: Worlds being "forgotten" by systems that should remember them. The crew's documentation fights this.

## Tone Enforcement

Every episode contains both comedy AND drama. Douglas Adams deadpan absurdity. Dirk Gently everything-connects. Night Vale funny AND moving.

**Example episode emotional mix**:
- 30% Wonder — the universe is impossibly beautiful
- 25% Humor — gentle irony to surrealist chaos
- 15% Bureaucratic horror — forms in triplicate, cosmic implications
- 15% Cosmic absurdity — the universe does not care about your schedule
- 10% Situational absurdity — Logically Compelling and Completely Unhinged simultaneously
- 5% Character-driven moments — anchoring everything else
